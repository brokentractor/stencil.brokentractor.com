Where the TR270B fits in the Case skid steer / CTL line

The TR270B sits in Case’s alpha-series TR (radial-lift) compact track loader range between the Case TR270 and the Case TR310. Tracked undercarriage, radial-lift loader geometry, Tier 4 Final aftertreatment. The radial-lift geometry favors ground-level digging and truck-loading work, while the tracked undercarriage delivers the flotation and low ground-pressure footprint that a wheeled skid steer cannot match on soft material or sensitive turf.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The TR270B runs a Tier 4 Final compact diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a tracked undercarriage. Closed-loop hydrostatic pumps drive final-drive motors at each track, with independent track speed control for steering and counter-rotation. Loader hydraulics use a load-sensing piston pump with electrohydraulic control valves, supporting a wide range of high-flow and standard-flow attachments.

The CTL undercarriage gives the TR270B flotation on soft material that a wheeled skid steer can’t match — but track wear monitoring becomes the largest single recurring cost on the platform. Sprocket, idler, roller, and track chain condition all need to be tracked as service-life items.

Common service items on the TR270B

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final compact diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. Common-rail high-pressure injection on Tier 4 builds.
  • Tier 4 aftertreatment: DPF service intervals, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, very tight water-separator filtration.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulics: Loader lift and bucket tilt cylinder seal kits (86570919 / 86570931 / 86570922 cross-fit on NH/Case alpha-series), piston-pump rebuilds, electrohydraulic control valves.
  • Drivetrain: Hydrostatic dual-path pumps and drive motors, charge pump, filters. CTL undercarriage adds track chains, sprockets, idlers, rollers.
  • Bucket coupler hardware: 47396814K bucket pin kit cross-fits L216-L230, C232-C238, SR160-SR250, SV185-SV300, TV380, TR270-TR340.
  • Hardware: 191048A2 axle stud & nut cross-fits broadly across the Case skid steer line.
  • Brakes / control: Park brake, control linkage, 48139268 control shock absorber on Case/NH alpha-series control valves.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, machine controller, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness, telematics module.
  • Cab / operator station: Cab HVAC, seat, gauges, joystick controls, cab glass, 47405956 windshield wiper blade on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Aftertreatment + undercarriage generation. Tier 4 Final aftertreatment configuration varies by build year; CTL undercarriage components (tracks, sprockets, idlers, rollers) saw revisions - confirm by serial.
  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the Case TR270B before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations.

Why the TR270B still earns a paycheck

The Case TR270B is an alpha-series CTL for operations that need the flotation and ground-pressure profile of tracks with the ground-level reach pattern of radial-lift geometry. Landscape, sensitive turf, soft material, and any operation where wheels would sink — the TR270B keeps working.

Maintenance priorities on a TR270B: clean fuel and tight water separation, DPF regen behavior, hydrostatic charge pressure, and undercarriage wear monitoring (track tension, sprocket and roller wear, idler condition). CTL undercarriage life is the single largest recurring cost on this machine — service-life tracking matters.
