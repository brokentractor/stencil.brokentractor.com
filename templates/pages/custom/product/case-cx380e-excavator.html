Where the CX380E fits in the Case excavator line

The CX380E is a 38-ton class E-Series excavator sitting between the CX370D and the CX460 in Case’s full-size excavator lineup. The E-Series brought Tier 4 Final emissions to the heavy end of the Case excavator family, paired with refined load-sensing hydraulics and electrohydraulic main control valves.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX380E runs a Tier 4 Final diesel with common-rail injection, cooled EGR, DPF, and SCR aftertreatment with DEF dosing. The main hydraulic circuit is a load-sensing piston pump arrangement feeding boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel through electrohydraulic control valves. Travel is hydrostatic dual-path through final drives onto a steel-track undercarriage.

The hydraulic system on a 38-ton class production excavator is the heart of the machine - cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild parts, swing motor service, and electrohydraulic valve components are the highest-volume long-life service items. The Tier 4 Final aftertreatment adds DPF service intervals, DEF tank refills, NOx sensor service, and SCR catalyst monitoring to the maintenance program.

Common service items on the CX380E

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, bearings.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service and cleaning, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler service, NOx sensors.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, high-pressure fuel pump, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket, and swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild kits, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track chains and shoes, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear, swing bearing, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The CX380E’s DPF and SCR systems are dependable on clean fuel and quality DEF, but they need attention. Keep the DEF tank topped up with spec-grade fluid. Expect periodic DPF high-temperature regenerations. NOx sensors fail occasionally and will trigger engine derate if not replaced. Factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget on any production CX380E.

What to verify before ordering

  • Machine serial number. Confirm the serial on the CX380E before ordering year-specific parts. The plate is on the RH lower cab frame, below the windshield.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions across the production run - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX380E still earns a paycheck

The Case CX380E is a 38-ton class production excavator - general site work, utility installation, residential and commercial earthmoving, and mining and quarry work depending on configuration.
