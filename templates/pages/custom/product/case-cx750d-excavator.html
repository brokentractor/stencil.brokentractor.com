Where the CX750D fits in the Case excavator line

The CX750D is a 75-ton class D-Series hydraulic excavator. It slots between the CX490D and the CX800 in Case's full-size excavator lineup, aimed at mining, quarry, and large production earthmoving applications where machine weight, bucket capacity, and hydraulic power matter more than mobility.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX750D runs a Tier 4 Final diesel engine through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump (commonly a load-sensing piston pump) drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel circuits through electrohydraulic control valves.

Tier 4 Final emissions compliance means a DPF (diesel particulate filter), SCR (selective catalytic reduction) catalyst with DEF dosing, cooled EGR, and a common-rail fuel injection system feeding the engine. The aftertreatment package adds service requirements not present on earlier C-Series machines - DEF tank top-ups, periodic DPF regenerations, and NOx sensor service all factor into the maintenance budget.

Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The CX750D's DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up, DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations, and NOx sensors fail occasionally and can trigger derate conditions. For fleets running CX750D machines in production, build aftertreatment service into the planned maintenance schedule.

Common service items on the CX750D

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, common-rail injection components.
  • Tier 4 Final aftertreatment: DPF service and regeneration, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket and swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick-coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track chains, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear, swing bearing, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the machine serial before ordering year-specific parts - the D-Series saw running changes through production.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number, not just the machine serial.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Seal kit fit depends on cylinder bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Standard and mass-excavation undercarriage variants exist - confirm track shoe width and roller count.

Why the CX750D still earns a paycheck

The Case CX750D is a true production-class machine - 75-ton excavators are deployed for mining, quarry, large-scale site preparation, utility installation at scale, and heavy commercial earthmoving. At this size class, machine availability and parts response time directly drive job economics, so component-level support matters as much as the original purchase decision.

Maintenance priorities on a CX750D: clean fuel and tight water separation for the common-rail injection system, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic oil condition monitoring, swing-bearing lubrication on schedule, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service across the boom and dipper.
