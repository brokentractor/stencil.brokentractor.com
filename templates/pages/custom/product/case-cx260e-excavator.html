Where the CX260E fits in the Case excavator line

The CX260E is a 26-ton class E-Series excavator. It sits between the CX250D and the CX290 in Case's full-size excavator lineup and represents the current Tier 4 Final emissions generation of the 26-ton platform.

The E-Series carries forward the proven Case CX architecture - load-sensing piston main pump, electrohydraulic control valves, hydrostatic dual-path travel, and a steel-track undercarriage - with refined Tier 4 Final aftertreatment, modernized cab electronics, and updated operator controls.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX260E runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump - typically a load-sensing piston pump - drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel functions through electrohydraulic control valves.

Tier 4 Final aftertreatment uses DPF for particulate control and SCR with DEF dosing for NOx control. The common-rail fuel system requires clean fuel and tight water separation to keep injectors healthy. Swing is driven by a dedicated swing motor and reduction gear riding a serviced swing bearing.

Tier 4 Final - service implications The CX260E's DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they need attention. DEF tank must stay topped up. DPF will request periodic regenerations. NOx sensors fail occasionally and trigger derate conditions. Common-rail injectors don't tolerate fuel contamination - keep water separators current.

Common service items on the CX260E

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. Common-rail injection.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service and regeneration behavior, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket, and swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the CX260E before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX260E still earns a paycheck

The Case CX260E is a 26-ton class production excavator built for general site work, utility installation, residential and commercial earthmoving, and mining and quarry work depending on configuration. It carries the current Tier 4 Final emissions package on a proven full-size Case CX architecture, with parts coverage backed by the broader CNH/Case dealer network and aftermarket.

Maintenance priorities on a CX260E: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regeneration behavior tracking, hydraulic oil condition, swing-bearing lubrication, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service across the boom and dipper.
