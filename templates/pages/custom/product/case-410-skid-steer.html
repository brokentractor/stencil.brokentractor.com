Where the 410 fits in the Case skid steer / CTL line

The 410 sits in the Case 400-series skid steer / CTL range between the Case 95XT and the Case 420. Tier 3 compact diesel architecture, electronic engine controls, and the cab and instrumentation that defined Case’s mid-2000s skid steer / CTL line before the alpha rename.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 410 runs a Tier 3 compact diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain. Loader hydraulics use a piston pump with electrohydraulic control valves. A wet-disc park brake is integrated with the hydrostatic deceleration logic.

The drive system gives the operator independent left/right track or wheel control through dual joysticks (on ISO/H-pattern equipped machines) or the legacy hand-and-foot configuration on earlier orders. Loader-arm geometry is the radial-lift pattern Case carried throughout the 400-series.

Common service items on the 410

  • Engine internals: Tier 3 compact diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. Common-rail high-pressure injection.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, very tight water-separator filtration.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulics: Loader lift and bucket tilt cylinder seal kits (86570919 / 86570931 / 86570922 cross-fit on NH/Case alpha-series), piston-pump rebuilds, electrohydraulic control valves.
  • Drivetrain: Hydrostatic dual-path pumps and drive motors, charge pump, filters.
  • Hardware: 191048A2 axle stud & nut cross-fits broadly across the Case skid steer line.
  • Brakes / control: Park brake, control linkage, 48139268 control shock absorber on Case/NH alpha-series control valves.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, machine controller, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness, telematics module.
  • Cab / operator station: Cab HVAC, seat, gauges, joystick controls, cab glass, 47405956 windshield wiper blade on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the Case 410 before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Aftertreatment generation. Tier 3 configuration varies by build year - confirm EGR/DPF layout before ordering.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations.
  • Control pattern. ISO/H-pattern vs. hand-and-foot - control valves and linkage differ.

Why the 410 still earns a paycheck

The Case 410 is the mid-2000s Case skid steer loader that bridged the XT-series and the current alpha lineup. Tier 3 emissions, modern electronics, and cab ergonomics that put the operator in a better seat than the XT predecessor.

Maintenance priorities on a 410: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, EGR behavior on Tier 3 builds, hydrostatic charge pressure, and drive-chain tension. Broken Tractor maintains parts coverage across the 400-series and the broader Case skid steer / CTL family.
