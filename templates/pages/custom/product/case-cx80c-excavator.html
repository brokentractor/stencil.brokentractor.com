Where the CX80C fits in the Case excavator line

The CX80C sits in Case's midi excavator range (7-10 ton class), between the mini line and the full-size CX130 and larger machines. Its conventional tail-swing geometry maximizes counterweight effectiveness and gives the operator full mid-size digging stability and bucket reach, while still keeping the road footprint small enough for utility, residential, and urban site work.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX80C runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. Main hydraulic pump drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel functions through electrohydraulic control valves. Conventional tail-swing geometry maximizes counterweight effectiveness for digging stability, and steel tracks support the heavier midi-class weights and abrasive-material work that mini machines aren't built for.

Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - DPF, DEF dosing, SCR catalyst, and EGR - adds service requirements that didn't exist on earlier emissions tiers. Clean fuel, quality DEF, and routine DPF service are not optional on a machine in this class.

Common service items on the CX80C

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, common-rail injection components.
  • Tier 4 Final aftertreatment: DPF service intervals and regeneration behavior, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket, and swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harnesses, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications DPF and SCR systems on the CX80C are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they aren't maintenance-free. DEF tank must stay topped up, the DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations, and NOx sensors and DPF pressure sensors fail occasionally and can trigger derate conditions if ignored. Factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the CX80C before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX80C still earns a paycheck

The Case CX80C is the midi-class excavator for jobs that demand more reach, depth, and bucket capacity than a mini can deliver but don't need the road footprint of a full-size 13+ ton machine. Utility contractors, urban construction crews, mid-size site work, and residential bulk excavation are the natural home for the 8-ton class.

Maintenance priorities on a CX80C: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic system condition, steel-track tension and wear monitoring, swing-bearing lubrication, and pin and bushing service across the boom and dipper.
