Where the 245 P-Tier fits in the JD excavator line

The 245 P-Tier sits between the 210 P-Tier and 250 P-Tier in the modern John Deere P-Tier construction excavator lineup. It runs Tier 4 Final emissions hardware and benefits from the long-running JD/Hitachi shared platform, which keeps service-part availability strong across the adjacent 230, 245, and 250 families. The hydraulic seal kit PV7003 (230CLC/250G/250GLC/250P) covers the closely related 250 family and gives an idea of how broadly Deere shares service parts across this class.

What the P-Tier generation brings

  • Tier 4 Final emissions. Cooled EGR, DPF, and SCR with DEF dosing for NOx control. Service discipline on clean fuel and quality DEF is essential.
  • Refined operator station. Larger sealed-and-pressurized cab, improved climate control, full-color monitor display, refined joystick controls.
  • Electronic hydraulic management. Pump and valve controls modulate flow against load for better fuel efficiency and finer multifunction control.
  • JD/Hitachi platform commonality. The 245 P-Tier shares many service parts with adjacent JD excavator families - boom pins, hydraulic seal kits, undercarriage components - which helps parts coverage in the field.
  • Standard LED work lighting. Most configurations ship with LED packages.
Locating your serial number On the John Deere 245 P-Tier the serial number plate is mounted on the upper frame near the cab entry, typically on the right-hand side beneath the door threshold or beside the operator’s seat. Always verify the serial number before ordering parts to ensure proper fitment - the P-Tier production run includes running changes to engine, aftertreatment, and hydraulic components.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 245 P-Tier runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic drive into a steel-track undercarriage. The base engine drives the main hydraulic pump that supplies the implement circuit (boom, arm, bucket), the swing motor, and the travel motors at each track. Electronic engine and hydraulic controls coordinate engine speed and pump displacement to deliver power on demand while meeting fuel-economy targets.

Boom and arm pivot pins on the 245 are part of the broad JD construction-excavator family. The AT317252 cross-pin is the broad JD excavator family service pin (180CW/190DW/200/200C/200G/210G/225C/230GW/2054/180G/190GW/210P/220DW/225D/245G/2154D) that fits across the JD P-Tier and G-Tier construction line - a good example of how the shared JD/Hitachi platform consolidates service parts.

Common service items on the 245 P-Tier

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel rebuild kits - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets, head bolts.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF assemblies, SCR catalysts, DEF dosing units, NOx sensors, DPF differential pressure sensors.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, high-pressure pump, lift pump, fuel filters, water separator.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, hydraulic oil cooler, EGR cooler, charge-air cooler, heat exchanger, hoses, thermostat.
  • Hydraulics: Main pump, control valve, swing motor, travel motors, boom/arm/bucket cylinders, seal kits.
  • Boom pins: AT317252 cross-pin (broad JD family fitment).
  • Undercarriage: Steel track chains, shoes, rollers, idlers, sprockets, idler tensioners.
  • Cab and electrical: Glass, weather seals, A/C compressor, alternator, starter, ECU, monitor display, harnesses, sensors.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The 245 P-Tier’s DPF and SCR systems run reliably when fed clean diesel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up, DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations, and NOx sensors fail occasionally - triggering derate conditions if not replaced. For customers running a 245 P-Tier in production, factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the upper frame before ordering year-specific parts - the P-Tier run includes running changes.
  • Engine and aftertreatment serial. Tier 4 Final emissions parts are serial-specific. Match against the engine and aftertreatment tags.
  • Aftertreatment generation. DPF and SCR revisions saw running changes through the production run.
  • Boom-pin position. The AT317252 cross-pin fits a broad JD family - confirm pin position (boom foot, dipper, bucket linkage) before ordering.
  • Hydraulic pump generation. Main pump and valve revisions saw running changes across the JD/Hitachi platform.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Confirm track shoe width and link count against the machine spec tag.

Why the 245 P-Tier earns its paycheck

The 245 P-Tier stays in service because modern P-Tier 24.5-ton JD excavators deliver the Tier 4 Final fuel efficiency and operator-station refinement that contractors rely on for production digging, trenching, and utility work. JD/Hitachi platform parts sharing means service parts are widely available, and the broad cross-fit of items like the AT317252 boom pin keeps downtime short when wear items need to come out.

Maintenance priorities on a 245 P-Tier: clean fuel and quality DEF (Tier 4 Final common-rail injectors and SCR catalysts don’t tolerate contamination), aftertreatment service discipline, hydraulic oil cleanliness, scheduled boom-pin service, undercarriage replacement on wear schedule, and cab and electrical service.
