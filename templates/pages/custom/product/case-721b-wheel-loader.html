Where the 721B fits in the Case wheel loader line

The 721B is the B-revision of Case's 721 platform - between the original 721 and the 721C. The B-gen ZF-axle option ties brake-disc parts to the 621B and 821B siblings, making the 721B the middle entry in a three-machine brake-disc cross-fit family.

Mechanically the 721B carries the articulated frame, power-shift transmission, and Case inline diesel that defined the original 721, with revisions to the driveline, axle options, and operator station that distinguish the B-generation from its predecessor.

What defines the B-generation 721

  • Driveline refinement. The B-gen refined the power-shift driveline carried over from the original 721 platform.
  • ZF axle option. Many 721B units shipped with ZF axles, defining the 119433A1 / 119430A1 brake-disc fitment family shared with the 621B and 821B.
  • Brake-disc cross-fit. The 621B / 721B / 821B trio share wet-disc brake service parts on ZF-equipped machines, simplifying parts identification across the B-gen wheel loader range.
  • Operator station. Cab and ROPS canopy configurations both ran through production. Cab-equipped machines use the 47880728 fresh-air filter.
  • Pre-emissions architecture. Mechanical fuel system, no aftertreatment - straightforward service compared to later Tier 4 machines.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 721B runs a Case inline diesel through a power-shift transmission and articulated drive axles. ZF-axle configurations are common - they define the 119433A1 / 119430A1 brake-disc fitment family shared with the 621B and 821B. The loader hydraulic circuit drives the bucket lift and tilt cylinders through a pump and control valve sized to the 721 chassis.

The articulated steering uses hydraulic steering cylinders at the center pivot. The G109152 steering cylinder bushing is a common service item that fits across the 521F, 621, 721, and 821 series. Wet-disc service brakes on ZF axles are the highest-volume cross-fit service category on this generation.

Common service items on the 721B

  • Engine internals: Case diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. The R49482 engine motor mount is a high-volume Case diesel service item.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Loader hydraulics: Cylinder seal kits, pump rebuilds, control valves, hoses.
  • Articulation steering: Steering cylinder seal kits, articulation pivot pins and bushings. G109152 steering cylinder bushing fits 521F, 621, 721, 821 series.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, bushings, pivot pins.
  • Drivetrain: Power-shift clutch packs, transmission, axle seals, planetary final drives.
  • Brakes: Wet-disc service brakes. 119433A1 lined brake disc and 119430A1 brake disc fit 621B/721B/821B with ZF axles. S300718 brake shoe, A12985 slave cylinder, and N8695 master cylinder are recurring items.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.
  • Operator station: Seat, gauges, control levers, cab parts. 47880728 cab fresh-air filter on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Generation matters. The 721-series saw multiple generations - confirm by serial before ordering.
  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the left side frame near the articulation joint before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Case diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Axle make. ZF vs. non-ZF axle configurations affect brake-disc, planetary, and final-drive parts.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied. Cab-equipped units carry filters and HVAC parts that ROPS-only units don't.

Why the 721B still earns a paycheck

The 721B is the B-revision production wheel loader from 1994-1999. Pre-emissions, mechanical service architecture, and the ZF-axle brake-disc cross-fit that simplifies parts identification across the 621B / 721B / 821B trio make it a straightforward machine to keep running. The Case inline diesel and power-shift transmission are both well-documented, well-supported platforms with a deep aftermarket parts pool.

For customers running a 721B, maintenance priorities are: clean fuel and quality filtration, hydraulic seal replacement on aging cylinders, and brake-disc service for ZF-axle machines. The B-gen brake-disc family available at Broken Tractor covers the entire 621B/721B/821B production run.
