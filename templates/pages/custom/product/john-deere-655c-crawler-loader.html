John Deere 655C Crawler Loader: 115 HP Production Track Loader

Produced in the mid-2000s, the 655C stepped JD's crawler loader line up to roughly 115 HP from a turbocharged PowerTech 4045 diesel - a larger machine than the mid-class 555G with the bucket capacity, cycle speed, and breakout force needed for production load-and-carry work.

Where the 655C fits in the JD crawler loader family

JD's crawler loader line stepped through compact (450/455-series at roughly 80 HP) and mid-size (555G) classes before reaching the production-class 655. The 655C is the larger production track loader - built on a heavier chassis than the 555G, with more bucket capacity, more breakout force, and the JD PowerTech 4045 turbo diesel making roughly 115 HP.

The 655C carries the hydrostatic dual-path drive established across JD's crawler loader line: two independent pump-and-motor circuits, one per track, with operator joystick or T-bar control. Counter-rotation, infinitely variable speed, and the operator workflow that defines a modern track loader.

What sets the 655C apart from the 555G

  • Power class. The 655C's turbocharged PowerTech 4045 makes roughly 115 HP - a meaningful step up over the mid-class 555G for production load-and-carry work.
  • Chassis. Heavier frame, larger undercarriage footprint, and bucket capacities sized for production cycles rather than mid-class utility work.
  • Hydraulics. Larger implement circuit feeds bigger bucket cylinders and supports 4-in-1 multi-purpose configurations with clamshell operation.
  • Pre-Tier 4 emissions. The 655C runs without DPF or SCR - no DEF, no regeneration cycles, simpler aftertreatment service compared with the K-generation successor.
  • Engine commonality. The PowerTech 4045 platform is shared with the 650H / 650J crawler dozers, which keeps internal engine parts well-stocked across the dealer and aftermarket networks.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 655C's turbocharged JD PowerTech 4045 diesel powers the hydrostatic dual-path drive system, the implement hydraulic circuit (bucket lift, tilt, and clamshell on 4-in-1 configurations), and the auxiliary hydraulics. The PowerTech 4045 platform is one of the most widely produced JD engine families - broad dealer support, well-stocked aftermarket parts, and parts commonality with the 650H / 650J crawler dozers built on similar engine architecture.

Bucket configurations included standard production buckets in multiple capacities, 4-in-1 multi-purpose buckets with clamshell operation, and specialty buckets for forestry, demolition, and material handling. Cylinder bore and stroke vary with bucket configuration. The undercarriage carries the JD-pattern sealed-and-lubricated track chain, planetary final drives, and JD-pattern rollers and idlers in standard, narrow, and LGP variants.

Common service items on the 655C

  • Hydrostatic pumps and motors: Drive pump rebuilds, drive motor rebuilds, charge pump, filter housings, case drain hoses.
  • Turbocharger: Turbo rebuilds, oil supply and drain lines, charge-air piping, intercooler on equipped configurations.
  • Hydraulic filtration: Suction strainers, return filters, charge filter, hydraulic oil.
  • Undercarriage: Track chains, track shoes, rollers, idlers, sprockets, idler springs.
  • Engine internals: JD PowerTech 4045 turbo rebuild kits - pistons (turbo-specific), rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, high-pressure fuel pump, lift pump, fuel filters, water separator.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, hydraulic oil cooler, water pump, thermostat, hoses, fan belt.
  • Bucket components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, side cutters, pins, bushings.
  • Implement hydraulics: Bucket cylinders, seal kits, control valves, hoses.
  • Final drives: Sprockets, planetary gears, seals, bearings.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, harnesses, switches, gauges, control modules.
  • Cab: Cab glass, weather seals, door parts, seat, A/C components.
Pre-Tier 4 simplicity The 655C runs without DPF, SCR, or DEF dosing. That removes a meaningful service burden compared with the K-generation successor and is one reason these machines remain attractive in production fleets a decade later.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Match parts to the machine serial - running changes occurred through the production run.
  • Engine serial. PowerTech 4045 turbo diesel internal parts must match the engine serial.
  • Turbocharger variant. Turbo part numbers can vary across the production run.
  • Hydrostatic pump/motor variant. Drive pump and motor part numbers can vary.
  • Bucket configuration. Standard, 4-in-1, and specialty buckets have different cylinder configurations and tooth patterns.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Standard, narrow, and LGP variants existed.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Glass, A/C, and door parts differ.

Why the 655C is a productive used pick

The 655C hits a production-class sweet spot in the JD crawler loader line: PowerTech 4045 turbo power for breakout force and fast cycle times, hydrostatic dual-path drive for maneuverability, and pre-Tier 4 emissions simplicity (no DPF, no SCR, no DEF). Parts availability is strong because the 4045 engine family and the JD-pattern undercarriage are shared across high-volume dozer and loader machines.

Maintenance priorities on a 655C: clean cool hydraulic oil, on-schedule filter changes, turbocharger oil supply integrity, undercarriage replacement as it wears, and engine rebuilds when wear demands. Broken Tractor maintains parts coverage across the 655 family.
