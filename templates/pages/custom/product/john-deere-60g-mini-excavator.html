The John Deere 60G is the largest member of the JD G-Series mini excavator line - a 6.0-ton machine that sits at the top of the mini class. Configurable with steel or rubber tracks, it delivers more lift and dig than the 50G while keeping the mini-class trailerability contractors want.

Where the 60G fits in the JD excavator line

The 60G is the largest member of the JD G-Series mini excavator line - sitting above the 50G. With a 6-ton operating weight, it offers more lift capacity and dig depth than the 50G while remaining within mini-class trailering envelope. Steel-or-rubber-track configurations let the machine match the job - rubber for sensitive surfaces, steel for rocky or abrasive ground.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 60G runs a Yanmar diesel through a hydrostatic drive into a rubber- or steel-track undercarriage. The Yanmar engine family is widely supported in the mini-excavator world, and JD’s service literature on the G-Series is comprehensive. The MIU802262 starter is a direct-fit Case 60G excavator service part that cross-fits JD skid steers and backhoes - one of the broader cross-platform service items on the machine.

The hydraulic system drives the boom, arm, bucket, swing, and travel circuits through a main pump and control valve assembly. Travel motors live in the final drives at each end of the undercarriage. The swing circuit uses a dedicated swing motor and reduction gearbox riding on a swing bearing at the upper-to-lower-frame interface.

Common service items on the 60G

  • Engine: Yanmar diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets, overhaul kits.
  • Starter / electrical: MIU802262 starter (direct fit, cross-fits skid steers and backhoes), alternator, sensors, harness.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, hydraulic oil cooler.
  • Hydraulics: Main pump, control valve, swing motor, travel motors, cylinders.
  • Undercarriage: Rubber or steel tracks, idlers, track rollers, sprocket, recoil spring and adjuster.
  • Cab and glass: Cab glass replacement service, door, mirror.
  • Swing: Swing bearing, swing motor, swing reduction gearbox.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the frame below the cab door before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Yanmar internals are calibrated to specific engine serials.
  • Track type. 60G can be rubber- or steel-track equipped - confirm undercarriage configuration before ordering tracks or shoes.
  • Starter cross-fit. MIU802262 fits 60G + skid steers + backhoes - confirm engine bay configuration before ordering.
  • Hydraulic generation. Pump revisions saw running changes during G-Series production.

Why the 60G still earns a paycheck

The 60G stays in service because contractors who need more lift and dig than the 50G - utility deep-trench work, light demolition, larger landscape projects - but still want mini-class trailerability and tight-access capability default to the 60G. The steel-track option makes the machine useful in rocky or abrasive conditions where rubber tracks would wear too fast.

Maintenance priorities on a 60G: clean Yanmar fuel, undercarriage wear monitoring (track-type dependent), swing bearing grease, hydraulic oil cleanliness, and electrical service.
