Where the 721 fits in the Case wheel loader line

The original 721 launched the 721-series lineage - predecessor to the 721B through the current 721G. It sits as a production-class chassis above the 621-series and below the 821-series in the Case lineup.

The 721 platform set the formula carried forward through every subsequent generation: a Case inline diesel, power-shift transmission, articulated frame, and a hydraulic loader sized for a production duty cycle. Serial-plate location is on the left frame behind the front tire near the articulation joint.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The original 721 runs a Case inline diesel through a power-shift transmission to planetary final drives at each axle. Loader hydraulics use a gear or piston pump feeding mechanical control valves and direct-acting cylinders for lift and tilt. Articulation steering is driven by hydraulic cylinders between the front and rear chassis halves.

Service brakes are wet-disc. Cooling is a single-radiator setup with mechanical fan drive. The cab on equipped units offers a fresh-air ventilation circuit; ROPS canopy configurations were also offered.

Common service items on the 721

  • Engine internals: Case diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. The R49482 engine motor mount is a high-volume Case diesel service item.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Loader hydraulics: Cylinder seal kits, pump rebuilds, control valves, hoses.
  • Articulation steering: Steering cylinder seal kits, articulation pivot pins and bushings. G109152 steering cylinder bushing fits 521F, 621, 721, and 821 series.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, bushings, pivot pins.
  • Drivetrain: Power-shift clutch packs, transmission internals, axle seals, planetary final drives. Transmission shift lever assembly for service.
  • Brakes: Wet-disc service brakes. 119433A1 lined brake disc and 119430A1 brake disc fit 621B/721B/821B with ZF axles. S300718 brake shoe, A12985 slave cylinder, and N8695 master cylinder are recurring items.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.
  • Operator station: Seat, gauges, control levers, cab parts. 47880728 cab fresh-air filter on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Generation matters. The 721-series saw multiple generations through B/C/D/E/F/G. Confirm by serial before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the left frame behind the front tire before ordering.
  • Engine serial. Case diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Axle variant. ZF-axle and Case-axle configurations differ - brake disc and final-drive part numbers depend on axle make.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between cab and canopy units.

Why the 721 still earns a paycheck

The original Case 721 is the foundation of a production-class wheel loader lineage that ran from the original through B/C/D/E/F/G generations - a platform Case kept refining for decades because the formula worked. Articulated, power-shift, Case-diesel-powered, and sized for a production duty cycle, the 721 still pulls real shifts at landscape yards, municipal yards, and small material-handling operations across the country.

Maintenance priorities on a 721: clean fuel and quality filtration on the Case diesel, hydraulic seal replacement on the loader and steering circuits, articulation pivot service, and wet-disc brake service on schedule.
