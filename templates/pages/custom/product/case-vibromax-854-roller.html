Where the Vibromax 854 fits in the Case roller line

The Case Vibromax 854 is the smaller of the two Case Vibromax rollers in the North American Case construction lineup - below the Vibromax 1102 in drum width and machine weight. Tandem vibratory configuration sized for residential paving, parking lots, and mid-volume compaction work that doesn’t justify the 1102’s drum width and operating weight.

The articulated tandem layout - front and rear drums with an articulation joint between them - gives operators tight maneuverability around curbs, driveways, and confined work zones while still delivering true dynamic compaction force on both passes.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The Vibromax 854 runs a hydrostatic drive into twin steel drums - front and rear - with an articulation joint between them. Each drum carries a vibration assembly (eccentric shaft with bearings) driven by its own hydraulic motor for dynamic compaction force. Smaller drum width and lower static weight than the 1102, but the same architecture and the same service patterns.

The diesel engine powers a hydrostatic pump that feeds drive motors on each drum, with a parallel hydraulic circuit driving the vibration motors. Clean fuel, clean hydraulic oil, and disciplined filter service are the foundations of long hydrostatic life on this machine.

Common service items on the Vibromax 854

  • Engine internals: Diesel engine pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets - full overhaul kits matched to the production engine.
  • Cooling system: Water pump, radiator, hoses, thermostat, fan, oil cooler service.
  • Hydrostatic drive: Hydrostatic pump and motor rebuild, charge pump, hydrostatic filters, drive-circuit hoses and fittings.
  • Vibration assembly: Vibration bearing kits (drum eccentric shaft), vibration motors, isolation mounts, vibration drive hoses.
  • Drums: Drum scraper bars, scraper hardware, drum bearing service, drum drive coupling.
  • Articulation: Articulation joint pivot pins, bushings, steering cylinder seal kits.
  • Main hydraulics: Main hydraulic pump rebuild, control valve service, hydraulic filters, return-line hoses.
  • Operator station + electrical: Seat, joysticks, controls, gauges, alternator, starter, wiring harness.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the chassis near the rear wheel housing or the cab data plate before ordering year-specific drum, vibration, or hydrostatic parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts are calibrated to specific engine serial groups - confirm separately from the chassis serial.
  • Vibration bearing position. Drum vibration bearing kits are position-specific (drive end vs. idle end) - confirm before ordering.
  • Hydrostatic generation. Hydrostatic pump and motor revisions saw running changes during production - confirm by serial.
  • Drum width. Vibromax 854 uses a narrower drum than the 1102 - confirm drum-width-specific parts (scraper bars, drum-edge hardware) before ordering.

Why the Vibromax 854 still earns a paycheck

The Case Vibromax 854 stays in service because mid-size tandem vibratory rollers are the right tool for residential paving, parking lots, and trench compaction jobs that don’t justify the 1102’s drum width and operating weight. Articulated steering and twin-drum coverage make the machine versatile across both soil and asphalt work.

Maintenance priorities on a Vibromax 854 are the same as on the 1102 - vibration bearings (severe dynamic load), hydrostatic drive (clean oil and filtration), articulation pivot lubrication, and cooling system condition. Broken Tractor maintains parts coverage for the Case Vibromax line.
