Where the 621G fits in the Case wheel loader line

The 621G is the current production version of Case's 621 platform - successor to the 621F. G-series Tier 4 Final emissions kit, G-series cab, refined power-shift transmission, telematics integration. It sits between the smaller 521G and the larger 721G in the current G-generation lineup.

What changed from the 621F to the 621G

  • Engine. Tier 4 Final Case diesel with refined common-rail fuel system and electronic engine control.
  • Aftertreatment. Full DPF + DEF + SCR package - periodic DPF regenerations, DEF tank service, NOx sensor monitoring, EGR cooler service.
  • Cab and operator station. G-series cab with refined controls, color monitor, and telematics integration.
  • Power-shift transmission. Refined modulation and shift logic carried into the G generation.
  • Loader hydraulics. Piston pump with electrohydraulic control valves.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The 621G's DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up. DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations. NOx sensors fail occasionally and trigger derate conditions if not replaced. Factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget on any production 621G.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 621G runs a Tier 4 Final Case diesel through a power-shift transmission with full DPF + DEF + SCR aftertreatment. Loader hydraulics use a piston pump with electrohydraulic control valves. Articulation steering is hydraulic, with steering cylinders driving the center pivot.

The serial plate is stamped on the left frame rail near the front axle, with a duplicate data plate inside the cab. Confirm the serial before ordering year-specific parts - running changes occurred through the G production run.

Common service items on the 621G

  • Engine internals: Case diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. R49482 engine motor mount is a recurring service item.
  • Tier 4 aftertreatment: DPF, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injection components, electronic injectors, lift pump, tight water-separator filtration.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Loader hydraulics: Cylinder seal kits, piston-pump rebuilds, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Articulation steering: Steering cylinder seal kits, articulation pivot pins and bushings. G109152 steering cylinder bushing fits 521F, 621, 721, 821 series.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, bushings, pivot pins, quick coupler.
  • Drivetrain: Power-shift clutch packs, transmission, axle seals, planetary final drives. Transmission shift lever assembly for service.
  • Brakes: Wet-disc service brakes. S300718 brake shoe, A12985 slave cylinder, and N8695 master cylinder are recurring items across the platform.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness, telematics module. 87528688 AC blower speed resistor fits 521E through 1121F.
  • Cab / Climate: Seat, gauges, control levers, cab parts. 47880728 cab fresh-air filter on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Generation matters. The 621-series saw multiple generations - confirm by serial before ordering.
  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the left frame rail near the front axle before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Case diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Aftertreatment configuration. DPF and SCR part numbers can vary across the G production run.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied - confirm before ordering cab-specific parts.

Why the 621G still earns a paycheck

The 621G is Case's current production-class wheel loader at the 621 size. Tier 4 Final emissions, DEF dosing, telematics, and the G-series cab give it a modern operator interface and modern service envelope. The 621 platform has decades of fleet history behind it, and the G generation is the version customers are running in current production fleets.

Maintenance priorities on a 621G: clean fuel and tight water separation (common-rail injection does not tolerate contamination), DPF/DEF/SCR service per Case schedule, hydraulic system condition, and brake service.
