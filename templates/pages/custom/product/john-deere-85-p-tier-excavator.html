John Deere 85 P-Tier Excavator: 8.5-Ton Top-of-Compact-Class Tier 4 Final

The John Deere 85 P-Tier is an 8.5-ton class compact excavator and the largest member of the JD P-Tier compact line. It carries Tier 4 Final emissions, an updated cab and controls, and a JD/Hitachi-shared service-parts heritage that reaches back through the 85D and 85G generations.

Where the 85 P-Tier fits in the John Deere excavator line

The 85 P-Tier is the largest member of the JD P-Tier compact excavator class - above the 75 P-Tier and stepping right up to the threshold of the mid-size excavator class. As the 8.5-ton class machine, it sits at the top of the compact-excavator size range where the platform overlaps with light mid-size work.

The lineage runs through the 85D and 85G generations of the same JD/Hitachi-platform 8-ton-class compact excavator before the P-Tier emissions-modernized refresh. Many service-parts dimensions carry across the generations - the 4684428 cab door glass fits 85D and 85G as a shared service part, and the same cab-opening compatibility extends to the P-Tier where dimensions match.

Locating your serial number On the John Deere 85 P-Tier the serial number plate is on the right-hand side of the upper frame, beneath the cab entry threshold or adjacent to the operator’s seat. Always verify the serial before ordering parts - the P-Tier saw running changes through production.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 85 P-Tier runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic drive into a steel-track undercarriage. Aftertreatment includes a diesel particulate filter (DPF) and selective catalytic reduction (SCR) catalyst with DEF dosing for NOx control. Clean fuel and quality DEF are non-negotiable on this generation.

The implement hydraulic circuit drives boom, arm, bucket, and an auxiliary line for thumb or hydraulic-coupler attachments. The swing motor and travel motors are JD/Hitachi-pattern components with strong dealer and aftermarket support. The steel-track undercarriage uses sealed-and-lubricated track chain, JD-pattern rollers and idlers, and a torsion-spring recoil and adjuster system.

Common service items on the 85 P-Tier

  • Engine: Tier 4 Final diesel internals - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets, overhaul kits.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF, DEF dosing units, SCR catalyst, NOx sensors, DPF pressure sensors.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, hydraulic oil cooler.
  • Hydraulics: Main pump, control valve, swing motor, travel motors, boom/arm/bucket cylinders, seal kits.
  • Undercarriage: Steel tracks, shoes, idlers, rollers, sprockets, recoil spring and adjuster.
  • Cab glass: 4684428 cab door glass (85D + 85G shared, fits P-Tier cab opening).
  • Electrical: Alternator, starter, sensors, harness, ECU, monitor display.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The 85 P-Tier’s DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up, the DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations, and NOx sensors fail occasionally and can trigger derate conditions if not replaced.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the machine serial on the upper-frame plate before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Tier 4 Final internal parts are matched to specific engine serial ranges.
  • Aftertreatment generation. Confirm DPF and SCR revision by serial - running changes occurred.
  • Cab glass position. 4684428 is the door-glass position only - windshield, side, and rear glass are separate part numbers.
  • Hydraulic pump revision. Main pump and travel motor part numbers can vary across the production run.
  • Auxiliary hydraulic configuration. Thumb-equipped and hydraulic-coupler configurations carry different valve and hose assemblies.

Why the 85 P-Tier still earns a paycheck

The 85 P-Tier stays in service because the 8.5-ton compact class is the workhorse size for commercial site prep, utility deep-trench work, residential foundation work, and light demolition. It’s big enough to do real production work and small enough to trailer on a standard tag-along behind a 3/4 or 1-ton truck.

For customers running an 85 P-Tier, maintenance priorities are: clean fuel and proper DEF (Tier 4 Final injectors and SCR catalysts don’t tolerate contamination), aftertreatment service intervals, steel-track undercarriage wear monitoring, hydraulic oil cleanliness, cab glass (4684428 door glass cross-fits 85D and 85G), and electrical service.
