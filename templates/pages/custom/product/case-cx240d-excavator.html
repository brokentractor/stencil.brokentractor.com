Where the CX240D fits in the Case excavator line

The CX240D is a 24-ton class D-Series excavator. It slots between the earlier B-Series CX240B and the short-radius CX245D SR in Case’s full-size excavator lineup. The D-Series brought the 24-ton platform to Tier 4 Final emissions with DPF and SCR aftertreatment, refined electrohydraulic controls, and a modernized cab.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX240D runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump - a load-sensing piston pump - drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel circuits through electrohydraulic control valves. Tier 4 Final aftertreatment adds DPF and SCR (with DEF dosing) to the exhaust side.

The implement side of the machine is conventional D-Series: boom, dipper, and bucket cylinders feed off the main pump; the swing motor and reduction gear drive the upper frame through a swing bearing; the propel motors drive the steel-track undercarriage with planetary reductions and proven rollers, idlers, and sprockets.

Common service items on the CX240D

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, common-rail injection components.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service and regeneration management, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water separation.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket, and swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild components, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The CX240D’s DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF must stay topped up, the DPF will request periodic regenerations, and NOx sensor or DPF pressure sensor faults can trigger derate. Factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget for any production CX240D.

What to verify before ordering

  • Machine serial number. Confirm the CX240D serial before ordering year-specific parts - the D-Series saw running revisions.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal-kit fit depends on bore - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and sensors are calibrated to specific build configurations.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX240D still earns a paycheck

The Case CX240D is a 24-ton class production excavator - general site work, utility installation, residential and commercial earthmoving, mining and quarry work depending on configuration. The D-Series platform combines a current-emissions Tier 4 Final engine with a load-sensing hydraulic system and modern electrohydraulic controls, making it a machine that fits cleanly into modern fleets without retrofit headaches.

Maintenance priorities on a CX240D: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic oil condition, swing-bearing lubrication, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service.
