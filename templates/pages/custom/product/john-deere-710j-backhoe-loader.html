The 710J is a heavy-duty machine engineered for demanding digging, loading, and earthmoving applications. Built with a Tier 3 PowerTech turbocharged engine and reinforced structural components, the 710J stands out as the largest and most-capable production backhoe in JD's J-Series lineup.

Where the 710J fits in the JD backhoe line

The 710J succeeded the 710G as JD's largest production backhoe loader. The J refresh brought EPA Tier 3 emissions compliance with a refined PowerTech turbocharged diesel, AutoShift transmission, electrohydraulic joystick controls, and continued refinement of the heaviest-duty hydraulic and structural systems in JD's industrial backhoe line.

For pipeline contractors and large-site excavation operations running production work, the 710J is the largest backhoe-loader JD builds in the J generation - maximum reach, maximum breakout force, maximum loading capacity from a single backhoe-loader platform.

What changed from the 710G to the 710J

  • Engine. EPA Tier 3 JD PowerTech turbocharged diesel. Refined fuel system with common-rail injection, electronic engine control, and cooled EGR.
  • AutoShift transmission. Electronically-controlled multi-speed transmission with automatic shifting under load - productivity matters on a backhoe of this size during transit between dig site and stockpile.
  • Electrohydraulic joystick controls. Replaces mechanical-linkage backhoe controls with electronic pilot-operated joysticks. Smoother modulation, lower operator effort.
  • Reinforced structural components. Continued refinement of the heaviest-duty loader arms, backhoe boom and stick.
  • Cab refinements. Updated displays, refined climate control, improved sightlines.
Tier 3 - no DPF, no DEF The 710J's PowerTech Tier 3 turbocharged diesel meets emissions with cooled EGR and common-rail injection - no diesel particulate filter (DPF), no DEF tank, no SCR catalyst. Materially simpler service than the Tier 4 backhoes that followed. For pipeline contractors and large-site operations that want modern J-Series productivity without aftertreatment service, the 710J is the preferred used pick at the largest end of the JD industrial backhoe line.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 710J's PowerTech Tier 3 turbocharged diesel drives an AutoShift transmission through wet-disc brakes into reinforced planetary final drives. AutoShift gives operators automatic shifting under load - important on a machine of this size where the operator is constantly transitioning between dig site, stockpile, and truck loading area. The largest-class hydraulic system delivers production flow and pressure to the largest backhoe in the JD industrial line.

Common service items on the 710J

  • Engine internals: JD PowerTech Tier 3 turbo rebuild kits - pistons (turbo-specific), rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Turbocharger: Turbo rebuilds, oil supply and drain lines, charge-air piping.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, high-pressure fuel pump, lift pump, fuel filters, water separator.
  • Engine electronics: ECU, sensors, harnesses, monitor display.
  • AutoShift transmission: Transmission internals, shift solenoids, electronic controls.
  • Electrohydraulic controls: Joystick assemblies, pilot control valves, control modules.
  • Wet-disc brakes: Brake disc packs, seals, oil-cooled brake housing components.
  • Largest-class hydraulics: Pump rebuilds, loader cylinder seal kits, backhoe boom/stick/bucket cylinder seal kits, control valves, hoses.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, EGR cooler, thermostat, hoses.
  • 4WD components (where equipped): Front axle assemblies, transfer case parts, drive shaft U-joints.
  • Loader/backhoe components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, side cutters, pins, swing tower pin kits, stabilizer leg cylinders.
  • Cab: Cab glass, weather seals, door parts, seat, A/C parts.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Match parts to the machine serial.
  • Engine serial. PowerTech Tier 3 internal parts must match the engine serial.
  • Tier 3 emissions configuration. Confirm cooled-EGR setup and common-rail fuel system components against engine serial.
  • Turbocharger variant. Turbo part numbers can vary across the production run.
  • 2WD vs. 4WD configuration. Critical to confirm before ordering front axle or drive components.
  • Loader/backhoe configuration. Standard, quick-attach, and extended-reach configurations had different cylinder and mounting specs - particularly important on the largest-class 710J backhoe.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Glass, weather seals, A/C parts, and door parts differ.

Why the 710J remains a pipeline contractor pick

The 710J is the largest production JD backhoe of the Tier 3 era - Tier 3 emissions without DPF/DEF, AutoShift productivity, electrohydraulic joystick controls, and the maximum reach and breakout force available from a backhoe-loader platform. For pipeline contractors and large-site excavation operations that need maximum capacity without Tier 4 aftertreatment service, the 710J is the heavy-class sweet spot.

Maintenance priorities on a 710J: clean fuel (Tier 3 common-rail doesn't tolerate contamination), AutoShift fluid changes on schedule, turbo oil supply integrity, wet-disc brake fluid service, largest-class hydraulic seal replacement, 4WD front axle service (where equipped), swing tower pin service, and engine rebuilds when wear demands.
