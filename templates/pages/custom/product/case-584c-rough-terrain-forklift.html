Where the 584C fits in the Case forklift line

The 584C is the C-series successor to the original pre-C Case 584. It sits between the original 584 and the larger 585C mid-range forklift in the Case rough terrain forklift family. The C-series shares its canonical steering motor valve (D90752) with the 585C, 586C, 584D, 585D, and 586D - making this one of the most cross-compatible service components on the C/D-series RT forklift platform.

Mechanically the 584C is a straightforward machine: Case diesel engine, power-shuttle transmission, mechanical front and rear axles, and a single-section gear-pump hydraulic system feeding the mast. That simplicity is exactly why these machines are still working - they are easy to diagnose, easy to repair, and the parts cross-fit broadly across the Case construction line.

What changed from the original 584 to the 584C

  • Steering hydraulics. The C-series brought the D90752 steering motor valve, which became the canonical steering component across the entire C and D-series RT forklift line.
  • Operator station refinements. Updated controls, gauges, and seat package compared to the original 584.
  • Drivetrain hardening. Refined power-shuttle modulation and axle components shared with the broader Case loader-backhoe and forklift family.
  • Cab options. ROPS canopy standard with an enclosed cab option available on equipped configurations.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 584C runs a Case mechanical-injection diesel through a power-shuttle transmission and mechanical front and rear axles. Mast hydraulics are single-section gear-pump driven with mechanical control valves and direct-acting lift and tilt cylinders. The D90752 steering motor valve is the canonical steering component across the C/D-series forklifts - and incorrect port connections during service cause immediate damage, so follow the installation line routing carefully.

The power-shuttle transmission is a defining feature on Case RT forklifts. It gives the operator instant forward/reverse direction changes without a manual clutch - a major productivity advantage in tight load-and-stack work. Clutch-pack and modulation-valve service are the principal wear items.

Common service items on the 584C

  • Engine internals: Case mechanical-injection diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, bearings.
  • Fuel system: Mechanical injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Power shuttle / transmission: Clutch packs, forward/reverse modulation valves, transmission filters and oil.
  • Steering hydraulics: D90752 steering motor valve - the canonical service part for 584C/D, 585C/D, 586C/D rough terrain forklifts.
  • Mast hydraulics: Lift cylinder seal kits, tilt cylinder seal kits, chain tensioner, control valves, hoses.
  • Mast components: Mast carriage rollers, lift chain inspection and replacement, fork carriage bushings, side-shift attachment service.
  • Drivetrain: Front and rear axles, planetary final drives, driveshaft, axle differential service. 175978A1 needle bearing pack and K395075 thrust washer cross-fit the broader Case forklift / loader-backhoe family.
  • Brakes: A168790 parking brake disc cross-fits Case forklift / loader-backhoe / trencher applications. Service brakes and brake hydraulics.
  • Cab / operator station: Seat, joystick and lever controls, gauges, key switch, ROPS canopy or enclosed cab. 377594A5 cab air filter fits Case loader tractor / backhoe / forklift cabs.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the 584C before ordering year-specific parts - running changes happened through the C-series.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number, not just the chassis serial.
  • Mast configuration. Lift height, carriage width, fork length, and side-shift options vary - confirm mast spec before ordering carriage, chain, or cylinder parts.
  • Two-wheel vs. four-wheel drive. Some Case RT forklift configurations exist in 2WD and 4WD - drivetrain parts differ.
  • Steering motor lines. D90752 service must follow port connection instructions - incorrect line routing causes immediate steering damage.
  • Power-shuttle generation. Power-shuttle transmissions saw revisions through production - confirm by serial before ordering clutch-pack components.

Why the 584C still earns a paycheck

The Case 584C is a long-production-run rough terrain forklift that earned its place in construction, lumberyard, and agricultural fleets. 5,000 lb capacity covers a wide range of jobsite loads, and the C-series cab and operator station are straightforward to maintain. Parts cross-fit across the broader Case construction line - the same drivetrain, brake, and cab components that show up on Case loader-backhoes and trenchers also serve the 584C.

Maintenance priorities on a 584C are practical: clean fuel, power-shuttle oil quality, mast chain inspection and lift cylinder seal replacement on schedule, steering motor service (D90752 is the canonical part), and brake service (A168790 cross-fits across the Case family).
