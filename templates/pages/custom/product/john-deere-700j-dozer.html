John Deere 700J Crawler Dozer: J-Generation Mid-to-Large Hydrostatic

Produced from the early 2000s through the 2010s, the 700J brought the JD mid-to-large crawler dozer line to EPA Tier 3 emissions with a PowerTech 6068 6-cylinder diesel, refined hydrostatic dual-path drive, and a modernized cab. The 700J slots between the 650 and the heavy-class 750.

Where the 700J fits in the JD crawler family

The 700-series JD crawler dozer slots between the mid-large 650 line and the heavy-class 750. The 700J - the J-gen iteration - runs the JD PowerTech 6068 6-cylinder diesel meeting EPA Tier 3 emissions, paired with hydrostatic dual-path drive. The step up from 4-cylinder (450 / 550 / 650 family) to 6-cylinder marks the move into the larger-displacement engine class used across JD’s heavier construction equipment.

Hydrostatic dual-path drive carried forward unchanged in concept - two independent pump-and-motor circuits, one per track, with operator T-bar or joystick control. Counter-rotation, infinitely variable speed, and independent track control across the larger 700J chassis. Standard, LGP, and LT (long-track) configurations were available.

What changed with the J generation

  • Engine. EPA Tier 3 JD PowerTech 6068 6-cylinder diesel. Cooled EGR and refined combustion meet Tier 3 without DPF or DEF.
  • Drivetrain. Refined hydrostatic dual-path drive with smoother modulation and improved low-speed control on the larger 700-class chassis.
  • Operator station. Modernized sealed cab with improved climate control and an updated monitor display showing engine, drive, and hydraulic status.
  • Configurations. Standard, LGP, and LT (long-track) undercarriage options to suit ground pressure and finish-grading needs.
  • Service profile. No diesel particulate filter (DPF), no DEF tank, no SCR catalyst - service is materially simpler than the Tier 4 Final 700K successor.
Tier 3 - no DPF, no DEF The 700J’s PowerTech 6068 Tier 3 diesel meets emissions with cooled EGR and refined combustion - no diesel particulate filter (DPF), no DEF tank or SCR catalyst. Service is materially simpler than the Tier 4 Final 700K. For customers in regions where Tier 3 machines are acceptable, the 700J is often the preferred used pick in this class.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 700J’s PowerTech 6068 Tier 3 6-cylinder diesel powers the dual-path drive system, the implement hydraulic circuit, and steering hydraulics. The 6068 platform is JD’s mid-large 6-cylinder workhorse - used across construction equipment, forestry machines, and agricultural tractors. Broad dealer support and well-stocked aftermarket parts.

The implement hydraulic circuit drives a 6-way blade on most configurations. Cylinder bore and stroke vary with blade configuration. The undercarriage carries the JD-pattern sealed-and-lubricated track chain, planetary final drives, and JD-pattern rollers and idlers in standard, LGP, or LT length configurations.

Common service items on the 700J

  • Hydrostatic pumps and motors: Drive pump rebuilds, drive motor rebuilds, charge pump components, filter housings, case drain hoses.
  • Hydraulic filtration: Suction strainers, return filters, charge filter, hydraulic oil.
  • Undercarriage: Track chains, track shoes, rollers, idlers, sprockets, idler springs.
  • Engine internals: JD PowerTech 6068 Tier 3 rebuild kits - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, high-pressure fuel pump, lift pump, fuel filters, water separator.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, hydraulic oil cooler, EGR cooler, water pump, thermostat, hoses.
  • Implement hydraulics: Blade cylinders, seal kits, control valves, hoses.
  • Final drives: Sprockets, planetary gears, seals, bearings.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, harnesses, ECU, monitor display, switches.
  • Cab: Cab glass, weather seals, door parts, seat, A/C compressor and components.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Match parts to the machine serial.
  • Engine serial. PowerTech 6068 Tier 3 internal parts must match the engine serial.
  • Hydrostatic pump/motor variant. Drive pump and motor part numbers can vary across the production run.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Standard, LGP, and LT (long-track) variants existed.
  • Blade configuration. 6-way, angle-only, and straight-blade configurations have different cylinder specs.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Glass, A/C parts, and door parts differ between configurations.

Why the 700J is a service-friendly pick

The 700J is the modern hydrostatic JD mid-to-large crawler dozer at a service-friendly price point - Tier 3 emissions without DPF or DEF complexity, PowerTech 6068 6-cylinder engine with broad parts availability, hydrostatic drive that’s well-understood by service techs. For customers who want a 100+ HP-class JD crawler without Tier 4 Final aftertreatment service requirements, the 700J is the preferred generation.

Maintenance priorities on a 700J: clean fuel (Tier 3 common-rail injectors don’t tolerate contaminated fuel), clean cool hydraulic oil, on-schedule filter changes, undercarriage replacement as it wears, and engine rebuilds when wear demands.
