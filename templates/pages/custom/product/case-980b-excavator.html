Where the 980B fits in the Case excavator line

The 980B is part of Case's legacy excavator lineage - the predecessor family to the modern CX-series excavator line. In the legacy sequence it sits between the 880D and the 9010, serving as a large production digging machine for contractors, utility crews, and material yards.

The B-series designation places this machine in the pre-Tier 4 emissions era. Mechanical injection appears on the earliest builds, with electronic engine control coming in on later production. Customers running 980B machines today are typically owners who have maintained the platform through multiple work cycles and value its mechanical accessibility.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 980B runs a Case or Cummins diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain. The platform is pre-Tier 4 emissions - mechanical injection on the earliest builds, electronic engine control on later production. There is no DPF, no SCR, and no DEF tank to maintain, which is part of why these machines remain attractive to owners willing to do their own wrench work.

The main hydraulic pump drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel circuits through mechanical or electrohydraulic control valves depending on production date. Steel-track undercarriage carries the rotating upper structure on a heavy-duty swing bearing with dedicated swing motor and reduction gear.

Common service items on the 980B

  • Engine internals: Pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets. Mechanical-injection or early-electronic depending on production year.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary fuel filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom / dipper / bucket / swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the machine serial on the 980B before ordering year-specific parts - production saw running revisions.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number, not just the machine serial.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure boom, dipper, and bucket cylinder bores before ordering.
  • Production-window revision. Engine, fuel system, and hydraulic components saw multiple revisions during the production run.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm pattern before ordering attachment-side hardware.
  • Mechanical vs. electronic injection. Earlier 980B builds use mechanical injection; later builds went electronic. Fuel system parts differ accordingly.

Why the 980B still earns a paycheck

The Case 980B is a legacy excavator from the era before the modern CX-series. Pre-Tier 4 emissions, mechanical or early-electronic injection, and service architecture that rewards a wrench, a manual, and a parts catalog. For owners and operators running these machines, parts availability through the legacy Case service network and aftermarket remains broad.

Maintenance priorities on a 980B: clean fuel and quality filtration, hydraulic oil at temperature with clean filters, swing-bearing lubrication, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin / bushing service at every working joint.
