Case W24 Wheel Loader: Heavy Production-Class Articulated

Where the W24 fits in the Case wheel loader line

The W24 follows the W20 / W20C production-class line and runs alongside the W26B as Case continued refining its heavy wheel loader segment. Larger frame, larger loader cylinders, heavier breakout, larger bucket - paired with the same mechanical-injection architecture that defined the rest of the family. No DPF, no SCR, no emissions tiering - just a Case diesel running an articulated production loader.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The W24 runs a larger Case inline diesel through a power-shift transmission and heavy articulated front and rear drive axles. Loader hydraulics use a high-flow gear pump with mechanical control valves and direct-acting cylinders sized for heavy bucket work. Wet-disc service brakes, planetary final drives, and a heavier ROPS-equipped operator station match the W24’s intended duty cycle - quarry pads, scrap operations, snow handling, and any operation where bucket capacity outranks cycle speed.

Common service items on the W24

  • Engine internals: Case diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets, full overhaul kits. The R49482 engine motor mount moves consistently across the Case diesel family.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Loader hydraulics: Cylinder seal kits, hydraulic pump rebuilds, control valves, hoses.
  • Articulation steering: Steering cylinder seal kits, articulation pivot pins and bushings.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, bushings, pivot pins.
  • Drivetrain: Power-shift clutch packs, transmission components, axle seals, planetary final drives.
  • Brakes: Wet-disc brake service. Recurring service parts on Case wheel loader brakes include the L113007 master cylinder with air chamber, the A12985 slave cylinder, and the L15782 brake pedal valve.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.
  • Operator station: Seat, gauges, control levers, ROPS canopy parts, cab fresh-air filter (47880728) on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the right frame near the rear articulation before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Case diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Brake system revision. Wet-disc service brakes saw multiple revisions - confirm master/slave cylinder fit by serial.
  • Power-shift revision. Power-shift transmissions saw clutch-pack revisions during production.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations.

Why the W24 still earns a paycheck

The W24 is a Case heavy production-class wheel loader from the era when mechanical injection and mechanical hydraulics defined the segment. No electronics to fail, no aftertreatment to maintain - when the W24 hits a problem, it responds to a wrench, a parts catalog, and a service manual. For operations that want production capacity without the maintenance footprint of a Tier 4 machine, the W24 is a fleet-keeper.

Maintenance priorities on a W24: clean fuel and quality filtration, hydraulic seal replacement before cylinders weep, articulation pivot service when slop develops, brake service when actuation softens, and engine overhaul when wear demands.
