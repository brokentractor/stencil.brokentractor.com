The Case 1455B Crawler Loader is the heavy-class crawler-loader sibling to the Case 1450B dozer. The 1455B shares chassis, engine, transmission, and undercarriage with the 1450B - but where the 1450B mounts a dozer blade and pushframe, the 1455B mounts a heavy-duty hydraulic loader bucket on lifting arms. Crawler loaders are the heavy-class material-handling machine for sites where wheel loader traction is inadequate (loose ground, rough terrain, snow, soft soil, mine sites). The 1455B combines crawler tractor stability and traction with loader-quick heavy-class material handling.

The 1455B uses the proven Case A504BDT turbocharged inline six-cylinder diesel - 504 cubic inches of displacement (approximately 8.3 liters), with turbocharging for the heavy-class output needed for loader work. Power shift transmission with torque converter, like the 1450B.

1455B = 1450B chassis + heavy-class hydraulic loader The 1455B and 1450B share chassis, drivetrain, engine, undercarriage, operator station, and cab. The difference is on the front: dozer blade and pushframe (1450B) vs. hydraulic loader bucket and loader arms (1455B). When ordering parts, dozer-side hardware (blade cylinders, blade carrier, cutting edges) is 1450B-specific; loader-side hardware (bucket cylinders, bucket linkage, bucket teeth) is 1455B-specific. Chassis, engine, transmission, and undercarriage parts are common between the two machines.

Crawler loader vs. crawler dozer

The crawler loader configuration uses a front-mounted hydraulic bucket on lifting arms, similar in concept to a wheel loader but mounted on a crawler tractor chassis. The advantages over a wheel loader in heavy applications:

  • Traction and stability - crawler tracks provide superior traction on loose, muddy, or slippery surfaces compared to rubber tires.
  • Soft-ground capability - wider track footprint reduces ground pressure, allowing operation on soft soil or wetlands where a wheel loader would sink.
  • Heavy-class load handling - the 1455B's heavy chassis and turbocharged engine handle large bucket loads of dense material (rock, ore, frozen soil).
  • Multi-task versatility - load, push, grade, and (with the right bucket options) clamp and dump.

The trade-offs vs. a wheel loader are operating speed (crawlers are slower over distance) and transport (crawlers require a lowboy trailer; wheel loaders can typically drive between job sites).

Multi-purpose (4-in-1) bucket

The 1455B was commonly ordered with a multi-purpose (4-in-1) bucket. The 4-in-1 bucket is a hinged bucket - the floor can be opened hydraulically, turning a standard loader bucket into a clam-shell, grading blade, or dozer-style pushing tool. 4-in-1 bucket cylinders, hinges, and seal kits are routine service items on machines that use the bucket regularly. Confirm bucket type when ordering parts - standard bucket vs. 4-in-1 bucket service kits are different.

Case A504BDT inline 6-cylinder turbocharged engine

The 1455B uses the Case A504BDT inline six-cylinder turbocharged diesel - same engine as the 1450B dozer. 504 cubic inches of displacement (approximately 8.3 liters), with turbocharging for heavy-class output. Approximately 165-175 horsepower flywheel.

The A504 engine ecosystem is broad - accessory parts (water pump, alternator, starter, oil pump) share with other heavy Case industrial 6-cyl applications of the era. Injection pump and injector service is well-documented. Turbocharger service is a routine wear item. Browse the Case Dozer Parts hub for current 1455B engine service kits.

Power shift transmission with torque converter

The 1455B uses the same power shift with torque converter transmission as the 1450B. Clutchless gear changes under load, multiple forward and reverse speeds, torque converter providing smooth power transfer. Routine service items: shift solenoids and pressure switches, clutch packs, torque converter, transmission oil cooler, transmission filter.

Loader hydraulic system

The 1455B loader uses heavy-duty hydraulic cylinders for boom lift, bucket curl/dump, and (on 4-in-1 buckets) bucket floor open/close. Standard service items at production-run age:

  • Boom cylinder seal kits - routine seal-replacement candidate.
  • Bucket cylinder seal kits - routine seal-replacement candidate.
  • 4-in-1 bucket clam cylinder seal kits - routine on machines that use the 4-in-1 function frequently.
  • Cylinder rod chrome service - corrosion on rods causes premature seal wear.
  • Loader valve seal kits - control valve seals can develop weep at production-run age.

Variants: Standard, LGP

The 1455B shipped in two primary track configurations:

  • Standard - standard-width track, balanced general loader work.
  • LGP (Low Ground Pressure) - wider grouser pads and extended track frame for soft-ground / wetlands / agricultural-soil work where flotation is the priority.

Track configuration affects undercarriage and frame parts. Confirm Standard vs. LGP by serial before ordering.

Undercarriage

The 1455B undercarriage is the same as the 1450B undercarriage - heavy-class components shared across the 1450 family. Sprocket bolt patterns, idler geometries, master pin dimensions, roller designs all cross-fit with the 1450B. Confirm by part number against your serial. Track chain, roller, idler, and sprocket are all routine wear items at production-run age.

Browse the Case Dozer Parts hub for current 1455B / 1450B-era undercarriage availability.

Common 1455B service items

Engine accessory aging

Water pump, alternator, starter, injection pump, and turbocharger on the A504BDT are routinely rebuilt or replaced over the engine's service life. Cooling system service is a routine maintenance milestone on 30-plus-year-old machines.

Turbocharger wear

The A504BDT turbocharger is a routine wear item. Symptoms: blue smoke at startup or under load, oil consumption, audible whine outside the normal range, visible shaft play.

Power shift / torque converter service

Shift solenoid failure, clutch pack wear, torque converter degradation. The transmission filter and fluid should be on a scheduled service interval.

Loader hydraulic seal failure

Boom, bucket, and 4-in-1 clam cylinder seals are routine seal-replacement candidates at production-run age. Cylinder rod chrome corrosion is also typical.

Bucket teeth and edge wear

Bucket teeth and edge wear are the most frequent service items on a working 1455B. Replacement teeth and edges are widely available aftermarket.

4-in-1 bucket hinge and seal service

The 4-in-1 bucket hinge mechanism and clam cylinder are routine service items on machines that use the 4-in-1 function regularly. Hinge pin and bushing wear can develop play in the bucket floor.

Undercarriage replacement cycles

Same heavy-class undercarriage as the 1450B. Track chains, rollers, idlers, and sprockets all have measurable wear lives.

Operator station and ROPS

Cab and canopy hardware, seat cushion replacement, and floorboard wear are typical aging-machine issues. ROPS structural integrity should be verified before resale.

Wiring and electrical

30-plus-year-old wiring harnesses are routinely brittle.
