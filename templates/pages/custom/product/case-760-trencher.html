Where the 760 fits in the Case trencher line

The Case 760 sits above the 660 and below the turbocharged 860 in the Case ride-on trencher lineup. Heavy-duty chassis, larger digger configuration, and longer chain options support deeper utility-installation work for utility, pipeline, and infrastructure contractors.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The Case 760 runs a CNH industrial diesel through a heavy-duty hydrostatic propel system. Digger boom, chain drive motor, and chain pull are sized for extended-cycle utility and pipeline work. Heavier chassis and larger fuel and hydraulic capacity than the 660 support longer working days between fueling and service stops.

The hydrostatic propel circuit gives the operator infinitely variable ground speed for trenching control, while the digger boom is driven by a dedicated heavy-duty hydraulic motor sized for sustained chain pull through hard material. Hydraulic chain tensioning keeps the digger chain at the correct working tension without manual adjustment.

Common service items on the 760

  • Engine internals: Diesel engine pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets. Most Case trenchers use Case or CNH industrial diesel engines through the production run. The J901774 flywheel ring gear cross-fits the Case backhoe, trencher, skid steer, forklift, and dozer family.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary fuel filters. D139225 in-line fuel filter cross-fits broadly across Case backhoes, dozers, skid steers, loaders, excavators, and trenchers.
  • Air intake: A173290 air filter cross-fits broadly across Case backhoe, dozer, forklift, loader, skid steer, and trencher applications.
  • Exhaust: A173180 muffler cross-fits Case backhoe, forklift, and trencher applications.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Trencher digging chain: Digger chain, teeth (rock, dirt, frost, carbide), chain side links, digger boom bearings, chain tensioner.
  • Digger hydraulics: Digger boom drive motor, chain drive sprocket, hydraulic chain tensioning cylinder, control valves, high-pressure hoses. The 1543252C1 hydraulic cylinder seal kit cross-fits across Case dozers, loaders, backhoes, and trenchers.
  • Hydraulic filtration: A177615 hydraulic spin-on filter cross-fits Case telehandler and trencher hydraulic systems.
  • Drivetrain: Hydrostatic propel pump and motors, ground-drive transmission (where mechanical), axles, planetary final drives. K260218 front axle stud is a Case backhoe, forklift, and trencher cross-fit hardware item.
  • Brakes: Service and park brakes. A168790 parking brake disc cross-fits Case forklifts, loader-backhoes, and trenchers - a high-volume Case service item.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches, key switch.
  • Operator station: Seat, controls, joystick and lever linkage, ROPS canopy, gauges.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the Case 760 before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Digger chain configuration. Chain pitch, link count, and tooth type (rock, dirt, frost, carbide) vary by configuration - confirm before ordering.
  • Hydrostatic pump generation. Pump and motor revisions occurred during the production run - confirm by serial.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations.

Why the 760 still earns a paycheck

The Case 760 is the production-class Case trencher for utility, pipeline, and infrastructure contractors who need more chassis and more hydraulic capacity than the 660 delivers. Extended trenching cycles, larger digger configurations, and the kind of chassis durability that supports rental-fleet duty.

Maintenance priorities on a 760: digger chain and tooth condition, hydrostatic system condition with clean filtration, brake service (A168790 cross-fit), and engine service per schedule. Broken Tractor maintains parts coverage across the Case trencher family.
