Where the CX245D SR fits in the Case excavator line

The CX245D SR is a 24.5-ton class D-Series short-radius excavator. It sits between the CX240D and the CX250C in Case’s full-size excavator lineup. SR (short-radius) geometry keeps the counterweight inside or very close to the track width, which makes the CX245D SR ideal for confined-space utility work, curbside jobs, and urban builds where rear swing clearance is a constraint.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX245D SR runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump - commonly a load-sensing piston pump - drives the boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel circuits through electrohydraulic control valves. Short-radius tail-swing geometry reduces the rear counterweight overhang for confined-space work, at the cost of slightly lower swing-stability when lifting at full reach.

Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - DPF, SCR catalyst with DEF dosing, and EGR cooler - is fully integrated into the engine package. These systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require active management: DPF regen behavior monitoring, DEF top-ups, and replacement of NOx and pressure sensors as they fail.

Tier 4 Final - service implications The CX245D SR’s DPF and SCR systems require clean ULSD fuel and quality DEF. Common-rail injectors are intolerant of fuel contamination. Track DPF regen behavior - blocked DPFs trigger engine derate. Budget aftertreatment service alongside conventional engine and hydraulic maintenance.

Common service items on the CX245D SR

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, common-rail injection components.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service and replacement, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, NOx sensors, EGR cooler.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Main pump rebuild, boom / dipper / bucket / swing cylinder seal kits, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb hardware, quick-coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harnesses, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wipers: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.

What to verify before ordering

  • Machine serial number. Confirm the serial on the CX245D SR before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX245D SR still earns a paycheck

The Case CX245D SR is a 24.5-ton class production excavator handling general site work, utility installation, residential and commercial earthmoving, and mining or quarry work depending on configuration. The SR tail-swing geometry adds urban, curbside, and confined-space capability that conventional-radius 24-ton excavators can’t offer.

Maintenance priorities on a CX245D SR are clean fuel with tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic oil condition, swing-bearing lubrication, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service at every boom and dipper joint.
