Where the 310SE fits in the JD backhoe line

JD's industrial backhoe lineup of the late 1990s offered two configurations in the mid-class slot: the standard 310E and the heavier-duty 310SE (Super E). Same chassis platform, same engine family, same drivetrain architecture - but the SE configuration ran upgraded structural components, more aggressive hydraulic settings, and equipment packaging aimed at customers running production-pace work day after day.

The 310SE shipped from 1997 through 2000 alongside the 310E. Customers who needed maximum digging force, faster cycle times, and the kind of structural reinforcement that holds up to commercial contractor use went with the Super E. Utility crews, light-duty municipal work, and landscape contractors typically stayed with the standard 310E.

What the SE configuration adds vs. the 310E

  • Upgraded structural components. Heavier-duty loader arms, reinforced backhoe boom and stick, beefier swing tower components.
  • More aggressive hydraulics. Higher operating pressure and flow settings for faster cycle times and more breakout force.
  • Heavier-duty stabilizers. Larger stabilizer leg cylinders and reinforced footpads.
  • Production-grade hardware. Pin and bushing sizes upgraded at high-wear pivot points throughout the backhoe assembly.
  • Shared with the 310E: Engine, drivetrain, cab, operator controls, and the majority of service items.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 310SE shares its turbocharged JD diesel and refined power shuttle drivetrain with the standard 310E. Where the SE differs is in the hydraulic settings and structural components - the engine and drivetrain themselves are the same. That commonality means many engine, fuel system, and drivetrain parts cross-fit between the 310E and 310SE, while loader, backhoe, and structural parts are SE-specific.

Common service items on the 310SE

  • Heavy-duty backhoe components: Swing tower pin kits (SE-specific sizing), stabilizer leg cylinders, boom/stick/bucket pin kits.
  • Hydraulics: Pump rebuilds, loader cylinder seal kits, backhoe cylinder seal kits, control valves, hoses. Higher pressure than the 310E so seal kit specs may differ.
  • Turbocharger: Turbo rebuilds, oil supply and drain lines, charge-air piping.
  • Engine internals: JD turbocharged diesel rebuild kits - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump rebuilds, injectors, lift pump, fuel filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, hoses, fan belt.
  • Power shuttle: Shuttle clutch packs, control valves, hydraulic actuation components.
  • Drivetrain: Transmission components, axle seals, final drive gears.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, harnesses, switches, gauges.
  • Cab: Cab glass, weather seals, door parts, seat, A/C parts.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Use the table above to confirm production year before ordering year-specific parts.
  • SE vs. standard 310E. Backhoe structural and hydraulic parts differ between the Super E and the standard 310E. Confirm SE configuration when ordering loader/backhoe components.
  • Engine serial. JD turbocharged diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Turbocharger variant. Turbo part numbers can vary across the production run.
  • Loader configuration. Standard, quick-attach, and specialty bucket configurations had different cylinder and mounting specs.
  • Backhoe configuration. Standard reach vs. extendable boom configurations differ in cylinder bore and stroke.
  • Drivetrain configuration. 2WD and 4WD/MFWD configurations have different front axle and transfer case parts.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Glass, weather seals, A/C parts, and door parts differ.

Why the 310SE remains a contractor pick

The 310SE is the heavy-duty 310 of the late 1990s - built for the customers who needed maximum digging force and structural durability without electronic-engine-control complexity. Pre-Tier 2 simplicity, turbocharged power, refined power shuttle drivetrain, and SE-specific structural reinforcement that holds up to production work.

Maintenance priorities on a 310SE: turbo oil supply integrity, clean fuel, power shuttle fluid changes on schedule, hydraulic seal replacement as cylinders age, heavy-duty swing tower pin service, and engine rebuilds when wear demands.
