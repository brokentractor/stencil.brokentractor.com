Where the 60XT fits in the Case skid steer line

The 60XT sits in the XT-series Case skid steer range between the Case 40XT and the Case 70XT. It pairs a Cummins industrial diesel with a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain, and the XT-series cab provides improved ergonomics over the 18-series predecessors. The XT generation served as the bridge between the simpler vintage 1800-series Case skid steers and the later alpha-series machines that introduced more electronic controls.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 60XT runs a Cummins 4B 3.9L four-cylinder industrial diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain - closed-loop pumps drive sprocket-shaft motors with chain final drive to the wheels. Loader hydraulics use a piston pump feeding either electrohydraulic-pilot or mechanical control valves, with direct-acting lift and tilt cylinders to the loader arms.

Early-production 60XT units carried mechanical-injection Cummins 4B engines; later units shifted to electronically-managed variants. Confirm by engine serial when ordering internal parts. The 4B family is one of the most-supported industrial engines on the market, which keeps parts coverage strong on this machine.

Common service items on the 60XT

  • Engine internals: Cummins 4B / 4BT pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. Mechanical-injection (early) vs. electronic (late) variants - confirm by serial.
  • Fuel system: Cummins fuel injection components, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler (turbo variants), hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulics: Loader lift and bucket tilt cylinder seal kits, piston-pump rebuilds, control valves, hoses. DMEL1075 cylinder spanner wrench for cylinder service.
  • Drivetrain: Hydrostatic dual-path pumps and drive motors, drive chains, sprockets, axles. D64175 sprocket shaft needle bearing cross-fits broadly across the Case skid steer family.
  • Hardware: 191048A2 axle stud & nut cross-fits Case skid steer axle studs.
  • Brakes / control: Park brake components, control linkage, 48139268 control shock absorber for Case/NH skid steer control valves.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches, 47405956 windshield wiper blade on cab-equipped XT units.
  • Cab / operator station: Seat, cab glass, ROPS canopy, gauges, joystick controls.
Drive chain tension matters Hydrostatic dual-path skid steers use chain final drive from the drive motor to the wheel sprockets. Chain tension drifts over time and a loose chain accelerates sprocket and chain wear, eventually causing skipping under load. Check tension at scheduled intervals and replace chains in matched sets to keep tracking even.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the machine serial on the 60XT before ordering year-specific parts - the XT-series had running changes through production.
  • Engine serial. Cummins 4B internal parts must match the engine serial number, and mechanical-injection vs. electronic variants take different fuel system components.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Lift and tilt cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between cab and open-canopy configurations - affects glass, wipers, HVAC, and electrical.
  • Hand vs. foot controls. XT-series machines shipped in different control configurations affecting joystick assemblies and linkages.

Why the 60XT still earns a paycheck

The Case 60XT is the XT-series Case skid steer that bridges the vintage 18-series simplicity and the alpha-series modern electronics. Cummins diesel reliability, hydrostatic dual-path service-friendliness, and modern-enough cab ergonomics keep it as a working machine on contractor yards and rental fleets. Parts coverage is strong because the 4B engine family is one of the most-supported industrial engines on the market, and the Case skid steer family shares major service components across the XT range.

Maintenance priorities on a 60XT: clean fuel and quality filtration on the Cummins, hydraulic oil kept clean and at temperature, drive-chain tension, cylinder seal replacement on schedule, and brake/control linkage service.
