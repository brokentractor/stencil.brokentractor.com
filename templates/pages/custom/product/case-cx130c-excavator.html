Where the CX130C fits in the Case excavator line

The CX130C is a 13-ton class C-Series excavator. It sits between the CX130B and the CX130D in Case's full-size excavator lineup, representing the Tier 4 Interim generation of the 13-ton platform. The C-Series brought updated cab ergonomics, refined electrohydraulic control valves, and emissions-compliant powertrain components while retaining the proven hydrostatic dual-path drive and conventional tail-swing configuration of the platform.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX130C runs a Tier 4 Interim diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump - commonly a load-sensing piston pump - drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel circuits through electrohydraulic control valves. The Tier 4 Interim engine adds EGR cooler service, a diesel particulate filter (DPF), and on some configurations SCR aftertreatment with DEF dosing.

Common-rail injection demands clean fuel and tight water separation - the most frequent cause of premature injector failure on the C-Series is fuel contamination. The swing system runs a dedicated swing motor and reduction gear over a large-diameter swing bearing, with a swing brake holding upper-frame position when idle.

Common service items on the CX130C

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Interim diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. Common-rail injection.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service intervals and regeneration behavior, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket and swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick-coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
Tier 4 Interim aftertreatment - service implications The CX130C's DPF and (where equipped) SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they demand attention. DEF tank must stay topped up, DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations, and NOx sensors fail occasionally and trigger derate conditions if not replaced. Factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget on production machines.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the CX130C before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX130C still earns a paycheck

The Case CX130C is a 13-ton class production excavator - general site work, utility installation, residential and commercial earthmoving, and mining or quarry work depending on configuration. Operators who came up on the CX130B find the C-Series cab and controls a natural progression, and the platform's parts ecosystem benefits from heavy commonality with the contemporary Link-Belt excavator family.

Maintenance priorities on a CX130C: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic oil condition, swing-bearing lubrication, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service at every boom and dipper joint.
