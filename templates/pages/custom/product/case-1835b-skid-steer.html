Where the 1835B fits in the Case skid steer line

The 1835B sits in the vintage Case 18-series skid steer lineup - between the Case 1835 and the Case 1835C. Mechanical injection, gear hydraulics, hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain. Pre-electronic architecture means service is wrench-and-manual rather than diagnostic-and-laptop, which is a large part of why owners keep these machines working decades after production ended.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 1835B runs through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain - closed-loop hydrostatic pumps drive sprocket-shaft motors that turn the drive chains and wheels. Loader hydraulics use a gear pump with mechanical control valves and direct-acting lift and tilt cylinders. Pre-electronic: no ECU, no aftertreatment, mechanically injected diesel or gasoline engines depending on configuration.

The architecture is component-level serviceable. Pumps and motors come apart on the bench, control valves are mechanical, and cylinder seal kits can be installed with hand tools. Drive chains and sprockets are standard Case skid steer pattern.

Common service items on the 1835B

  • Engine internals: Pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. Pre-electronic mechanical-injection diesel or gasoline depending on configuration.
  • Fuel system: Mechanical injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulics: Loader lift and bucket tilt cylinder seal kits, gear pump rebuilds, control valves, hoses. The DMEL1075 cylinder spanner wrench is the right tool for servicing skid steer cylinders without rounding the gland nut.
  • Drivetrain: Hydrostatic dual-path pumps and motors, sprocket-shaft bearings (cross-fit D64175 sprocket shaft needle bearing), drive chains, final-drive sprockets, axle bearings.
  • Hardware: Skid steer axle studs and nuts (191048A2 stud & nut cross-fits broadly across Case skid steers).
  • Brakes / control: Park brake, control linkage. The 48139268 control shock absorber cross-fits Case and New Holland skid steer control valves.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches, key switch.
  • Cab / operator station: Seat, ROPS canopy, gauges, controls.
Hydraulic service tip Clean hydraulic oil at proper operating temperature is the single most important variable on a 1835B’s longevity. The hydrostatic dual-path drive is unforgiving of contamination - debris in the closed loop wears pump and motor swashplates fast. Filter on schedule, and any time a hydraulic component comes apart, flush the system before reassembly.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the Case 1835B before ordering year-specific parts. Running changes through 1982–1987 affect some components.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number, and gasoline vs. diesel configurations are completely different parts lists.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations.
  • Drive chain size. Verify chain pitch and link count from the existing chain before ordering replacement.

Why the 1835B still earns a paycheck

The Case 1835B is the vintage Case skid steer that owners keep working because parts are still available and the architecture rewards a wrench-and-manual approach. No electronics to fail, no aftertreatment to maintain, no DEF tank to keep fresh - service is mechanical, immediate, and component-level. For a small operation that needs a compact loader and doesn’t want a payment book, the 1835B remains a practical choice.

Maintenance priorities on a 1835B: clean hydraulic oil at temperature, drive-chain tension, axle bearing service, cylinder seal replacement when leaks develop, and engine service per the original Case schedule.
