Case W30 Wheel Loader: Heavy Production-Class with LEFT-Frame Serial

The W30 is one of the largest of Case's vintage heavy production-class wheel loaders - a big-bucket articulated machine with a Case diesel, power-shift drivetrain, and the LEFT-frame serial layout (plus cab ID plate) that distinguishes it from the right-frame layout used on earlier W-series machines.

Where the W30 fits in the Case wheel loader line

The W30 sits above the W26B in the heavy production-class segment and below the largest W36. Big frame, big loader cylinders, large bucket. The W30 carries Case's mechanical-injection diesel and power-shift transmission paired with revised hydraulics, and like the W20C and later C-revision machines it places the serial on the LEFT frame rather than the right.

Locating your serial number on the W30

Serial number - LEFT-frame layout On the Case W30 the serial number is stamped on the LEFT side of the loader frame near the articulation point. There is also a metal ID plate inside the cab. This is a deliberate departure from the right-frame layout of the early W-series - always check the left frame first on a W30, and verify the serial before ordering year-specific parts.

The W30 does not carry a right-frame plate. If you can only find numbers on the right side of the chassis, you are likely looking at a different W-series variant. Always cross-check the LEFT-frame stamp against the metal ID plate inside the cab before placing an order for year-specific parts.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The W30 runs a large Case inline diesel through a power-shift transmission and heavy articulated front and rear drive axles. Loader hydraulics use a high-flow gear pump with mechanical control valves and direct-acting cylinders sized for big-bucket production loadout. Articulation steering uses heavy hydraulic cylinders between the chassis halves.

Wet-disc service brakes, planetary final drives, and a heavier ROPS-equipped operator station carry the W30 through quarry pads, scrap operations, and full-shift production loadout. The mechanical-injection diesel and electronics-free architecture are part of what keeps these machines working - parts are still available and an experienced tech can keep one in service with a wrench and a manual.

Common service items on the W30

  • Engine internals: Case diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets, full overhaul kits. The R49482 engine motor mount is a high-volume Case diesel service item.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Loader hydraulics: Cylinder seal kits, hydraulic pump rebuilds, control valves, hoses.
  • Articulation steering: Steering cylinder seal kits, articulation pivot pins and bushings.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, bushings, pivot pins.
  • Drivetrain: Power-shift clutch packs, transmission components, axle seals, planetary final drives. Transmission shift lever assemblies move regularly across the Case wheel loader family.
  • Brakes: Wet-disc brake service. Recurring service parts include the L113007 master cylinder with air chamber, the S300718 brake shoe, and the L15782 brake pedal valve.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.
  • Operator station: Seat, gauges, control levers, ROPS canopy parts, cab fresh-air filter (47880728) on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number - LEFT frame. Check the LEFT frame near the articulation point, plus the metal ID plate inside the cab. The W30 does NOT carry a right-frame plate.
  • Engine serial. Case diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Power-shift revision. Power-shift transmissions saw clutch-pack revisions during production.
  • Brake system revision. Wet-disc service brakes saw multiple revisions - confirm master/slave cylinder fit by serial.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations.

Why the W30 still earns a paycheck

The W30 is a large Case production-class wheel loader from the era when mechanical injection, no electronics, and no aftertreatment defined the segment. Bucket capacity, breakout, and serviceability - the W30 is the kind of machine that owners keep running because parts are still available and the architecture rewards a wrench-and-manual approach. On quarry, scrap, snow, and large-yard work, the W30 still moves material.

Maintenance priorities on a W30: clean fuel and quality filtration, hydraulic seal replacement before cylinders weep, articulation pivot service when slop develops, brake service when actuation softens, and engine overhaul when wear demands.
