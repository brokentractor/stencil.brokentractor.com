Where the 345 P-Tier fits in the JD excavator line

The 345 P-Tier sits between the 300 P-Tier and 350 P-Tier in the modern John Deere P-Tier construction excavator lineup. It is a 34.5-ton class machine built on the JD/Hitachi shared platform, which keeps service-part availability strong because many components carry across multiple JD and Hitachi excavator models in the same weight class.

P-Tier is JD’s current production designation for the modern, Tier 4 Final emissions generation of construction equipment. On the 345 P-Tier that means a refined diesel engine, full aftertreatment (DPF + SCR with DEF), a modernized cab, and current-generation electronic hydraulic controls.

Locating your serial number On the John Deere 345 P-Tier the serial number plate is mounted on the upper frame near the cab entry, typically on the right-hand side beneath the door threshold or beside the operator’s seat. Always verify the serial number before ordering parts to ensure proper fitment - the P-Tier generation has seen running changes through production.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 345 P-Tier runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic drive into a steel-track undercarriage. The aftertreatment package includes a diesel particulate filter (DPF) for particulate control and an SCR catalyst with DEF dosing for NOx control - standard for the Tier 4 Final emissions tier.

The implement hydraulic circuit drives the boom, arm, bucket, and swing functions through current-generation electronic hydraulic control. The AT317252 cross-pin is the broad JD excavator family service pin that fits across the JD P-Tier and G-Tier construction line (180CW, 190DW, 200, 200C, 200G, 210G, 225C, 230GW, 2054, 180G, 190GW, 210P, 220DW, 225D, 245G, 2154D and more).

Common service items on the 345 P-Tier

  • Engine: Tier 4 Final diesel internals - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service, DEF dosing units, NOx sensors, SCR catalyst.
  • Cooling: Radiator, water pump, hydraulic oil cooler, heat exchanger, hoses.
  • Hydraulics: Main pump, control valve, swing motor, travel motors, boom/arm/bucket cylinders.
  • Boom pins: AT317252 cross-pin (broad JD family fit).
  • Undercarriage: Steel tracks, shoes, idlers, rollers, sprockets, idler springs.
  • Cab and electrical: Cab glass, alternator, starter, sensors, harnesses, ECU, monitor display.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The 345 P-Tier’s DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up. DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations. NOx sensors fail occasionally and trigger derate conditions if not replaced. Build aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget on any production P-Tier machine.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the upper frame before ordering year-specific parts - the P-Tier generation has seen running changes.
  • Engine and aftertreatment serial. Tier 4 Final emissions parts are serial-specific. Match against the engine and aftertreatment serial tags.
  • Aftertreatment generation. DPF and SCR revisions have running changes through the production run.
  • Boom-pin position. AT317252 fits a broad JD family but the boom, arm, and bucket positions each have specific cross-pin part numbers - confirm position before ordering.
  • Hydraulic pump generation. Main pump revisions have running changes - match against the pump tag.

Why the 345 P-Tier still earns a paycheck

The 345 P-Tier stays in service because modern 34.5-ton JD excavators deliver Tier 4 Final fuel efficiency and operator-station refinements that contractors rely on for production work. JD/Hitachi platform parts sharing means service parts are widely available across the dealer network and aftermarket.

Maintenance priorities on a 345 P-Tier: clean fuel and quality DEF (Tier 4 Final common-rail injectors and SCR catalysts don’t tolerate contamination), aftertreatment service discipline, hydraulic oil cleanliness, boom-pin service (AT317252 cross-fit covers most positions), undercarriage replacement on schedule, and cab and electrical service.
