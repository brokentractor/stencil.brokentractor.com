Case CX17C Mini Excavator: 2017+ 1.7-Ton C-Series Compact

The Case CX17C is the 1.7-ton class C-series mini excavator from Case Construction Equipment - a long-production-run compact built for residential landscape, light utility, plumbing, irrigation, and any tight-quarters digging job. Where the CX17C fits in the Case excavator line

The CX17C sits in the heart of Case’s 1.5-to-2-ton mini excavator range - between the smaller CX12D and CX19D and the larger CX26C and CX30C. Long production run from 2017 to present, retractable undercarriage option, folding TOPS canopy or fixed cab, and the C-series cab layout that defines Case’s modern compact mini design. The CX17C is a high-volume rental-fleet machine - straightforward service, broad parts availability, and a duty cycle that’s well understood across the contractor community.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX17C runs a small Tier 4 Final compact diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a rubber-track undercarriage (retractable on equipped units). The main hydraulic pump drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel through electrohydraulic-pilot or mechanical control valves. The zero / minimum-swing configuration tucks the counterweight close to the chassis envelope, which is what makes the CX17C usable inside a fenced backyard or alongside a finished wall.

Common-rail fuel injection runs across the production window, and a compact DPF aftertreatment package handles particulate control. Hydraulic auxiliary plumbing is standard for thumb and breaker attachments, with quick-coupler interfaces varying across build years.

Common service items on the CX17C

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final compact diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. Common-rail injection across the production window.
  • Tier 4 Final aftertreatment: DPF service intervals and regen behavior; DEF dosing where SCR is fitted. EGR cooler service when soot loading appears in coolant or oil.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Compact radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Main pump rebuild, swing motor, propel motors, boom / dipper / bucket cylinder seal kits, control valves, auxiliary hydraulic hoses for attachments.
  • Undercarriage: Rubber track replacement (retractable variants have additional retraction-cylinder service), rollers, idlers, drive sprockets.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, side-shift boom pivot service on offset-boom configurations, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware.
  • Brakes / propel: Park-brake service, propel-motor seals, planetary final-drive components.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab and canopy: Folding TOPS canopy hardware (canopy units), operator seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, glass on cab-equipped builds.
  • Cab glass / wiper: Cab glass and wiper service - KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade cross-fits across the Case and Link-Belt excavator range.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the right side of the lower frame before ordering - production-window-specific parts vary.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Cab vs. canopy. Folding-TOPS canopy and fixed-cab builds differ in operator-station hardware.
  • Retractable vs. fixed undercarriage. Retract-equipped CX17Cs have additional retraction-cylinder hardware not present on fixed-track builds.
  • Quick coupler. Hydraulic quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX17C still earns a paycheck

The CX17C is the everyday Case mini excavator - the size and configuration that fits the most common compact-excavator duty cycles. Residential landscape, light utility, irrigation, swimming pool installation, plumbing service laterals, and any job where a 1.5-to-2 ton class machine is the right tool. Long production run means parts availability is broad and the service community knows the machine well.

Maintenance priorities on a CX17C: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic oil at temperature with clean filtration, rubber-track tension and wear monitoring, and pin / bushing service at every working joint.
