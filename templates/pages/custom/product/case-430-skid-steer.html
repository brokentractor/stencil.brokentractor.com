Case 430 Skid Steer Loader: 400-Series Tier 3 Hydrostatic Skid Steer

The Case 430 is part of Case’s 400-series skid steer / compact track loader family — Tier 3 compact diesel, hydrostatic dual-path drive, and the operator interface that bridged the XT-series and the modern alpha (SR/SV/TR/TV) lineup. Built from 2004 into 2010, the 430 is the mid-2000s Case skid steer that brought modern electronic engine controls and updated cab ergonomics to the platform.

Where the 430 fits in the Case skid steer / CTL line

The 430 sits in the Case 400-series skid steer / CTL range between the Case 420CT and the Case 435. Tier 3 compact diesel architecture, electronic engine controls, and the cab and instrumentation that defined Case’s mid-2000s skid steer / CTL line before the alpha rename to the SR/SV/TR/TV nomenclature.

Confirm the serial before ordering — the 400-series saw multiple revisions and the Tier 3 aftertreatment configuration changes by year.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 430 runs a Tier 3 compact diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain. Loader hydraulics use a piston pump with electrohydraulic control valves. The wet-disc park brake is integrated with the hydrostatic deceleration logic, and the auxiliary hydraulic circuit supports both standard-flow and high-flow attachment configurations depending on build.

Cab and ROPS-canopy configurations were offered. Cab units add HVAC, sealed glass, and joystick electrohydraulic controls; ROPS-canopy units run mechanical hand-and-foot controls on most builds. Confirm operator-station configuration before ordering control or HVAC parts.

Common service items on the 430

  • Engine internals: Tier 3 compact diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. Common-rail high-pressure injection on later builds.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, very tight water-separator filtration.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulics: Loader lift and bucket tilt cylinder seal kits (86570919 / 86570931 / 86570922 cross-fit on NH/Case alpha-series), piston-pump rebuilds, electrohydraulic control valves.
  • Drivetrain: Hydrostatic dual-path pumps and drive motors, charge pump, filters.
  • Hardware: 191048A2 axle stud & nut cross-fits broadly across the Case skid steer line.
  • Brakes / control: Park brake, control linkage, 48139268 control shock absorber on Case/NH alpha-series control valves.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, machine controller, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness, telematics module.
  • Cab / operator station: Cab HVAC, seat, gauges, joystick controls, cab glass, 47405956 windshield wiper blade on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. The 400-series saw multiple running revisions. Match parts to the machine serial.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Aftertreatment / emissions configuration. Tier 3 configuration varies by build year — confirm EGR and aftertreatment layout before ordering.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension — measure before ordering.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations — HVAC, glass, and joystick electrohydraulic controls are cab-only on most builds.
  • Auxiliary hydraulic flow. Standard-flow vs. high-flow configurations affect pump, valve, and coupler part numbers.

Why the 430 still earns a paycheck

The Case 430 is the mid-2000s Case skid steer loader that bridged the XT-series and the current alpha lineup. Tier 3 emissions, modern electronics, and the cab ergonomics that put the operator in a better seat than the XT predecessor. The 400-series platform shares a lot of architecture with the New Holland L-series skid steers of the same era under the CNH umbrella, so cross-fit hydraulic, control, and cab parts are broadly available.

Maintenance priorities on a 430: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, hydrostatic charge pressure monitoring, drive-chain tension, and cylinder seal service before bore damage sets in.
