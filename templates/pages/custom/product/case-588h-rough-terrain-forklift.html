Where the 588H fits in the Case forklift line

The 588H is the top of Case’s current H-series rough terrain forklift line — successor to the Tier 3 588G, and the largest current Case rough terrain forklift. 8,000 lb capacity with Tier 4 Final emissions, refined mast control, and optional 4WD for off-road and soft-ground work define the model.

Below the 588H sit the smaller 586H and the entry H-class. The platform shares family DNA with the G-series rough terrain forklifts — many drivetrain and chassis service components cross-fit between the H and G generations and the Case loader-backhoe line.

Locating your serial number On the Case 588H the serial number is stamped on the frame near the operator platform, with a duplicate data plate in the cab. Tier 4 Final aftertreatment and ECU calibrations are matched to specific engine serials — confirm before ordering year-specific parts.

What changed from the 588G to the 588H

  • Engine emissions. Tier 4 Final replaces the Tier 3 powerplant on the 588G. Common-rail injection, electronic engine control with closed-loop feedback, cooled EGR, and DPF/SCR aftertreatment where fitted.
  • Aftertreatment. DPF and (on SCR-equipped configurations) DEF dosing add service points not present on the Tier 3 588G — periodic DPF regens, DEF tank refills, and NOx/pressure sensor service.
  • Operator station. Refined cab and controls, updated gauges and monitor display, and improved climate control on enclosed-cab configurations.
  • Power shuttle. Refined power-shuttle modulation for smoother forward/reverse transitions under load.
  • Drivetrain options. 2WD and 4WD configurations carried forward, with 4WD remaining the preferred choice for off-road and soft-ground material handling.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment — service implications The 588H’s DPF and (where fitted) SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tanks must stay topped with spec fluid. DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations. NOx sensors and DPF pressure sensors fail occasionally and can trigger engine derate if not replaced.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 588H runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a refined heavy-duty power-shuttle transmission. 8,000 lb mast and chassis components handle the lifting and reach, with optional 4WD for off-road and soft-ground capability. Tier 4 Final emissions equipment (DPF, DEF/SCR where fitted) defines the service envelope versus the Tier 3 588G predecessor.

The mast hydraulic circuit drives lift and tilt cylinders plus optional side-shift attachments. Steering hydraulics run a dedicated motor and cylinder system. The drivetrain uses planetary final drives at each axle, with 4WD configurations adding a front driveshaft and front-axle differential service points.

Common service items on the 588H

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel — pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, common-rail injection.
  • Tier 4 Final aftertreatment: DPF service intervals, DEF dosing and SCR catalyst where fitted, EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Power shuttle / transmission: Power shuttle clutch packs, forward/reverse modulation, transmission filters and oil. Power-shuttle is a defining feature on Case RT forklifts.
  • Steering hydraulics: Steering motor, steering cylinder seal kit, control valve, hoses.
  • Mast hydraulics: Lift cylinder seal kits, tilt cylinder seal kits, chain tensioner, control valves, hoses.
  • Mast components: Mast carriage rollers, lift chain inspection and replacement, fork carriage bushings, side-shift attachment service.
  • Drivetrain: Front and rear axles, planetary final drives, driveshaft, axle differential service. 175978A1 needle bearing 10-pack and K395075 thrust washer are G-series direct fits that also cover Case loader-backhoe applications.
  • Brakes: A168790 parking brake disc cross-fits Case forklift, loader-backhoe, and trencher applications — high-volume service item. Service brakes and brake hydraulics.
  • Cab / operator station: Seat, joystick / lever controls, gauges, key switch, ROPS canopy or enclosed cab. 377594A5 cab air filter fits Case loader tractor, backhoe, and forklift cabs.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the chassis serial on the 588H before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts and Tier 4 Final aftertreatment components must match the engine serial.
  • Mast configuration. Lift height, carriage width, fork length, and side-shift options vary — confirm mast spec before ordering carriage, chain, or cylinder parts.
  • Two-wheel vs. four-wheel drive. 2WD and 4WD configurations exist — front-axle, driveshaft, and differential parts differ.
  • Tier 4 Final emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production — confirm by serial.
  • Power-shuttle generation. Power-shuttle transmissions saw revisions — confirm by serial before ordering clutch-pack components.

Why the 588H still earns a paycheck

The 588H is Case’s top-of-line current production rough terrain forklift — 8,000 lb capacity, Tier 4 Final emissions, and optional 4WD for rough-terrain work. Heavy construction, forestry, large lumberyards, and industrial material handling are its core jobs, and parts availability is supported by the broader Case G/H-series and loader-backhoe cross-fit catalog.

Maintenance priorities on a 588H: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior, DEF quality on SCR-equipped units, power-shuttle service, mast hydraulic service, driveline service (4WD adds front-axle, driveshaft, and differential service), and brake service (A168790 cross-fit).
