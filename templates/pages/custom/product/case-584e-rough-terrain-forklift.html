Where the 584E fits in the Case forklift line

The 584E is the E-series successor to the 584D - refined frame, updated hydraulics, and improved operator station versus the D-generation. It sits at the upper end of the 584-class lineage before the platform transitions to the larger 585 and 586 rough terrain forklifts. The 584E shares a large portion of its drivetrain and brake parts ecosystem with the Case loader-backhoe and trencher lines, which means parts availability is strong across the broader Case construction catalog.

What changed from the 584D to the 584E

  • Frame and chassis. Refined frame structure carried forward from the D-series with reinforcement in high-stress areas.
  • Hydraulics. Updated mast hydraulic flow capacity and control valving for smoother lift modulation.
  • Operator station. Improved seat, control layout, and cab visibility versus the D-series predecessor.
  • Power shuttle. Refined forward/reverse modulation - same proven Case power-shuttle architecture used across the RT forklift and loader-backhoe lines.
  • Engine. Continued Case mechanical-injection diesel - pre-emissions, dealer- and aftermarket-supported.
Locating your serial number On the Case 584E the serial number is stamped on the frame near the operator platform, with a duplicate on the cab data plate. E-series production saw multiple running revisions to the power-shuttle and mast hydraulic circuits - confirm the serial before ordering year-specific clutch-pack or valve components.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 584E runs a Case mechanical-injection diesel through a refined power-shuttle transmission. The E-series brought improvements in hydraulic flow capacity, operator station ergonomics, and cab visibility versus the D-series predecessor. Mast hydraulics, axles, and brakes remain in the broad Case RT forklift parts ecosystem - many components cross-fit the 585G, 586G, and 588G as well as the Case loader-backhoe and trencher lines.

The power-shuttle transmission is the defining drivetrain feature on Case RT forklifts. It allows the operator to change direction without engaging a clutch pedal, which is critical for production lift cycles. The same power-shuttle architecture is used on Case loader-backhoes, so clutch-pack, modulation valve, and shuttle filter parts are well-supported.

Common service items on the 584E

  • Engine internals: Case mechanical-injection diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, bearings.
  • Fuel system: Mechanical injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Power shuttle / transmission: Power shuttle clutch packs, forward/reverse modulation, transmission filters and oil.
  • Steering hydraulics: Steering motor, steering cylinder seal kit, control valve, hoses.
  • Mast hydraulics: Lift cylinder seal kits, tilt cylinder seal kits, chain tensioner, control valves, hoses.
  • Mast components: Mast carriage rollers, lift chain inspection and replacement, fork carriage bushings, side-shift attachment service.
  • Drivetrain: Front and rear axles, planetary final drives, driveshaft, axle differential service. The 175978A1 needle bearing 10-pack and K395075 thrust washer fit 585G / 586G / 588G plus loader-backhoe applications.
  • Brakes: The A168790 parking brake disc cross-fits Case forklift / loader-backhoe / trencher applications - high-volume service item. Service brakes and brake hydraulics.
  • Cab / operator station: Seat, joystick / lever controls, gauges, key switch, ROPS canopy or enclosed cab. The 377594A5 cab air filter fits Case loader tractor / backhoe / forklift cabs.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the Case 584E forklift before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Mast configuration. Lift height, carriage width, fork length, and side-shift options vary - confirm mast spec before ordering carriage, chain, or cylinder parts.
  • Two-wheel vs. four-wheel drive. Some Case RT forklift configurations are available in 2WD and 4WD - drivetrain parts differ.
  • Power-shuttle generation. Power-shuttle transmissions saw running revisions - confirm by serial before ordering clutch-pack components.

Why the 584E still earns a paycheck

The 584E is the E-series 5,000 lb rough terrain forklift - improved over the 584D in ergonomics and hydraulic refinement while retaining the rugged construction-grade durability that defines the 584-class lineage. Pre-emissions mechanical-injection diesel keeps the 584E simple to service in the field, and the power-shuttle drivetrain shares architecture with the broader Case loader-backhoe and RT forklift family.

Maintenance priorities on a 584E: clean fuel, power-shuttle oil quality, mast chain and lift cylinder seal replacement, brake service (A168790 cross-fit), and engine service per the Case schedule. Broken Tractor maintains parts coverage across the 584-class platform alongside the 585G, 586G, and 588G G-series machines that share many drivetrain and brake components.
