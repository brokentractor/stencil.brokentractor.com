John Deere 700K Crawler Dozer: Tier 4 Final Mid-to-Large Hydrostatic

Built from the mid-2010s forward, the 700K brought the JD mid-to-large crawler dozer line to EPA Tier 4 Final emissions with a refined PowerTech 6068 6-cylinder diesel, DPF and SCR aftertreatment, a modernized sealed cab, and SmartGrade-ready electronics. The K-gen platform set the stage for the JD 700 P-Tier.

Where the 700K fits in the JD crawler family

The 700K succeeded the 700J as JD’s EPA Tier 4 Final mid-to-large hydrostatic crawler dozer. The K-gen platform took the 700 line to the strictest current emissions tier with a Tier 4 Final PowerTech 6068 6-cylinder engine paired with diesel particulate filter (DPF) and selective catalytic reduction (SCR) aftertreatment.

Hydrostatic dual-path drive carried forward - refined but unchanged in concept. The 700K cab was modernized further with a larger sealed-and-pressurized operator station, refined joystick controls, a full-color monitor display, and integrated grade-control compatibility. Standard, LGP, and LT configurations were factory options.

What changed from the 700J to the 700K

  • Engine. EPA Tier 4 Final JD PowerTech 6068. Refined fuel system, electronic engine control with closed-loop feedback, cooled EGR, DPF for particulate control, and SCR catalyst with DEF dosing for NOx control.
  • Aftertreatment. DPF and SCR add service requirements not present on the Tier 3 700J - periodic DPF cleaning, DEF tank refills, NOx sensor service, and DPF differential pressure sensor monitoring.
  • Operator station. Larger sealed cab with improved climate control, refined joystick controls, full-color monitor display, integrated grade-control compatibility.
  • Hydrostatic drive. Refined pump and motor controls with smoother modulation and improved low-speed response.
  • Lighting. Standard LED lighting on most configurations.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The 700K’s DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up. DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations. NOx sensors fail occasionally and trigger derate conditions. Factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget on K-gen machines.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 700K’s PowerTech 6068 Tier 4 Final engine powers the dual-path drive system, the implement hydraulic circuit, and the steering hydraulics. The 6068 platform is JD’s mid-large 6-cylinder workhorse - shared across construction, forestry, and agricultural equipment, and well-supported by the dealer network and aftermarket.

The implement hydraulic circuit drives a 6-way blade on most configurations. Cylinder bore and stroke vary with blade configuration. The undercarriage carries the JD-pattern sealed-and-lubricated track chain, planetary final drives, and JD-pattern rollers and idlers. Standard, LGP, and LT (long-track) configurations are factory options.

Common service items on the 700K

  • Aftertreatment: DPF cleaning service, DEF dosing units, NOx sensors, DPF pressure sensors, SCR catalyst service.
  • Hydrostatic pumps and motors: Drive pump rebuilds, charge pump components, drive motor rebuilds, case drain hoses, filter housings.
  • Hydraulic filtration: Suction strainers, return filters, charge filter, hydraulic oil.
  • Undercarriage: Track chains, track shoes, rollers, idlers, sprockets, idler springs.
  • Engine internals: JD PowerTech 6068 Tier 4 Final rebuild kits - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, high-pressure fuel pump, lift pump, fuel filters, water separator.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, hydraulic oil cooler, EGR cooler, water pump, thermostat, hoses.
  • Implement hydraulics: Blade cylinders, seal kits, control valves, hoses.
  • Final drives: Sprockets, planetary gears, seals, bearings.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, harnesses, ECU, monitor display, switches, sensors.
  • Cab: Glass, weather seals, door parts, seat, A/C compressor and components.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. The 700K had running changes through its production. Match parts to the machine serial.
  • Engine serial. PowerTech 6068 Tier 4 Final internal parts must match the engine serial.
  • Aftertreatment configuration. DPF and SCR part numbers can vary across the production run. Confirm against existing aftertreatment serial tags.
  • Hydrostatic pump/motor variant. Drive pump and motor part numbers can vary.
  • Blade configuration. 6-way, angle-only, and straight-blade configurations have different cylinder specifications.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Standard, LGP, and LT (long-track) variants existed.
  • Grade-control package. Equipped vs. non-equipped configurations affect cab harnesses and hydraulic control valves.

Why the 700K remains a production-grade pick

The 700K is the modern, full-emissions JD mid-to-large hydrostatic crawler dozer that customers in current production fleets are running. It runs the proven PowerTech 6068 6-cylinder engine family, a refined hydrostatic dual-path drive, and a cab that meets the operator-experience expectations of any modern crawler.

For customers running a 700K, maintenance priorities are: clean fuel (Tier 4 Final common-rail injectors don’t tolerate contamination), DEF quality (poor DEF damages SCR catalysts), DPF service intervals, clean cool hydraulic oil, undercarriage replacement on schedule, and engine rebuilds when wear demands.
