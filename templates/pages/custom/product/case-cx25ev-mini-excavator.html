Where the CX25EV fits in the Case excavator line

The CX25EV is Case’s battery-electric entry in the 2.5-ton mini excavator class. It runs lithium-ion energy storage in place of a diesel engine and fuel tank, with electric motors driving the hydraulic system and the dual-path hydrostatic propel circuit. The footprint, dig envelope, and operator interface are sized for the same compact-excavator duty cycles as a comparable diesel 2.5-ton machine.

In Case’s compact lineup the CX25EV sits between the smaller CX19D and the larger CX26C. Where the diesel siblings target outdoor general-purpose duty, the CX25EV is the right tool for indoor demolition, enclosed structures, urban utility work, and any site with emissions or noise restrictions.

What makes the CX25EV different

  • Battery in place of engine. A lithium-ion battery pack replaces the diesel engine and fuel tank. There is no engine oil, no coolant for combustion, no fuel filter, no DPF or DEF system to maintain.
  • Electric pump drive. The main hydraulic pump is driven by an electric motor, not by a belt or direct engine PTO. Hydraulic delivery is on demand and modulated electronically.
  • Electric propel. Drive motors and the swing motor are part of a power-electronics-controlled circuit, with the hydrostatic dual-path drive carried forward at the wheel motor.
  • Zero tailpipe emissions. No exhaust at the machine. Indoor work, enclosed structures, and emissions-restricted sites are all in play.
  • Low noise. The dominant noise sources are the hydraulic pump and the cooling fans for power electronics - far quieter than a diesel.
  • HV-qualified service. Battery pack, inverter, and high-voltage cabling require high-voltage qualified technicians.
High-voltage service - safety first The CX25EV’s battery, inverter, and drive-motor cabling operate at voltages that require trained HV technicians and proper PPE. Do not attempt to service the battery pack, inverter, or HV harness without proper qualification. Pin/bushing, cylinder seal, undercarriage, and cab work is conventional - the HV system is the line to respect.

Battery, electric drive, and hydraulics

The CX25EV’s lithium-ion battery pack feeds an inverter and power-electronics module that drives the main hydraulic pump motor and the propel/swing drive motors. The hydraulic circuit downstream is conventional - a main pump feeds boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel functions through a control valve block, with cylinders at the boom, dipper, and bucket and motors at the swing and propel circuits.

The undercarriage is rubber-track on the 2.5-ton mini frame: drive sprockets, idlers, rollers, recoil springs, and rubber tracks. The swing system uses a swing motor with reduction gearing and a swing bearing - same architecture as the diesel mini excavator siblings.

Common service items on the CX25EV

  • Battery system: Lithium-ion pack inspection, cell balancing, BMS diagnostics, firmware updates, scheduled pack replacement.
  • Electric drive motors: Drive motor bearings and seals, pump motor bearings and encoders.
  • Power electronics: Inverter, contactors, HV cabling, charger interface, cooling for power electronics.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket, and swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, control valve service, hoses and fittings.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb hardware, quick-coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Rubber track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
  • Low-voltage electrical: 12V system, gauges, switches, lighting, harness.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the machine serial before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Battery generation and firmware. Lithium-ion pack and BMS firmware may have revisions across the production run - confirm before ordering pack-level components.
  • Power-electronics revision. Inverter and controller part numbers can vary - match by serial.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Quick-coupler interface. Confirm coupler interface before ordering attachment-side hardware.
  • Track configuration. Rubber track width and pitch must match - confirm against existing tracks.
  • HV qualification. Battery, inverter, and HV cabling service require qualified technicians - confirm your service path before ordering HV components.

Why the CX25EV earns its place

The Case CX25EV is sized for compact-excavator duty cycles where a 2.5-ton machine is the right tool - residential landscape, utility, irrigation, plumbing, and tight-quarters digging where larger excavators won’t fit. Zero tailpipe emissions and electric drive make it the right choice for indoor demolition, urban utility work, and any site with emissions or noise constraints.

Maintenance priorities on a CX25EV: battery health management and cycle counting, hydraulic seal replacement, rubber-track inspection and replacement, pin and bushing service, and electric drive-motor inspection. The conventional side of the machine - hydraulics, undercarriage, swing, boom, and cab - is parts-supported the same way as any 2.5-ton mini excavator. The HV side requires qualified service.
