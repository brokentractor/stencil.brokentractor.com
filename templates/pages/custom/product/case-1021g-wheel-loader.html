Where the 1021G fits in the Case wheel loader line

The 1021G is the current production version of Case's 1021 platform - successor to the 1021F and a sibling to the 921G below it and the 1121G above it in the G-series production sequence.

The G-generation took the Case large production wheel loader to full Tier 4 Final emissions: diesel particulate filter (DPF), diesel exhaust fluid (DEF) dosing, and a selective catalytic reduction (SCR) catalyst. Drivetrain remained the proven power-shift transmission with articulated frame steering. Cab refinements, telematics, and an updated electronic control architecture round out the G-series changes.

What changed from the 1021F to the 1021G

  • Emissions. Tier 4 Final aftertreatment with DPF, DEF dosing, and SCR catalyst replaces the earlier Tier 4 Interim configuration.
  • Electronic controls. Updated engine, transmission, and machine ECUs with refined calibration and improved diagnostics.
  • Cab. Updated G-series operator station with refined climate control, monitor display, and control layout.
  • Telematics. Current-generation telematics module integrated from the factory.
  • Drivetrain. Power-shift transmission carried forward with calibration updates.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The 1021G's DPF, DEF, and SCR systems perform well on clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up. The DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations. NOx sensors, DEF dosing modules, and DPF differential pressure sensors are recurring service items. Factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget on a production 1021G.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 1021G runs a Tier 4 Final Case diesel through a power-shift transmission feeding articulated front and rear axles with planetary final drives and wet-disc service brakes. The implement hydraulic circuit drives the loader linkage with a piston pump and electrohydraulic control valves. Articulation steering is hydraulic, with steering cylinders pivoting the front frame against the rear frame at the center articulation joint.

The aftertreatment system carries the full Tier 4 Final package: DPF for particulate control, DEF tank and dosing module feeding the SCR catalyst for NOx control, and an EGR cooler in the engine intake circuit. Common-rail fuel injection demands tight water separation and clean fuel.

Common service items on the 1021G

  • Aftertreatment: DPF cleaning, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst service, NOx sensors, DPF pressure sensors, EGR cooler.
  • Engine internals: Case Tier 4 Final diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets, and engine mounts. R49482 engine motor mount is a recurring service item.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, high-pressure pump, lift pump, water-separator filtration, fuel filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, charge-air cooler, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Loader hydraulics: Piston-pump rebuilds, cylinder seal kits, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Articulation steering: Steering cylinder seal kits, articulation pivot pins and bushings. G109152 steering cylinder bushing fits 521F, 621, 721, and 821 series.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, bushings, pivot pins, quick coupler.
  • Drivetrain: Power-shift clutch packs, transmission, axle seals, planetary final drives. Transmission shift lever assembly available.
  • Brakes: Wet-disc service brakes. S300718 brake shoe, A12985 slave cylinder, and N8695 master cylinder are recurring service items across the series.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness, telematics module. 87528688 AC blower speed resistor fits 521E through 1121F machines.
  • Operator station: Seat, gauges, control levers, cab parts. 47880728 cab fresh-air filter on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Generation matters. The 1021-series saw multiple generations (F, G). Confirm by machine serial before ordering generation-specific parts.
  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the left machine frame near the front axle.
  • Engine serial. Case diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Aftertreatment configuration. DPF, DEF, and SCR part numbers can vary across the G-series production run.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied.
  • Telematics module. Telematics hardware revisions vary across the production run.

Why the 1021G still earns a paycheck

The 1021G is Case's current production-class wheel loader at the 1021 size. Tier 4 Final emissions, DEF dosing, integrated telematics, and the refined G-series cab make it a fleet-ready machine for production loading, aggregate handling, and waste applications.

Maintenance priorities on a 1021G: clean fuel and tight water separation (common-rail injectors don't tolerate contamination), DEF quality and DPF/SCR service per the Case schedule, clean cool hydraulic oil, brake service intervals, and articulation joint inspection.
