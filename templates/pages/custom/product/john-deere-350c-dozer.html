John Deere 350C Crawler Dozer: Mechanical C-Generation Small-Class

The 350C is the C-generation refinement of John Deere’s long-running small-class crawler dozer line - a JD 3-cylinder diesel through a multi-speed mechanical transmission with steering clutches and brake bands, sized for utility, light construction, landscaping, and agricultural earthmoving. Mechanical-era simplicity and strong direct-fit parts availability keep these machines earning paychecks decades after production ended.

Where the 350C fits in the JD crawler family

The 350C succeeded the 350B and continued JD’s small-class mechanical-drive crawler dozer line. It carried forward the JD 3-cylinder diesel architecture, multi-speed mechanical transmission, and steering-clutch / brake-band control that defined the small-class JD dozer line through the late 1970s and into the mid 1980s, before the 350D brought further refinements.

For owners running 350-series machines, the 350C platform sits inside a strong cross-fit parts ecosystem. Most service items - engine side frames, track adjuster seal kits, blade tilt cylinders, idler hardware, transmission seals, cutting edges - cross-fit through 350 / 350B / 350C / 350D / 355D and (in some cases) further into the 400G line. That platform sharing is the key reason a well-maintained 350C is still a viable working machine.

What changed from the 350B to the 350C

  • Steering clutch and brake band refinements. The C-generation introduced updated steering clutch friction materials and brake band linings - AT117553 metallic steering clutch disc cross-fits 350C through 400G, and AT142174 steering clutch brake band is the 350C/350D/400G service part. These updates carried forward into the D-generation.
  • Refined transmission internals. Transmission seal evolution: AT13945 transmission seal is the 350C/350D/355D service item - distinct from the earlier 350/350B seal (AT12822).
  • Refined final drives. T112356-KIT final drive rebuild kit covers 350C and 350D specifically - the C-generation introduced final-drive refinements that the kit addresses.
  • Shared chassis / undercarriage with 350B. Engine side frames (AT36817 LH, AT36818 RH) and idler hardware (T16844 track idler shaft) carry across the 350/350B/350C/350D family.
  • Blade hydraulics carry over. AU13835 blade tilt cylinder is the 350/350B/350C service part - same hydraulic architecture.
Steering clutches and brake bands - the defining service profile The C-generation steering clutch and brake band design is the single most important service profile on a 350C. The mechanical steering system uses wet steering clutches to disengage power to the inside track during a turn, and brake bands to slow that track. Both wear under normal use. AT117553 metallic friction discs and AT142174 brake bands are direct-fit service parts that bring the steering system back to factory function - both also cross-fit into the 400G generation, so parts are widely stocked and available.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 350C’s JD 3-cylinder diesel drives a multi-speed mechanical transmission through a clutch and into final drives. Power to each track is independently disengaged by the steering clutches and modulated by the brake bands - that is the entire steering control on a mechanical-era crawler. The implement hydraulic circuit drives a 6-way (or angle) blade with the AU13835 tilt cylinder common across the 350/350B/350C generation.

Undercarriage on the 350C uses the small-class JD chain, roller, and idler hardware that cross-fits through 350D and back into the 2010 family - track idler shaft, idler plates, and adjuster seal kits are all multi-generation parts that simplify undercarriage rebuilds. The 905000 track adjuster seal kit is the direct-fit 350/350B/350C service part.

Common service items on the 350C

  • Steering system: AT117553 metallic steering clutch disc (350C-400G cross-fit) and AT142174 steering clutch brake band (350C/350D/400G).
  • Transmission: AT13945 transmission seal (C-generation specific) and T37064 second steering gear (24T, 24 spline).
  • Final drives: T112356-KIT final drive rebuild kit (350C/350D direct fit).
  • Engine internals: JD 3-cylinder diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets - overhaul kits widely available.
  • Engine side frames: AT36817 LH and AT36818 RH (350/350B/350C/350D/355D shared).
  • Implement hydraulics: AU13835 blade tilt cylinder (350/350B/350C).
  • Undercarriage: 905000 track adjuster seal kit, T16844 track idler shaft, T20851 threaded idler plate, T27516 track idler guide plate.
  • Blade cutting edge: U11707 center cutting edge and U11708 cutting edge end bits (350B/350C/350D).
  • Electrical / instruments: AT104755 engine temperature gauge (350-450C) and AT148149 tachometer (350B-555A).
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, hoses, fan belt.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. The 350C had running changes through its production. Confirm the serial before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. The JD 3-cylinder diesel internals are calibrated to specific engine serials - confirm separately from the chassis serial.
  • Steering clutch generation. AT117553 metallic friction disc fits 350C through 400G; AT142174 brake band fits 350C / 350D / 400G. Confirm before ordering that the clutch/band variant matches what is in the machine.
  • Transmission seal generation. AT13945 is the C-generation transmission seal (350C/350D/355D) - distinct from the earlier 350/350B AT12822 seal.
  • Final drive variant. T112356-KIT covers 350C and 350D - confirm the final drive matches the kit contents before ordering.
  • Cutting edge configuration. Center edge (U11707) and end bits (U11708) are standard 350B/350C/350D blade hardware - confirm blade width and bolt-hole pattern.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station parts differ between configurations.

Why the 350C remains a working machine

The 350C combines a stout JD 3-cylinder diesel, a mechanical transmission with steering clutches and brake bands that any experienced operator or mechanic can service, and a cross-fit parts ecosystem that runs from the 350 original through the 350D and into the 355D and 400G generations. For a small contractor, landscaper, or property owner who needs a small-class crawler without the service complexity of a modern hydrostatic Tier-4 machine, a well-maintained 350C is one of the best-value used JD crawlers on the market.

Maintenance priorities on a 350C: clean fuel on the 3-cylinder diesel, steering clutch and brake band service (AT117553 + AT142174 are the direct-fit parts), transmission oil and seal condition (AT13945), final-drive rebuild as the seals and bearings wear (T112356-KIT), undercarriage replacement as it wears, and standard cooling and electrical service.
