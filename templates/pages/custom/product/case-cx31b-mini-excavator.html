Where the CX31B fits in the Case excavator line

The CX31B is a 3.1-ton class B-Series mini excavator sitting in the heart of Case’s compact excavator range. The B-Series brought Tier 4 Final emissions compliance and a refreshed cab/control package to the small-class Case excavators, with the tight tail-swing geometry that defines the segment - the upper structure stays within the track footprint through swing, which makes the CX31B viable in spaces where a conventional-tail excavator simply can’t work.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX31B runs a small Tier 4 Final compact diesel feeding a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain on a rubber-track undercarriage. Boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel functions all draw from a single-section main hydraulic pump with electrohydraulic and mechanical pilot control on the joysticks.

The Tier 4 Final aftertreatment package on this size class typically includes a diesel particulate filter (DPF) for soot capture and an SCR catalyst with DEF dosing for NOx control. Common-rail fuel injection demands clean fuel and tight water separation - dirty fuel is the single most common cause of injector failure on this generation. The hydrostatic drive uses variable-displacement pumps and bent-axis or radial-piston travel motors with planetary final drives feeding the sprockets.

Common service items on the CX31B

  • Engine internals: Compact Tier 4 Final diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. Common-rail injection.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service intervals and regeneration behavior, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom / dipper / bucket / swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick-coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Rubber track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wipers: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
Tier 4 Final fuel and DEF discipline Common-rail injection on this engine class will not tolerate contaminated fuel - water and particulate destroy injector tips quickly and the parts are expensive. Keep the water separator drained, change filters on schedule, and only use API-spec DEF for the SCR system. Poor DEF damages the catalyst and triggers derate.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the machine serial - the B-Series had running changes through production.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.
  • Rubber track size. Verify track width and link count - the small-class machines have multiple rubber-track options.

Why the CX31B still earns a paycheck

The Case CX31B is sized for compact-excavator duty cycles where a 3.1-ton machine is the right tool - residential landscape, utility, irrigation, plumbing, and any tight-quarters digging where larger excavators won’t fit. Tier 4 Final emissions compliance, compact footprint, and rubber-track flotation suit residential and contractor work where a wheeled or larger tracked machine would mark up turf or fail to fit through gates.

Maintenance priorities on a CX31B: clean fuel with tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking and DEF quality discipline, hydraulic oil at temperature with clean filtration, rubber-track tension and wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service at every working joint.
