Where the 50G fits in the John Deere excavator line

The 50G is the largest of the small-mini G-Series mini excavators — above the 35G and below the 60G. With approximately 5.0 tons operating weight, the 50G provides more dig depth and reach than the 35G while staying small enough for residential and light commercial work.

The G-Series modernized the JD mini excavator line with refined hydraulics, Yanmar diesel power, and an updated operator station. The 50G shares a meaningful amount of service hardware with the rest of the mini line — particularly the cab glass and bucket linkage components.

Always verify the serial before ordering parts — running changes through the G-Series production affect bucket linkage, hydraulic, and electrical components.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 50G runs a Yanmar diesel through a hydrostatic drive into a rubber-track undercarriage. Yanmar engines are well-supported across the small-equipment aftermarket and parts coverage is strong. The hydrostatic drive feeds dual travel motors plus the implement and swing circuits through a main pump and control valve.

The 8063124 bucket H-link is direct fit and shared between the 50G and 50D, which makes it one of the highest-turnover wear parts on the 50G platform. The FYD00006272 front lower glass is shared across the 26G/30G/35G/50G mini line — one of the most-replaced cab parts in the entire G-Series.

Common service items on the 50G

  • Engine: Yanmar diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets, overhaul kits.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, hydraulic oil cooler.
  • Hydraulics: Main pump, control valve, swing motor, travel motors, boom and dipper cylinders, seal kits.
  • Bucket linkage: 8063124 bucket H-link direct fit (50G + 50D shared). Bucket pins, bushings, retention hardware.
  • Undercarriage: Rubber tracks, idlers, track rollers, sprocket, recoil spring and adjuster.
  • Cab glass: FYD00006272 front lower glass direct fit (mini-line shared).
  • Swing: Swing bearing, swing motor, swing reduction gearbox.
  • Electrical: Alternator, starter, sensors, harness, switches.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the frame beneath the operator platform before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Yanmar internals are calibrated to specific engine serials — match before ordering rebuild components.
  • Bucket H-link. 8063124 fits 50G + 50D — confirm bucket and linkage size before ordering.
  • Cab glass position. FYD00006272 is the front lower position only. Other glass positions have different part numbers.
  • Track width. Rubber track width is model-specific — measure before ordering.
  • Boom configuration. Conventional vs. boom-swing variants affect hydraulic hose routing and cylinder specifications.

Why the 50G still earns a paycheck

The 50G stays in service because the 5-ton class is the upper limit of what a contractor can comfortably trailer and operate without heavy-equipment infrastructure — and the lift, dig, and reach numbers cover the majority of residential, light commercial, and utility work that doesn’t require a 60G or larger.

Maintenance priorities on a 50G: clean Yanmar fuel, rubber-track wear monitoring, swing bearing grease, hydraulic oil cleanliness, cab glass (FYD00006272 cross-fits across the mini line), and bucket linkage wear (8063124 H-link cross-fits 50G + 50D).
