Where the CX210C fits in the Case excavator line

The CX210C is a 21-ton class C-Series excavator. It sits between the CX210B and the CX210D in Case’s full-size excavator lineup, and represents the Tier 4 Interim generation of the 21-ton platform. The C-Series brought refined cab ergonomics, updated electrohydraulic controls, and the emissions hardware that the platform would carry forward into the later D-Series.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX210C runs a Tier 4 Interim diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump - commonly a load-sensing piston pump - drives the boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel circuits through electrohydraulic control valves. Tier 4 Interim emissions hardware includes a DPF, EGR cooler, and on later builds DEF/SCR aftertreatment.

The boom and dipper run on the conventional 21-ton geometry with pinned and bushed joints at every pivot. Standard configurations include a counterweight sized for the 21-ton class and a steel-track undercarriage with sealed-and-lubricated chain.

Common service items on the CX210C

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Interim diesel rebuild kits - pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. Common-rail injection.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service intervals and regeneration behavior; DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst; EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom / dipper / bucket / swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: Cab glass and wiper service items - KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
Tier 4 Interim aftertreatment - service implications The CX210C’s DPF and (on equipped builds) SCR systems require clean fuel and quality DEF. DPF regenerations must be allowed to complete, DEF tank quality matters for SCR catalyst life, and NOx sensors will trigger derate conditions if they fail. Track aftertreatment service against engine hours.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the CX210C before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX210C still earns a paycheck

The Case CX210C is a 21-ton class production excavator - general site work, utility installation, residential and commercial earthmoving, mining and quarry work depending on configuration. The 21-ton class is one of the most-used excavator sizes in North American construction, and the CX210C remains a common sight on production fleets.

Maintenance priorities on a CX210C: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic oil condition, swing-bearing lubrication, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service at the boom and dipper joints.
