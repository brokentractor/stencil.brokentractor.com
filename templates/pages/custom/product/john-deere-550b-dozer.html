John Deere 550B Crawler Dozer: B-Generation Refresh of the Mid-Size 550

Built from the late 1970s into the mid 1980s, the 550B carried the mid-size JD crawler line forward with refined wet steering clutches, an upgraded JD 4-cylinder diesel, improved hydraulics, and a redesigned operator station, while keeping the mechanical drivetrain that made the 550 line a workhorse.

Where the 550B fits in the JD crawler family

The 550B succeeded the original 550 and brought the mid-size JD crawler dozer line in alignment with the wet-steering 450C philosophy. The defining mechanical change in the 550B was the move from the original 550’s dry steering clutches and brake bands to wet steering clutches running in oil - longer service life under aggressive operation and smoother engagement.

Beyond the steering upgrade, the 550B received an upgraded JD 4-cylinder diesel, refined implement hydraulics, an improved operator station with reorganized controls and better visibility, and ongoing undercarriage refinements. The multi-speed mechanical transmission carried forward.

What changed from the original 550 to the 550B

  • Wet steering clutches. The defining change - steering clutches now run in oil rather than dry. Longer service life under heavy production work.
  • Engine. Upgraded JD 4-cylinder diesel with refined injection system, improved cooling, and updated emissions controls as 1970s-1980s regulations evolved.
  • Hydraulics. Updated implement hydraulic pump, revised control valve package, improved cylinder seal designs.
  • Operator station. Reorganized controls, improved seat, refined gauges, revised ROPS canopy and cab options. Better visibility forward to blade corners.
  • Undercarriage. Continued refinement of sealed-and-lubricated track chain, roller frames, and idler designs.
Wet steering clutches - the B-gen identifier The fastest way to confirm a 550B vs. an original 550 is the steering clutch arrangement. Wet clutches require steering clutch case oil maintenance (drain and refill on a service interval). Dry-clutch original 550 machines don’t. The 550B operator’s manual specifies the steering clutch case fluid type and capacity.

Engine, drivetrain, and steering

The 550B ran a JD 4-cylinder diesel through a multi-speed mechanical transmission with planetary final drives. Steering was managed through wet steering clutches running in oil - the same architecture as the contemporary 450C, scaled up for the larger chassis. The implement hydraulic circuit drives blade lift, tilt, and angle on equipped configurations.

Common service items on the 550B

  • Wet steering clutch components: Clutch plates, separator plates, seals, springs, hydraulic actuation components.
  • Undercarriage: Track chains, track shoes, rollers, idlers, sprockets, idler springs.
  • Engine internals: JD 4-cylinder diesel rebuild kits - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump rebuilds, injectors, lift pump, fuel filters.
  • Cooling: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, hoses, fan belt.
  • Hydraulics: Pump rebuilds, cylinder seal kits, control valves, hoses.
  • Final drives: Sprockets, planetary gears, seals, bearings.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, harnesses, gauges.
  • Operator station: Seat, gauges, controls, ROPS canopy and cab parts.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. The 550B had running changes through its production. Match parts to the machine serial.
  • Engine serial. JD 4-cylinder diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Steering clutch generation. All 550Bs use wet steering clutches, but the clutch pack and seal design saw revisions during the production run.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Standard, narrow, and wider-track variants existed.
  • Blade configuration. Straight, angle, and 6-way blade configurations had different cylinder specs.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station parts differ between configurations.

Why the 550B is a strong used pick

The 550B is the sweet spot for customers who want a mid-size JD crawler with wet steering clutches but without hydrostatic drive complexity. It runs the proven JD 4-cylinder diesel, multi-speed mechanical transmission, wet steering clutches that hold up to production work, and a refined operator station compared to the original 550. Parts availability remains strong across the 550 family, available at Broken Tractor.

For customers running a 550B today, maintenance priorities are: monitor steering clutch case oil, keep up with undercarriage, rebuild the engine on a sensible interval, and replace hydraulic seals as they age. Broken Tractor maintains parts coverage across every 550 generation.
