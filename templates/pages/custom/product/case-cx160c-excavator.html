Where the CX160C fits in the Case excavator line

The CX160C is a 16-ton class C-Series excavator. It sits between the CX160B and the CX160D in Case's full-size excavator lineup. The C-generation refined the cab, brought a fuel-efficient Tier 4 Interim engine, and added advanced electrohydraulic control to the 16-ton class.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX160C runs a Tier 4 Interim diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump - commonly a load-sensing piston pump - drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel through electrohydraulic control valves. Compared to the earlier B-series, the C-series brings improved fuel efficiency through Tier 4 Interim emissions control, refined hydraulic flow sharing, and a modernized operator station.

The Tier 4 Interim aftertreatment system uses a diesel particulate filter (DPF), with EGR for NOx control. Some late-build configurations added DEF/SCR. Operators should track DPF regeneration behavior and watch DEF tank level where equipped.

Common service items on the CX160C

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Interim diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. Common-rail injection.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service intervals and regeneration behavior; DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst (where equipped); EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom / dipper / bucket / swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: Cab glass and wiper service items - KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
Tier 4 Interim aftertreatment - service implications The CX160C's DPF (and DEF/SCR where equipped) requires attention. Clean fuel is essential for common-rail injection longevity. Track regeneration behavior, keep DEF topped where applicable, and replace NOx and DPF pressure sensors when they fault to avoid derate conditions.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the CX160C before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX160C still earns a paycheck

The Case CX160C is a 16-ton class production excavator suited to general site work, utility installation, residential and commercial earthmoving, and mining or quarry work depending on configuration. The C-series sits at the modernization point in the Case 16-ton line - more refined and emissions-compliant than the B-series, but mechanically simpler in some respects than the later D-series.

Maintenance priorities on a CX160C: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic oil condition, swing-bearing lubrication, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service.
