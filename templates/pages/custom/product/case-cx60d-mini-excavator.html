Where the CX60D fits in the Case excavator line

The CX60D is a 6.0-ton class D-Series mini excavator and the 2022+ successor to the CX60C. It is the top of the Case mini class - the next size up, the CX75C SR, marks the start of the midi range. The CX60D is built for residential landscape, utility, irrigation, plumbing, and tight-quarters work where a larger excavator either won't fit or will mark up the site.

What changed from the CX60C to the CX60D

  • Engine generation. The D-Series carries forward a Tier 4 Final compact diesel with refined common-rail injection, electronic engine control, and updated aftertreatment calibration.
  • Aftertreatment. DPF, DEF dosing, and SCR catalyst remain - with running revisions during production. Confirm exact part numbers against the serial.
  • Operator station. Refined seat, joystick layout, instrument cluster, and HVAC over the C-Series.
  • Hydraulic controls. Updated electrohydraulic or mechanical control options on the main valve and pilot circuit.
  • Telematics. Equipped configurations include a telematics module for fleet monitoring.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX60D runs a small Tier 4 Final compact diesel into a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain riding on a rubber-track undercarriage. Boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel functions are fed from a single-section main hydraulic pump under electrohydraulic or mechanical control depending on configuration.

The rubber-track undercarriage gives the machine the flotation needed for residential and finished-grade jobs where steel tracks would tear up turf or pavement. Final drives are sealed planetary units; the swing system uses a swing motor, reduction gear, and a sealed swing bearing.

Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The CX60D’s DPF, DEF/SCR, and EGR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up. The DPF will request periodic high-temperature regens. NOx sensors fail occasionally and can trigger derate conditions. Plan aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget on any production CX60D.

Common service items on the CX60D

  • Engine internals: Compact Tier 4 Final diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, common-rail injection components.
  • Tier 4 Final aftertreatment: DPF service intervals and regen behavior, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler on turbo builds, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket, and swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick-coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Rubber track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wipers: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.

What to verify before ordering

  • Machine serial number. Confirm the serial on the CX60D before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX60D still earns a paycheck

The Case CX60D is sized for compact-excavator duty cycles where a 6.0-ton machine is the right tool - residential landscape, utility, irrigation, plumbing, and any tight-quarters digging where larger excavators won’t fit. Modern Tier 4 Final emissions compliance, a compact footprint, and rubber-track flotation suit residential and contractor work where a wheeled or larger tracked machine would mark up turf or simply not fit.

Maintenance priorities on a CX60D: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic oil at temperature with clean filtration, rubber-track tension and wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service at every working joint.
