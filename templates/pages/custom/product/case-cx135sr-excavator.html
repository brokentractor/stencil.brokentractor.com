Where the CX135SR fits in the Case excavator line

The CX135SR is a 13.5-ton class short-radius excavator sitting between the CX130D and CX140E in Case’s full-size excavator lineup. The SR (short-radius) geometry keeps the counterweight inside the track width, making it ideal for confined-space utility work, curbside jobs, and urban projects where a conventional-swing 13-ton machine would foul on adjacent structures or traffic.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX135SR runs a Tier 3 diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump - typically a load-sensing piston pump - drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel circuits through electrohydraulic control valves.

The short-radius tail-swing geometry reduces rear counterweight overhang for confined-space work. The tradeoff is slightly lower swing-stability when lifting at full reach compared to a conventional-swing 13-ton class machine, so lift charts and rated operating capacities differ from the standard-tail siblings.

Common service items on the CX135SR

  • Engine internals: Tier 3 diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, bearings.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket, and swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick-coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the machine serial before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Confirm shoe width and track pitch before ordering chain or pad sets.

Why the CX135SR still earns a paycheck

The Case CX135SR is a 13.5-ton class production excavator that handles general site work, utility installation, residential and commercial earthmoving, and quarry work depending on configuration. The short-radius tail-swing geometry adds urban, curbside, and confined-space capability that a standard-tail 13-ton machine simply can’t match.

Maintenance priorities on a CX135SR are clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, hydraulic oil condition, swing-bearing lubrication, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service at every boom joint.
