Case 586 Rough Terrain Forklift: Base Platform 586 RT Forklift

The Case 586 Forklift is the base platform for one of Case's most successful lines of rough terrain forklifts — preceding the lettered C/D/E/G/H variants. Designed for heavy-duty lifting in construction, lumber, and material-handling applications, the original 586 established the chassis, mast, and drivetrain architecture that carried forward through every successor generation.

Where the 586 fits in the Case forklift line

The original 586 is the foundational platform for the 586-class line that ran through C, D, E, G, and H lettered variants. It sits above the 584 and 585 lines in chassis size and lift capacity, and serves as the predecessor to the 586C and every later letter generation.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The original 586 runs a Case diesel through a power-shuttle transmission with mechanical front and rear axles. Heavier chassis than the 584/585 line, larger mast configurations available, and the platform that established the 586-class as Case's heavy-duty RT forklift backbone. The power-shuttle drivetrain — a defining feature on Case RT forklifts — gives the operator quick forward/reverse modulation without clutching, ideal for load-cycling work in lumber yards and construction sites.

Common service items on the 586

  • Engine internals: Case mechanical-injection diesel — pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets, bearings.
  • Fuel system: Mechanical injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Power shuttle / transmission: Power shuttle clutch packs, forward/reverse modulation, transmission filters and oil.
  • Steering hydraulics: Steering motor, steering cylinder seal kit, control valve, hoses.
  • Mast hydraulics: Lift cylinder seal kits, tilt cylinder seal kits, chain tensioner, control valves, hoses.
  • Mast components: Mast carriage rollers, lift chain inspection and replacement, fork carriage bushings, side-shift attachment service.
  • Drivetrain: Front and rear axles, planetary final drives, driveshaft, axle differential service. 175978A1 needle bearing 10-pack and K395075 thrust washer fit 585G / 586G / 588G plus loader-backhoe.
  • Brakes: A168790 parking brake disc cross-fits Case forklift / loader-backhoe / trencher applications. Service brakes and brake hydraulics.
  • Cab / operator station: Seat, joystick / lever controls, gauges, key switch, ROPS canopy or enclosed cab. 377594A5 cab air filter fits Case loader tractor / backhoe / forklift cabs.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.
Power-shuttle service — the highest-value drivetrain item The power-shuttle transmission is what makes a Case RT forklift productive, and it is the most expensive drivetrain component to neglect. Keep transmission oil clean and on schedule. Clutch-pack and modulation-valve service catches issues before a full shuttle rebuild becomes necessary.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the Case 586 forklift before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Mast configuration. Lift height, carriage width, fork length, and side-shift options vary — confirm mast spec before ordering carriage / chain / cylinder parts.
  • Two-wheel vs. four-wheel drive. Some Case RT forklift models are available in 2WD and 4WD configurations — drivetrain parts differ.
  • Power-shuttle generation. Power-shuttle transmissions saw revisions — confirm by serial before ordering clutch-pack components.

Why the 586 still earns a paycheck

The original Case 586 established the platform for one of the longest-running RT forklift product lines in Case Construction's history. Foundation chassis, mast, and drivetrain architecture carried through generation after generation, and parts coverage remains strong because so many components cross-fit to the lettered C/D/E/G/H successors and the broader Case loader-backhoe / forklift / trencher product family.

Maintenance priorities on a 586 (base): clean fuel, power-shuttle oil quality, mast chain and lift cylinder service, brake service (A168790 cross-fit), and engine service per Case schedule. Broken Tractor maintains parts coverage for the original 586 alongside every later 586-class variant.
