Case W20 Wheel Loader: Largest of the Original Case W-Series

The Case W20 sits at the top of the original-issue Case W-series wheel loaders - a heavy production-class articulated machine, big bucket, big breakout, big Case diesel, and the mechanical-injection, mechanical-hydraulic architecture that defined Case wheel loaders before the C-revision arrived.

Where the W20 fits in the Case wheel loader line

The W20 sits at the top of the original W-series - above the W18 and just under the C-revision W20C that succeeded it. Largest frame, largest loader cylinders, largest bucket capacity in the original W-series, and the same articulated layout, mechanical injection, mechanical hydraulics, and operator-serviceable architecture that has kept W20 units running on production sites for decades.

Locating your serial number On the Case W20 the serial number plate is on the right side of the loader frame near the rear articulation, beneath the engine cover. Confirm the serial before ordering year-specific parts - the W20 had multiple production revisions, and the serial is what pins the right component to the right machine.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The W20 runs a larger Case inline diesel through a mechanical or power-shift transmission and heavy articulated front and rear drive axles. Loader hydraulics use a heavy-flow gear pump with mechanical control valves and direct-acting cylinders sized for big-bucket production loadout. Wet-disc service brakes, planetary final drives, and a heavier ROPS-equipped operator station match the W20’s intended duty cycle - quarry pads, scrap operations, snow handling on large lots, and any operation where bucket capacity outranks cycle speed.

The mechanical injection pump and direct-acting hydraulic controls keep the W20 free of electronics. There is no engine ECU, no aftertreatment, and no monitor-driven diagnostics - a wrench, a parts catalog, and a service manual remain the primary tools for keeping a W20 in service.

Common service items on the W20

  • Engine internals: Case diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets, full overhaul kits. The R49482 engine motor mount moves consistently across the Case diesel family.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Loader hydraulics: Cylinder seal kits, hydraulic pump rebuilds, control valves, hoses.
  • Articulation steering: Steering cylinder seal kits, articulation pivot pins and bushings.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, bushings, pivot pins.
  • Drivetrain: Clutches, transmission components, axle seals, planetary final drives.
  • Brakes: Wet-disc brake service, master and slave cylinders, valves. The N8695 brake master cylinder, A12985 slave cylinder, and L15782 brake pedal valve are recurring Case wheel loader brake-service items.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.
  • Operator station: Seat, gauges, control levers, ROPS canopy parts, cab fresh-air filter (47880728) on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the right frame near the rear articulation before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Case diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Original W20 vs. W20C. The C-revision moved the serial plate to the LEFT frame and revised driveline and hydraulic components - make sure you’re ordering for the right generation.
  • Transmission type. Mechanical and power-shift drivetrains used different components.
  • Brake system revision. Wet-disc service brakes saw multiple revisions - confirm fit by serial.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations.

Why the W20 still earns a paycheck

The W20 is one of the largest production-class Case wheel loaders from the original W-series era. Mechanical injection, no electronics, no DPF, no SCR - the W20 responds to a wrench, a parts catalog, and a service manual. Owners who run high-volume loadout work and want a machine that can be rebuilt at the component level for decades keep these W20s working on quarry pads, scrap yards, and snow lots.

Maintenance priorities and ownership notes

Maintenance priorities on a W20: clean fuel and quality filtration, hydraulic seal replacement before cylinders weep into a problem, articulation pivot service when slop develops, brake service when actuation softens, and engine overhaul when wear demands.
