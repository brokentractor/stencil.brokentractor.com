Where the CX75C SR fits in the Case excavator line

The CX75C SR sits in Case’s midi excavator range (7-10 ton class), between the mini line and the full-size CX130 and up. Short Radius (SR) tail swing keeps the counterweight inside the track width, ideal for urban and curbside utility work where conventional-swing machines do not fit. It slots between the Case Mini and the larger Case Midi excavators.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX75C SR runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel circuits through electrohydraulic control valves. Short Radius tail-swing geometry reduces the rear counterweight overhang for confined-space work, while steel tracks support the heavier midi-class weights and handle abrasive-material applications.

The Tier 4 Final engine package includes common-rail fuel injection, DPF (diesel particulate filter), DEF dosing with an SCR catalyst, and EGR cooling. The aftertreatment stack is reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF but requires regular attention to filters, sensors, and regeneration behavior.

Common service items on the CX75C SR

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. Common-rail injection.
  • Tier 4 Final aftertreatment: DPF service intervals and regeneration behavior; DEF tank and dosing module; SCR catalyst; EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom, dipper, bucket, and swing cylinder seal kits; main pump rebuild; electrohydraulic control valves; hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear service, swing bearing inspection, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
Tier 4 Final - service implications The CX75C SR’s DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. Keep the DEF tank topped up, expect periodic high-temperature DPF regenerations, and watch for NOx sensor failures that can trigger derate conditions. Factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget.

What to verify before ordering

Why the CX75C SR still earns a paycheck

The Case CX75C SR is a midi-class excavator for jobs that demand more reach, depth, and bucket capacity than a mini can deliver, but don’t need the road footprint of a full-size 13+ ton machine. Utility contractors, urban construction crews, mid-size site work, and residential bulk excavation all lean on this class of machine. The SR geometry adds the confined-space versatility that conventional-swing midis do not have.

Maintenance priorities on a CX75C SR: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, DPF regen behavior tracking, hydraulic system condition, steel-track tension and wear monitoring, swing-bearing lubrication, and pin and bushing service.
