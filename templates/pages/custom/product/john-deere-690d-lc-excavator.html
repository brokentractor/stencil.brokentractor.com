Where the 690D LC fits in the JD excavator line

The 690D LC is the D-Series long-carriage heritage JD hydraulic excavator - the predecessor to the 690E and the later 200LC platform. It was produced from 1987 through 1991 and represents the late-classic, pre-electronic generation of JD construction excavators. Production-year cross-reference is by serial number, with 1987 machines starting in the #512741 range and stepping forward each model year through 1991.

Mechanically, the 690D LC is a straightforward hydraulic excavator: a JD diesel feeds a hydraulic main pump that drives the boom, arm, bucket, swing, and travel circuits. The long-carriage (LC) steel-track undercarriage gives the machine the stability and reach geometry that earned the platform its reputation as a contractor workhorse.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 690D LC runs a JD D-Series diesel through a hydraulic main pump into the boom, arm, bucket, swing, and travel circuits. Because the machine predates electronic engine controls and electronic hydraulic management, diagnostics are mechanical and service is straightforward for any shop comfortable with classic hydraulic excavators.

The long-carriage steel-track undercarriage carries the proven JD-pattern sealed-and-lubricated track chain, rollers, idlers, and sprockets. The AT152077 chain guide is direct fit and crosses to the 690E and 200 - useful when sourcing wear parts. For the swing, the AT106042 bearing is 690D direct fit and a high-value service item, particularly on machines that have seen heavy excavation duty.

Common service items on the 690D LC

  • Engine: JD D-Series diesel internals - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gasket sets, injectors.
  • Cooling: Radiator, water pump, hoses, fan, thermostat.
  • Hydraulics: Main pump, control valves, travel motors, swing motor, hoses.
  • Swing bearing: AT106042 swing bearing - 690D direct fit, available at Broken Tractor.
  • Arm cylinder: AT264189 - fits 690D, 790D, and Hitachi EX200.
  • Bucket cylinder: AT182451 - 690D direct fit.
  • Undercarriage: AT152077 chain guide (690D, 690E, 200), LC steel tracks, idlers, rollers, sprockets.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, harnesses, switches.
  • Cab: Glass, weather seals, door parts, seat.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Use the production-year serial cross-reference on the data plate (main frame near cab or engine housing) to confirm exact production year before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. JD D-Series diesel internals are serial-specific - match the engine serial when ordering rebuild components.
  • Swing bearing. AT106042 is 690D direct fit - confirm by application before ordering.
  • Cylinder cross-fit. AT264189 arm cylinder fits 690D, 790D, and Hitachi EX200. AT182451 bucket cylinder is 690D direct fit.
  • Chain guide cross-fit. AT152077 fits 690D, 690E, and 200 - useful when sourcing across a mixed fleet.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Long-carriage (LC) tracks differ from standard-carriage 690D in length and shoe pattern.

Why the 690D LC still earns a paycheck

The 690D LC stays in service because well-maintained 1987-1991 D-Series JD excavators are still earning paychecks. Mechanical reliability, strong direct-fit parts availability, and operator preference for pre-electronic-era construction equipment make these machines fleet workhorses for independent contractors and small excavation outfits. The platform sits early enough in the JD excavator timeline that diagnostics are mechanical, parts are accessible, and field service does not require dealer-only electronics tools.

Maintenance priorities on a 690D LC: clean fuel, hydraulic oil cleanliness, swing bearing grease and rotation schedule (AT106042 direct fit), cylinder seal service (AT264189 arm and AT182451 bucket - both direct fit at Broken Tractor), and LC undercarriage wear monitoring (AT152077 chain guide and associated wear parts).
