Where the TX742 fits in the Case telehandler line

The Case TX742 is the compact member of the modern Case Telehandler line - sized below the heavier TX170-45 and the boom-purpose-built 686G. What makes the TX742 stand out for parts customers is its driveline sharing with the Case 580M / 590SM backhoe loaders and the 586G rough-terrain forklift. That means service parts like the 85826230 engine fan belt cross-fit cleanly across the CNH backhoe-platform family.

For fleets that already run Case backhoes, the TX742 simplifies parts stocking - many cooling, engine, and driveline service items pull from the same shelf as the 580M/590SM.

Engine, transmission, and boom hydraulics

The TX742 runs a Case / CNH diesel - the same engine family that powers the Case 580M and 590SM backhoes - through a powershift transmission into a 4-wheel-drive driveline with steered front and rear axles. The single-stage telescopic boom carries the load through a hydraulic lift cylinder and extension cylinder, with a tilt cylinder controlling fork angle.

The 85826230 engine fan belt is a direct-fit Case TX742 service part - the same belt fits Case 580M, 590SM, 586G, and 440CT, which makes cooling-system service routine for fleets already stocking belts for the backhoe platform.

Common service items on the TX742

  • Engine internals: Case / CNH diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets, full overhaul kits - same engine family as Case 580M / 590SM.
  • Cooling system: 85826230 engine fan belt (direct-fit). Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, charge-air cooler.
  • Powershift transmission: Powershift clutch packs, transmission filters and oil, torque converter service.
  • Drivetrain - axles: 4WD front and rear axle service, planetary final drives, U-joints, driveshafts, steering joints.
  • Boom hydraulics: Lift cylinder, extension cylinder, tilt cylinder seal kits.
  • Boom wear pads: Side, top, and bottom wear pads - high-wear service items on telehandlers, replace as scoring appears.
  • Carriage / forks: Quick-attach carriage hardware, fork tine wear, fork retention pins.
  • Steering: Steering valve, steering cylinder seal kits, mode selector valve for crab / circle / front steering modes.
  • Brakes: Wet-disc service brakes, brake oil filters, park brake actuation.
  • Cab and electrical: Cab HVAC, ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness, alternator, starter.
Boom wear pads - the telehandler-specific maintenance item Boom wear pads are the most distinct service item on the TX742 versus the backhoe platform it shares parts with. Inspect for scoring at every service interval. Pads are position-specific (side vs. top vs. bottom, inner-section vs. outer-section) - confirm position and current thickness before ordering.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the chassis rail or cab data plate before ordering year-specific boom or transmission parts.
  • Engine serial. Case / CNH diesel internal parts are calibrated to specific engine serial groups.
  • Boom-wear-pad position. Wear pads are position-specific (side vs. top vs. bottom, inner-section vs. outer-section). Confirm position and current pad thickness before ordering.
  • Cross-platform parts. Many TX742 service items cross-fit Case 580M, 590SM, 586G, and 440CT - confirm the part number applies to the specific TX742 build position.
  • Steering-mode actuator. Telehandlers have 2WS / 4WS / crab steering modes selected through a hydraulic mode valve - confirm valve revision before ordering steering hydraulic service parts.

Why the TX742 still earns a paycheck

The Case TX742 stays in service because compact telehandlers earn their cost back on jobs where lift height matters but a full-size machine doesn’t fit - masonry sites, residential framing, lumber yards, equipment rental fleets. The TX742’s cross-fit driveline with Case 580M / 590SM backhoes simplifies parts sourcing for fleets that already run Case backhoes.

Maintenance priorities on a TX742: clean fuel and quality filtration on the CNH diesel, powershift transmission oil quality, boom-wear-pad inspection (scoring is the first sign), boom hydraulic cylinder seal condition, steering-mode valve health, and the 85826230 fan belt as part of normal cooling service.
