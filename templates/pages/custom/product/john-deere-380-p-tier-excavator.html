Where the 380 P-Tier fits in the JD excavator line

The 380 P-Tier sits between the 350 P-Tier and 870 P-Tier in the modern JD P-Tier construction excavator lineup. It carries Tier 4 Final emissions and benefits from JD/Hitachi shared platform service-part availability across the broader excavator family. The cross-fit AT250209 H-link (892DLC-370) applies to the 380 size class.

Locating your serial number On the John Deere 380 P-Tier the serial number plate is mounted on the upper frame near the cab entry, typically on the right-hand side beneath the door threshold or beside the operator’s seat. Always verify the serial number before ordering parts to ensure proper fitment.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 380 P-Tier runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic drive into a steel-track undercarriage. Aftertreatment combines DPF, DEF dosing, and an SCR catalyst to meet current emissions requirements. The AT317252 cross-pin is the broad JD excavator family service pin (180CW/190DW/200/200C/200G/210G/225C/230GW/2054/180G/190GW/210P/220DW/225D/245G/2154D) that fits across the JD P-Tier and G-Tier construction line.

The hydraulic system uses a main pump feeding a control valve stack with electronic hydraulic management - power modes, work modes, and attachment-specific flow settings are managed through the in-cab monitor. Steel-track undercarriage carries the JD-pattern sealed-and-lubricated chain, rollers, idlers, and sprockets.

Common service items on the 380 P-Tier

  • Engine: Tier 4 Final diesel internals - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF, DEF dosing units, SCR catalyst, NOx sensors, pressure sensors.
  • Cooling: Radiator, water pump, hydraulic oil cooler, heat exchanger, EGR cooler.
  • Hydraulics: Main pump, control valve, swing motor, travel motors, cylinders, hoses.
  • Boom pins: AT317252 cross-pin (broad JD family).
  • Undercarriage: Steel tracks, shoes, idlers, rollers, sprockets, chain.
  • Cab and electrical: Cab glass, alternator, starter, sensors, harnesses, ECU, in-cab monitor.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The 380 P-Tier’s DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up. DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations. NOx sensors fail occasionally and trigger derate conditions if not replaced. Budget aftertreatment service for any 380 P-Tier in production.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the upper frame before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine and aftertreatment serial. Tier 4 Final emissions parts are serial-specific.
  • Aftertreatment generation. DPF/SCR revisions saw running changes through production.
  • Boom-pin position. AT317252 cross-pin fits a broad JD family - confirm position before ordering.
  • Hydraulic pump generation. Pump revisions saw running changes.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Standard vs. long-track variants affect track and roller part numbers.

Why the 380 P-Tier earns a paycheck

The 380 P-Tier stays in service because modern P-Tier 38-Ton JD excavators deliver Tier 4 Final fuel efficiency and operator-station refinements that contractors rely on for production work. JD/Hitachi platform parts sharing means service parts are widely available across a broad family of similar-class machines.

Maintenance priorities on a 380 P-Tier: clean fuel and quality DEF, aftertreatment service discipline (DPF regens, NOx sensor replacement, SCR catalyst care), hydraulic oil cleanliness, boom-pin service (AT317252 has broad cross-fit), undercarriage wear monitoring, and cab plus electrical service.
