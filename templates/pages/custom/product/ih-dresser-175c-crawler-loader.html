Where the 175C fits in the IH/Dresser line

The 175C is the C-series Dresser refinement of the IH 175 crawler loader. It sits at the top of the IH/Dresser crawler loader range as the extreme-duty machine - built for mining, quarry work, and demolition where smaller TD7/TD8-class machines or the lighter 100/125 loaders would be overmatched. The C-series designation reflects Dresser-era updates that carried over from the late IH era after Dresser Industries took on the IH construction line.

The 175C runs a heavy Dresser diesel through a refined planetary drivetrain. Compared to the base 175, the C-series got upsized hydraulic capacity to drive the larger loader bucket and a heavier-class undercarriage to handle the abrasive conditions production users put these machines through.

What sets the C-series 175 apart

  • Hydraulics. Larger pump and circuit capacity than the base 175 to handle the heavier-duty bucket and loader arms.
  • Undercarriage. Heavy-class track chains, rollers, idlers, and sprockets sized for abrasive mining and quarry conditions.
  • Engine. Heavy Dresser diesel - the family used across the larger IH/Dresser crawler line, well-supported by aftermarket parts including head gasket sets and water pumps.
  • Operator station. Dresser-era cab and platform updates with revised seat package and controls.
  • Brakes. Steering brake service items that cross-fit with the TD7/TD8 dozer and 100/125 loader brake packages.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 175C’s heavy Dresser diesel drives both the steering/drive hydraulics and the loader implement circuit. The engine family is shared with the larger IH/Dresser crawler dozer line, which is good news for parts - head gasket sets, water pumps, and basic internal components are well-stocked aftermarket items.

The implement hydraulic circuit drives the loader bucket lift and tilt cylinders. The 623084C92 main hydraulic pump is the standard Dresser cross-fit for the platform. Steering uses oil-bath disc-type steering brakes that share service parts with the TD7/TD8 dozer brake packages - the DBSKIT and DBSKIT-2 are the common service kits.

Common service items on the 175C

  • Engine internals: IH/Dresser diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. The S.57678 head gasket set cross-fits the IH/Dresser engine family.
  • Cooling system: The 3132738R93 water pump fits IH/Dresser dozers and crawler loaders.
  • Hydraulic system: 623084C92 main hydraulic pump (Dresser cross-fit), loader bucket lift and tilt cylinder service.
  • Steering brakes: DBSKIT basic steering brake kit and DBSKIT-2 brake shoe kit cross-fit from TD7/TD8 and 100/125 brake service. 621037C1 brake shoe.
  • Operator station: PV862 seat package for IH/Dresser dozers and loaders. 621619C1 bottom seat cushion fits 100/125 loaders and TD7/TD8 dozers.
  • Bucket and loader: Cutting edges, bucket teeth, pins and bushings, loader arm pin service.
  • Undercarriage: Track chains, rollers, idlers, sprockets, recoil springs - heavy class.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, gauges, switches, harness.
Heavy-duty undercarriage service The 175C’s undercarriage is the most expensive wear item on the machine. In mining and quarry service, expect track chains, rollers, and idlers to wear faster than on lighter dozer work. Measure pin and bushing wear regularly and rotate components on schedule - replacing one worn idler is cheaper than chasing premature chain failure.

What to verify before ordering

  • Machine serial number. Confirm the serial on the 175C before ordering year-specific parts. IH-era and Dresser-era machines have running changes.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial - rings, sleeves, and head gasket dimensions can vary across the production run.
  • Generation. The 175 platform saw IH-era and Dresser-era revisions. Confirm by serial before ordering hydraulic or brake components.
  • Undercarriage spec. Track shoe count, roller and idler configuration vary by build and option package - measure before ordering.
  • Bucket configuration. General-purpose, multi-purpose, and rock-bucket configurations have different cutting-edge and tooth specs.

Why the 175C still earns a paycheck

The 175C is the extreme-duty Dresser crawler loader - mining, quarry, and demolition at the top of the IH-Dresser crawler loader line. Production users still run these machines because the IH/Dresser engine family is well-supported, hydraulics are serviceable, and the heavy-class undercarriage tolerates conditions that destroy lighter machines.

Maintenance priorities on a 175C: clean fuel, regular hydraulic oil and filter service, heavy-class undercarriage wear monitoring, and loader cylinder seal service on schedule.
