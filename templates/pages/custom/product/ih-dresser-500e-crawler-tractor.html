Where the 500E fits in the IH / Dresser line

The IH 500E is the E-series refinement of the original IH 500 compact crawler tractor. International Harvester used the 500 platform as a versatile multi-purpose tracked machine suited to grading, light clearing, utility work, and farm-and-ranch dozing. The E-series carried that mission forward with refinements aimed at operator comfort, drivetrain durability, and ease of service.

Through the IH and later Dresser eras, the 500 family went through several generational changes. The 500E sits as a late-IH-era refinement of the platform, with parts and service items that frequently cross-fit with the 500C and other IH / Dresser compact crawlers.

What changed from the 500 to the 500E

  • Operator station. Improved seat package and refined operator-platform layout for longer days behind the controls.
  • Drivetrain durability. Refined steering brake and clutch components - the 500C/500E brake band and steering disc pattern is the durable late-IH design.
  • Hydraulics. Refined blade hydraulic circuit and updated main pump pattern that cross-fits Dresser-era dozers.
  • Service access. Updated component placement and access points compared to earlier 500-series machines.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The IH 500E runs an IH diesel engine through a refined E-series mechanical drivetrain into a tracked undercarriage. The engine family is well-supported in the aftermarket - the S.57678 head gasket set cross-fits across IH / Dresser diesels used in this class, and the 3132738R93 water pump is a common service item across the IH dozer line.

Hydraulically, the 500E uses a main hydraulic pump (623084C92 pattern, cross-fitting Dresser dozers) feeding the blade circuit. Steering is via brake band and steering disc - the 676915R92 brake band and 676833R1 steel steering disc are direct-fit 500C/500E components, with the 675848R2 fiber steering disc covering the 500 fiber-pattern application.

Common service items on the 500E

  • Engine internals: IH 500-series diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. The S.57678 head gasket set is a known IH / Dresser cross-fit.
  • Cooling system: 3132738R93 water pump fits IH / Dresser dozers, plus thermostats, hoses, and radiators.
  • Steering brakes: 500C/500E direct-fit 676915R92 brake band, 676833R1 steel steering disc, and 675848R2 fiber steering disc for the IH 500 fiber pattern.
  • Hydraulic system: 623084C92 main hydraulic pump cross-fits Dresser dozers; blade angle and lift cylinders, valve seal kits, hoses.
  • Operator station: PV862 seat package fits IH / Dresser dozers and loaders, plus armrests and bottom cushions.
  • Blade: Cutting edges, mounting hardware including 5J4773 cutting edge bolts, pivot pins, and bushings.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, gauges, switches, and harness service items.
  • Undercarriage: Track chains, shoes, rollers, idlers, sprockets - measure before ordering as configurations vary by build.
Steering brakes - the 500E’s most common wear item The 676915R92 brake band and 676833R1 steel steering disc are direct-fit 500C/500E components and represent the most common drivetrain service on these machines. If your steering is grabbing, slipping, or pulling, plan a brake band and disc inspection before chasing other causes.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the 500E before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Generation. IH-era and Dresser-era machines saw multiple revisions - confirm by serial before ordering steering, brake, and drivetrain components.
  • Steering disc material. 500C/500E uses the 676833R1 steel disc; the 675848R2 fiber disc covers the IH 500 fiber-pattern application - confirm which is in your machine.
  • Undercarriage spec. Track shoe count, roller, and idler configuration vary by build - measure before ordering.
  • Blade configuration. Angle, straight, and 6-way blade variants have different cylinders and hardware.

Why the 500E still earns a paycheck

The IH 500E is the refined late-IH-era compact crawler that operators still pull off the back of the trailer for grading, clearing, and utility work. It runs a well-supported IH diesel, a refined mechanical drivetrain with the durable 500C/500E brake and steering pattern, and a hydraulic system that shares major service parts with the broader IH / Dresser dozer line.

Maintenance priorities on a 500E: clean fuel, brake band and steering disc service (676915R92 brake band + 676833R1 steering disc are 500C/500E direct-fit), engine service per IH schedule, hydraulic oil and filter changes, and undercarriage replacement on schedule.
