Case 1450B Crawler Dozer: Specs, Parts & Common Issues

Produced 1977 - 1985, the Case 1450B is the B-generation heavy-class Case crawler dozer - Case A504BDT inline six-cylinder turbocharged diesel, power shift transmission with torque converter, and a refined operator station and hydraulics over the original 1450. The 1450B's eight-year production run made it one of the most widely deployed heavy-class Case dozers of the late 1970s and early 1980s.

The Case 1450B Crawler Dozer is the B-generation heavy-class Case crawler dozer, produced from 1977 through 1985. The 1450B is the direct successor to the original Case 1450 and refines that machine's chassis, operator station, and hydraulics while retaining the proven Case A504BDT turbocharged six-cylinder diesel and power shift transmission. Production overlapped with the late 1450 (which ended in 1980) and continued for five more years through 1985.

For owners and operators today, the 1450B is one of the most refined pre-modern heavy-class Case dozers. The Case A504BDT turbocharged 8.3 L six-cylinder is well-documented, the power shift drivetrain is field-serviceable, and the chassis is substantial enough for serious commercial dirt work.

The 1450B is the workhorse heavy-class Case dozer of the late 1970s and early 1980s The 1450B's eight-year production run gives it the broadest used-market presence of any pre-modern heavy-class Case dozer. Compared to the original 1450, the B-generation operator station and hydraulics are substantially refined. Compared to the smaller 1150B family below, the 1450B has more displacement, more pushing power, and a heavier-duty undercarriage.

How the 1450B differs from the original 1450

  • Refined operator station - improved ROPS canopy, updated control layout, refined gauge cluster, better seat mount.
  • Updated hydraulic system - refined open-center implement hydraulics with improved valve response.
  • Higher engine output - refinements to the Case A504BDT bump output relative to the original 1450 base configuration.
  • Updated accessory packaging - alternator, starter, and accessory belt routing updated.
  • Same Case A504BDT 6-cyl engine - engine architecture continues from the 1450, providing parts continuity.

Case A504BDT inline 6-cylinder turbocharged engine

The 1450B continues the Case A504BDT inline six-cylinder turbocharged diesel from the original 1450 - 504 cubic inches of displacement (approximately 8.3 liters), with turbocharging. The A504 is the larger Case heavy-class diesel of the 1970s and early 1980s, used across heavy Case industrial applications of the era.

The A504 engine ecosystem is broad - accessory parts (water pump, alternator, starter, oil pump) share with other heavy Case industrial 6-cyl applications. Injection pump and injector service is well-documented. Turbocharger service is a routine wear item.

Power shift transmission with torque converter

The 1450B uses the same power shift with torque converter as the original 1450. Clutchless gear changes under load, multiple forward and reverse speeds, torque converter providing smooth power transfer. Routine service items: shift solenoids and pressure switches, clutch packs, the torque converter, transmission oil cooler, transmission filter.

The torque converter is a routine rebuild item on high-hour 1450Bs and is often paired with a transmission filter change at the same service interval.

Undercarriage

The 1450B uses heavy-class undercarriage shared with the original 1450. Larger sprockets, larger idlers, heavier track frame, wider track pad options than the 1150-class machines below. Sprocket bolt patterns, idler geometries, and master pin dimensions are 1450-family specific - confirm by part number against your serial.

Browse the Case Dozer Parts hub for current 1450B-era undercarriage availability.

Blade system and hydraulics

The 1450B ships with a 6-way bulldozer blade - wider and taller than the 1150-class blade. Angle and tilt control are hydraulically actuated. Blade cylinders, blade-carrier slider blocks, and cutting edges are routine service items. Cutting edge replacement is generally needed every few hundred to few thousand hours of dozing depending on material and operator habit.

The 1450B introduced refined hydraulic valving relative to the original 1450 - improved valve response. Seal kit availability for the blade cylinders is generally good.

Variants: Standard, LGP

The 1450B shipped in two primary track configurations:

  • Standard - standard-width track, balanced general dozing.
  • LGP (Low Ground Pressure) - wider grouser pads and extended track frame for soft-ground / wetlands / agricultural-soil work.

Track configuration affects undercarriage and frame parts. Confirm Standard vs. LGP by serial before ordering.

Common 1450B service items

Engine accessory aging

Water pump, alternator, starter, injection pump, and turbocharger on the A504BDT are routinely rebuilt or replaced over the engine's service life. Cooling system service (radiator core flush or replacement, fan belt, hose set) is a routine maintenance milestone on 35-plus-year-old machines.

Turbocharger wear

The A504BDT turbocharger is a routine wear item. Symptoms: blue smoke at startup or under load, oil consumption, audible whine outside the normal range, visible shaft play.

Power shift / torque converter service

Shift solenoid failure, clutch pack wear, torque converter degradation, transmission oil cooler service. The transmission filter and fluid should be on a scheduled service interval.

Hydraulic seal failure

Blade cylinder seal failures are common at this age. Cylinder rod chrome corrosion and seal weep are typical symptoms.

Undercarriage replacement cycles

Heavy-class undercarriage components have measurable wear lives. Modern aftermarket undercarriage is available for the 1450 family.

Operator station and ROPS

Cab and canopy hardware, seat cushion replacement, and floorboard wear are typical aging-machine issues. ROPS structural integrity should be verified before resale.

Wiring and electrical

35-plus-year-old wiring harnesses are routinely brittle.

Why the 1450B is still in service

The 1450B fills a productive niche for heavy-class dozer work - substantial pushing power and stability for major dirt work, road maintenance, and large-acreage land-clearing. The Case A504BDT turbocharged six-cylinder is well-documented and well-supported. The power shift drivetrain with torque converter is simpler to troubleshoot in the field than modern hydrostatic systems. For commercial dirt work, large agricultural site prep, and major land-clearing operations, a 1450B in good mechanical condition remains a productive machine.
