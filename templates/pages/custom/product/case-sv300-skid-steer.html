Where the SV300 fits in the Case skid steer line

The SV300 sits in Case’s alpha-series SV (vertical-lift) skid steer range between the Case SV280B and the Case SV340. It carries vertical-lift loader geometry, Tier 4 Final aftertreatment, the alpha-series operator cab, and electrohydraulic joystick controls. Vertical-lift geometry keeps the bucket parallel to the chassis through the lift arc — ideal for truck-loading, stacking, and material handling where forward reach at full height matters more than the ground-level reach pattern of a radial-lift SR-series machine.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The SV300 runs a Tier 4 Final compact diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain. Loader hydraulics use a load-sensing piston pump with electrohydraulic control valves. The vertical-lift loader geometry keeps the bucket parallel to the chassis through the lift arc — best matched to truck-loading, stacking, and material handling where forward reach at full height matters.

The alpha-series cab brought refined electrohydraulic joystick controls, an updated instrument cluster, and improved HVAC over the previous generation. Tier 4 Final aftertreatment adds a DPF, DEF tank and dosing module, and SCR catalyst to the exhaust path — all of which require attention to service intervals and DEF quality.

Common service items on the SV300

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final compact diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. Common-rail high-pressure injection on Tier 4 builds.
  • Tier 4 aftertreatment: DPF service intervals, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, very tight water-separator filtration.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulics: Loader lift and bucket tilt cylinder seal kits (86570919 / 86570931 / 86570922 cross-fit on NH/Case alpha-series), piston-pump rebuilds, electrohydraulic control valves.
  • Drivetrain: Hydrostatic dual-path pumps and drive motors, charge pump, filters.
  • Bucket coupler hardware: 47396814K bucket pin kit cross-fits L216-L230, C232-C238, SR160-SR250, SV185-SV300, TV380, TR270-TR340.
  • Hardware: 191048A2 axle stud & nut cross-fits broadly across the Case skid steer line.
  • Brakes / control: Park brake, control linkage, 48139268 control shock absorber on Case/NH alpha-series control valves.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, machine controller, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness, telematics module.
  • Cab / operator station: Cab HVAC, seat, gauges, joystick controls, cab glass, 47405956 windshield wiper blade on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Aftertreatment generation. Tier 4 Final configuration varies by build year - confirm DPF/DEF/SCR layout before ordering.
  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the Case SV300 before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations.

Why the SV300 still earns a paycheck

The Case SV300 is an alpha-series vertical-lift skid steer for truck-loading, stacking, and material-handling duty cycles where forward reach at full height matters more than radial-lift’s ground-level reach pattern. It pairs the modern alpha-series cab and electrohydraulic controls with proven hydrostatic dual-path drive and Tier 4 Final emissions compliance.

Maintenance priorities on a SV300: clean fuel and tight water separation (Tier 4 common-rail injectors don’t tolerate contamination), DPF regen behavior and DEF quality, hydrostatic charge pressure, drive-chain tension, and cylinder seal replacement when leaks develop.
