Where the 310J fits in the JD backhoe line

The 310J succeeded the 310G as the mid-class industrial backhoe loader in JD's lineup. Production ran from 2007 through 2011, with the Super-J variant (310SJ) running alongside as the heavier-duty option in the same chassis family. The J refresh brought EPA Tier 3 emissions compliance and a cab refinement pass focused on operator ergonomics.

Tier 3 on a backhoe of this class means a refined fuel system, electronic engine control, and cooled EGR - but still no DPF, no DEF, and no SCR. The Tier 3 engine is materially simpler to service than the Tier 4 K and L generations that followed, while still delivering the modern operator workflow customers expected from a late-2000s machine.

What changed from the 310G to the 310J

  • Engine. EPA Tier 3 JD PowerTech diesel. Refined fuel system with electronic engine control, cooled EGR, and updated emissions calibration.
  • Hydraulic performance. Improved hydraulic flow distribution and pump efficiency. Smoother control response across both loader and backhoe circuits.
  • Cab ergonomics. Enhanced operator station - refined seat, improved climate control, updated gauges and monitor display, reorganized controls.
  • Service access. Refined daily-service points and fluid sampling locations.
  • Electrical and electronics. Updated harnesses, sensors, and control modules to support Tier 3 engine control.
Tier 3 - no DPF, no DEF The 310J's Tier 3 engine meets emissions with cooled EGR and refined combustion - no diesel particulate filter (DPF), no DEF tank, no SCR catalyst. For customers in regions where Tier 3 machines are acceptable, the 310J is materially simpler to service than the Tier 4 310K and 310L. Often the preferred used pick for fleet managers avoiding Tier 4 aftertreatment service.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 310J's PowerTech Tier 3 diesel drives a refined power shuttle transmission into planetary final drives. The engine adds incremental electronic sophistication over the 310G - refined ECU calibration, updated sensors, and more precise injection timing - but the overall service profile is similar.

The implement hydraulic circuit feeds both the front loader and the center-pivot backhoe through a refined control valve stack. Loader cylinder bore and stroke vary with bucket configuration; the backhoe carries standard reach and extendable-dipper options. Drivetrain choices included 2WD and 4WD/MFWD axle configurations.

Common service items on the 310J

  • Engine internals: JD PowerTech Tier 3 rebuild kits - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, high-pressure fuel pump, lift pump, fuel filters, water separator.
  • Engine electronics: ECU, sensors, harnesses, monitor display.
  • Power shuttle: Shuttle clutch packs, control valves, hydraulic actuation components, shuttle fluid filter.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, EGR cooler, thermostat, hoses.
  • Hydraulics: Pump rebuilds, loader cylinder seal kits, backhoe boom/stick/bucket cylinder seal kits, control valves, hoses.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, pins, bushings.
  • Backhoe components: Bucket teeth, side cutters, pins, bushings, swing tower pin kits, stabilizer leg cylinders.
  • Drivetrain: Transmission components, axle seals, final drive gears.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, harnesses, switches, gauges.
  • Cab: Cab glass, weather seals, door parts, seat, A/C compressor and components.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. The 310J had running changes through its production. Match parts to the machine serial.
  • Engine serial. PowerTech Tier 3 internal parts must match the engine serial.
  • Tier 3 emissions configuration. Confirm cooled-EGR setup, ECU calibration, and common-rail fuel system components against engine serial.
  • Power shuttle revision. Shuttle clutch pack designs saw mid-run revisions.
  • Loader configuration. Standard, quick-attach, and specialty bucket configurations had different cylinder and mounting specs.
  • Backhoe configuration. Standard reach vs. extendable boom configurations differ in cylinder bore and stroke.
  • Drivetrain configuration. 2WD and 4WD/MFWD configurations have different front axle and transfer case parts.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Glass, weather seals, A/C parts, and door parts differ.

Why the 310J remains a strong used-machine pick

The 310J is the sweet-spot used 310 for customers who want a modern operator experience without Tier 4 aftertreatment service requirements. Tier 3 PowerTech engine with broad parts support, refined power shuttle drivetrain, improved hydraulics, and a cab that meets late-2000s operator-comfort expectations.

Maintenance priorities on a 310J: clean fuel (Tier 3 common-rail doesn't tolerate contamination), power shuttle fluid changes on schedule, hydraulic seal replacement as cylinders age, swing tower pin service, and engine rebuilds when wear demands.
