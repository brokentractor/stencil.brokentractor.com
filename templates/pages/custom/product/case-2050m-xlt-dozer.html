Case 2050M XLT Crawler Dozer: Specs, Parts & Common Issues

The Case 2050M XLT is the heaviest crawler dozer Case Construction Equipment has ever offered - flagship of the M-series line, Extended Long Track undercarriage, Cummins QSL Tier 4 Final diesel, and a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain scaled to handle large-acreage land-clearing, major commercial work, and mining-class push tasks.

Broken Tractor Editorial Team• Updated May 2026• 11 min read

The Case 2050M XLT is the largest crawler dozer Case produces - flagship of the M-series, Tier 4 Final emissions, Cummins QSL inline six-cylinder turbocharged diesel, and a hydrostatic dual-path transmission sized to match the chassis. The XLT designation marks the Extended Long Track undercarriage variant - longer track on the ground than the standard 2050M, lower ground pressure, more stability in the cut, more push-down force translated into productive blade work on heavy material.

Above the 1850M and outside the production envelope of the 1650M, the 2050M XLT targets applications that the smaller M-series machines run out of capacity for: large-scale land-clearing, major road and highway construction, mining and quarry work, large landfill operations, and heavy site prep where pushing power and undercarriage flotation both matter.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 2050M XLT runs the Cummins QSL inline six-cylinder turbocharged diesel - 8.9 liters of displacement, common-rail high-pressure fuel injection, electronic engine control, and Tier 4 Final emissions aftertreatment (DPF, DEF tank with SCR catalyst). The QSL is a heavy-class industrial Cummins with broad parts availability through Cummins service literature and industrial channels.

Drivetrain is hydrostatic dual-path: two closed-loop hydrostatic circuits - one per track - driven from the engine through a charge pump, controlled electrohydraulically through the operator’s joysticks. There is no torque converter and no power-shift transmission in the traditional sense; the hydrostatic dual-path layout is what gives modern Case dozers their counter-rotation and infinitely variable travel speed. Implement hydraulics are load-sensing on a separate circuit, sized for the heavy 6-way blade and rear ripper attachment options.

The XLT undercarriage extends track length on the ground versus the standard 2050M - translates to lower ground pressure (better flotation on soft material) and more stable blade work in undulating terrain. Final drives are planetary, sized for the engine output and undercarriage loads.

Common service items on the 2050M XLT

  • Engine internals: Cummins QSL pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets, full overhaul kits. Cummins industrial parts ecosystem applies.
  • Tier 4 Final aftertreatment: DPF service intervals and regeneration behavior, DEF tank and dosing module, SCR catalyst, EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Cummins common-rail high-pressure pump, electronic injectors, lift pump, very tight water-separator filtration - water in fuel is the fastest way to damage common-rail injectors.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts. Cab heater hose shut-off valve (301803R91) fits Case dozers from the 1150B era through the 2050M - a recurring service item.
  • Hydrostatic drive: Closed-loop hydrostatic pumps (two), track drive motors, charge pump, filters - keep this oil clean and at temperature.
  • Implement hydraulics: Blade lift, tilt, and angle cylinder seal kits, control valves, hoses, rear ripper cylinder service.
  • Undercarriage (XLT): Track chains, shoes, rollers, idlers, sprockets, recoil springs - heavy-duty XLT-spec components. Front idlers and bottom rollers run on tight tolerances under heavy ground pressure.
  • Drivetrain: Planetary final drive service, seals, bearings.
  • Brakes: Multi-disc wet brake service integrated with the hydrostatic deceleration logic.
  • Electrical / ECU: Cummins engine ECU, machine controller, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness, telematics module.
  • Operator station: Cab HVAC (heater valve 301803R91 cross-fits the dozer family), seat, gauges, joystick controls.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the right side of the cab platform before ordering year-specific parts. The 2050M has seen Tier 4 Final emissions revisions during production.
  • Engine serial. Cummins QSL internal and aftertreatment parts must match the engine serial. The QSL has multiple emissions and calibration revisions.
  • XLT vs. standard 2050M. Extended Long Track undercarriage uses different track chain length, idler and roller counts, and sprocket geometry than the standard 2050M.
  • SmartGrade configuration. SmartGrade-equipped machines have additional GNSS antennas, mast hardware, and control electronics - confirm by configuration before ordering grade-control components.
  • Tier 4 Final emissions revision. DPF, DEF dosing, SCR catalyst, and ECU calibration can vary by build year - confirm by serial.
  • Blade configuration. 6-way blade hydraulic geometry and rear ripper configuration vary - confirm by serial before ordering attachment hydraulics.

Why the 2050M XLT is the flagship

The 2050M XLT is the heaviest crawler dozer Case Construction offers - the machine that customers reach for when the 1850M runs out of pushing power or the application demands the flotation and stability of an Extended Long Track undercarriage. Major land-clearing, large-acreage site prep, mining and quarry push work, landfill operations, and heavy commercial road construction are the bread and butter applications.

Maintenance priorities on a 2050M XLT: clean fuel with tight water separation (common-rail injection demands it), DPF regen behavior and DEF quality tracking, hydrostatic charge pressure and filtration, undercarriage wear monitoring (XLT undercarriage components are sized for heavy ground pressure but track and roller life is still the largest single recurring cost on a dozer this size), and engine overhaul intervals tracked against Cummins QSL guidance.
