John Deere 350 Crawler Dozer: Specs, Parts & Common Issues

The John Deere 350 Crawler Dozer (original model, no letter) was introduced in 1965 as a compact crawler tractor designed for light dozing, grading, and slope work. With a John Deere 3-cylinder diesel engine producing approximately 42 horsepower, the 350 offered an ideal balance of maneuverability and capability for small farms, orchards, and light commercial dozing applications. The original 350 launched the long-running 350-series JD crawler line that ran through the 350B, 350C, and into modern JD compact crawler dozers.

The John Deere 350 Crawler Dozer is the original model in the long-running John Deere 350-series compact crawler line, introduced in 1965 as a successor to the John Deere 1010 / 2010 Crawler dozers. The 350 brought a modern 3-cylinder John Deere diesel engine, refined chassis, and improved hydraulics to the compact-class JD crawler segment - designed for light dozing, grading, and slope work where larger machines would be overkill.

For owners and operators today, the original 350 is a foundational compact JD crawler dozer. The combination of John Deere 3-cylinder diesel engine reliability, simple mechanical drivetrain, broad parts availability across the JD 300-series tractor family, and field-serviceable simplicity makes the 350 attractive to small farm operators, hobby restorers, and light commercial users.

The 350 launched a 50-plus-year JD compact crawler line The original 350 Crawler Dozer (1965 introduction, no letter suffix) is the starting point for the long-running John Deere 350 series. The line continued through the 350B (1971-1975), 350C (1975 - early 1980s), and later modern variants. When sourcing parts, confirm which 350 generation you’re working on - the original 350 uses chassis components specific to that era; later 350B/C parts may or may not cross-fit.

John Deere 3-cylinder diesel engine

The original John Deere 350 uses the John Deere 3-cylinder diesel engine - part of the broader John Deere “New Generation” engine lineup of the era. The 3-cylinder Deere diesel is well-documented and well-supported, with a parts ecosystem shared across multiple John Deere small tractor applications. Approximately 42 horsepower flywheel.

Routine engine service items: water pump, alternator, starter, fuel injection pump, injectors, head gasket, cylinder kits, bearings. The 3-cylinder Deere diesel is one of the most-supportable vintage compact diesel engines in the JD parts ecosystem. Browse the John Deere Dozer Parts hub for current 350 engine service kits.

Transmission and drivetrain

The 350 uses a mechanical transmission with multiple forward and reverse speeds. Steering is by track-disconnect clutches - pulling on the steering lever disengages drive to that track, causing the machine to pivot. The mechanical drivetrain is simple, field-serviceable, and well-documented.

Routine drivetrain wear items: clutch facing replacement, transmission gear and bearing service, steering clutch facings, final drive bearings. The drivetrain is straightforward to rebuild on a budget.

Track undercarriage

The 350 undercarriage is compact - sized for the small-class chassis. Sprocket bolt patterns, idler geometries, and master pin dimensions are 350-specific. Track chain elongation, sprocket tooth wear, roller seal failure, and idler bushing wear are routine items on a 50-plus-year-old machine.

Browse the John Deere Dozer Parts hub for current 350 undercarriage availability.

Blade system and hydraulics

The 350 ships with an angle or tilt dozer blade - hydraulically actuated. Blade cylinders, blade-carrier hardware, and cutting edges are routine service items. Cutting edge replacement is needed every few hundred to few thousand hours of dozing depending on material.

Common service items on a 50-plus-year-old 350

Engine accessory aging

Water pump, alternator, starter, fuel injection pump, and injectors on the JD 3-cyl diesel are routine service items on 50+ year old engines.

Clutch and steering clutch service

Main engine clutch and steering clutches wear with use. Replacement clutch facings, springs, and pressure plate service are routine.

Track undercarriage replacement

Track chain, sprocket, idler, and roller wear are all routine on 50-year-old undercarriage. Aftermarket replacement components are available.

Hydraulic seal failure

Blade cylinder seals fail with age. Cylinder rod chrome corrosion and seal weep are typical.

Operator station

Original seat cushions, floorboard wear, and the lack of factory ROPS on most early units. Aftermarket ROPS during restoration is common for safety.

Wiring and electrical

50-plus-year-old wiring harnesses are routinely degraded.

Why the 350 matters

The original 350 founded the John Deere compact crawler dozer line that ran for decades and continues in modern form today. For small farm operators, orchard maintenance crews, hobby restorers, and light commercial users, the 350 remains a practical, field-serviceable compact crawler with a well-supported JD 3-cylinder diesel engine and simple mechanical drivetrain.
