John Deere 110 Backhoe Loader: Compact Tractor-Loader-Backhoe

The 110 Backhoe Loader is JD's compact tractor-loader-backhoe (TLB) - designed for landscaping, light construction, and utility work where maneuverability and transport-friendly dimensions matter as much as digging capability. The 110 combines the comfort and visibility of a compact tractor with hydraulic loader and backhoe tools that punch above their size class.

Where the 110 fits in the JD backhoe line

The JD backhoe lineup spans three machine concepts: compact TLBs built on a tractor chassis, mid-size TLBs like the 210K, and the larger industrial backhoe loaders (the 310 through 410, 510, 610, and 710 lines). The 110 sits at the compact end - sized for landscape contractors, small utility crews, and rural property maintenance jobs where transporting between sites on a small trailer is part of the workflow.

The architecture is tractor-derived: front hydraulic loader, rear hydraulic backhoe on a center pivot, PTO availability for additional implements, and dimensions small enough to fit through standard gates and around residential or commercial landscaping confined-area work. The 110 isn't trying to compete with the industrial 310 line on digging depth or production capability - it's a different tool for a different job.

What sets the 110 apart from the industrial backhoes

  • Compact footprint. Built on a compact tractor chassis - through standard gates, on a small trailer, around residential landscaping with room to work.
  • Tractor-derived drivetrain. Optional MFWD for improved tractive effort on slopes, soft soil, and wet ground. PTO availability extends the machine to additional implements.
  • Center-pivot backhoe. Rear hydraulic backhoe on a center pivot, sized for landscape and utility work - not industrial production digging.
  • Hydraulic loader up front. Standard, quick-attach, and specialty bucket configurations were offered with different cylinder and mounting specs.
  • Operator station. ROPS canopy standard with cab configurations available on equipped machines.
Compact TLB vs. industrial backhoe - know which job you have The 110 isn't a small 310. The hydraulic flow, digging depth, lift capacity, and frame strength are sized for landscape and light-utility work. Customers who need the 110 are running it for the right job; customers pushing it into industrial backhoe territory will see component wear show up faster than expected.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 110 runs a JD compact diesel sized for the TLB platform. The hydraulic system serves both the front loader and rear backhoe circuits - typical of the TLB design where the operator transitions between loader work and backhoe work multiple times per cycle.

The drivetrain is tractor-derived with optional MFWD (mechanical front-wheel-drive) for improved tractive effort on slopes, soft soil, and wet conditions. PTO availability extends the machine to additional implements when needed. Cab and ROPS canopy configurations both existed - glass, weather seals, and door parts differ between them.

Common service items on the 110

  • Engine internals: JD compact diesel rebuild kits - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump, injectors, lift pump, fuel filters, water separator.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, hoses, fan belt.
  • Hydraulics: Pump rebuilds, loader cylinder seal kits, backhoe boom/stick/bucket cylinder seal kits, control valves, hoses.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, pins, bushings, quick-attach hardware (where equipped).
  • Backhoe components: Bucket teeth, side cutters, pins, bushings, swing tower components, stabilizer leg cylinders.
  • Drivetrain: Clutch, transmission components, axle seals, final drive gears.
  • MFWD (where equipped): Front axle assemblies, drive shaft U-joints.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, harnesses, switches, gauges.
  • Operator station: Seat, gauges, controls, ROPS canopy parts.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Match parts to the machine serial.
  • Engine serial. JD compact diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Loader configuration. Standard, quick-attach, and specialty bucket configurations had different cylinder and mounting specs.
  • Backhoe configuration. Standard reach vs. optional extended-reach configurations differ in cylinder bore and stroke.
  • Drivetrain configuration. 2WD and MFWD configurations have different front axle and drive components.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Glass, weather seals, and door parts differ.

Why the 110 remains a landscape contractor pick

The 110 is the right tool for crews that need a compact TLB they can transport on a standard trailer between sites, maneuver through residential gates, and operate in tight landscaping conditions - without compromising on JD reliability or hydraulic capability. Landscape contractors, small utility maintenance crews, and rural property operations all run the 110 for the same reason: it does the job a compact machine is supposed to do, and it shows up to work the next day.

Maintenance priorities on a 110: clean fuel, clean cool hydraulic oil, on-schedule filter changes, loader and backhoe cylinder seal replacement as they age, MFWD service (where equipped), and engine rebuilds when wear demands.
