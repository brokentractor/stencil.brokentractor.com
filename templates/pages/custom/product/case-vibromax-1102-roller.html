Where the Vibromax 1102 fits in the Case roller line

The Case Vibromax 1102 is the larger of the two Case Vibromax rollers commonly seen in the North American Case construction lineup - above the Vibromax 854 in drum width, machine weight, and compaction output. Twin-drum vibratory configuration built for paving and large soil-compaction jobs.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The Vibromax 1102 runs a hydrostatic drive feeding twin steel drums - front and rear - with an articulation joint between them for steering and tracking. Each drum has a vibration assembly (eccentric shaft with bearings) driven by its own hydraulic motor to deliver dynamic compaction force. The 40867501A oil cooler is the direct-fit Case Vibromax 1102 hydraulic / engine oil cooler.

The hydrostatic drive circuit and the vibration drive circuit share a common service philosophy: clean filtered oil at the right viscosity, and rebuilds done by serial-matched component number. Drum bearings see severe dynamic loading from the eccentric shaft and benefit from clean lubrication and timely replacement.

Common service items on the Vibromax 1102

  • Engine internals: Diesel engine pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets - full overhaul kits matched to the production engine.
  • Cooling system: 40867501A oil cooler is the direct-fit Case Vibromax 1102 oil cooler. Water pump, radiator, hoses, thermostat, fan.
  • Hydrostatic drive: Hydrostatic pump and motor rebuild, charge pump, hydrostatic filters, drive-circuit hoses and fittings.
  • Vibration assembly: Vibration bearing kits (drum eccentric shaft), vibration motors, isolation mounts, vibration drive hoses.
  • Drums: Drum scraper bars, scraper hardware, drum bearing service, drum drive coupling.
  • Articulation: Articulation joint pivot pins, bushings, steering cylinder seal kits.
  • Hydraulics - main: Main hydraulic pump rebuild, control valve service, hydraulic filters, return-line hoses.
  • Operator station + electrical: Seat, joysticks, controls, gauges, alternator, starter, wiring harness.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the frame near the rear axle or the ID plate before ordering year-specific drum, vibration, or hydrostatic parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts are calibrated to specific engine serial groups - confirm separately from the chassis serial.
  • Vibration bearing position. Drum vibration bearing kits are position-specific (drive end vs. idle end) - confirm before ordering.
  • Hydrostatic generation. Hydrostatic pump and motor revisions saw running changes during production - confirm by serial.
  • Drum scraper hardware. Scraper bar mounting pattern varies - measure or photograph before ordering replacement scraper assemblies.

Why the Vibromax 1102 still earns a paycheck

The Case Vibromax 1102 stays in service because tandem vibratory rollers earn their cost back fast on paving and large soil-compaction jobs - the twin-drum configuration delivers consistent density on every pass, and articulated steering makes the machine maneuverable in finish paving conditions where a rigid-frame compactor cannot turn cleanly.

Maintenance priorities on a Vibromax 1102 center on the vibration assembly (drum bearings see severe dynamic loading and benefit from clean lubrication and timely replacement), the hydrostatic drive (clean oil and filtration are non-negotiable), and the oil cooler (40867501A - fouling drives premature pump and hydrostatic failures).
