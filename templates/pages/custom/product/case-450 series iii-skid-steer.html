Where the 450 Series III fits in the Case skid steer line

The 450 Series III sits in the Case 400-series skid steer / CTL range between the Case 450 and the Case 465. It carries Tier 3 (Series III revision) compact diesel architecture, electronic engine controls, and the cab and instrumentation that defined Case’s mid-2000s skid steer line before the alpha (SR/SV/TR/TV) rename.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 450 Series III runs a Tier 3 (Series III revision) compact diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain. Loader hydraulics use a piston pump with electrohydraulic control valves. A wet-disc park brake is integrated with the hydrostatic deceleration logic.

Electronic engine controls and an EGR-based emissions strategy mean clean fuel, tight water separation, and clean air intake become the difference between a long-running machine and one that lives in the shop. The cab ergonomics on the Series III machines also moved the operator into a better seat than the XT predecessor, with refined joystick controls and improved gauge visibility.

Common service items on the 450 Series III

  • Engine internals: Tier 3 compact diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. Common-rail high-pressure injection components.
  • Emissions / EGR: EGR cooler service is a common wear item on Tier 3 builds.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, very tight water-separator filtration.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulics: Loader lift and bucket tilt cylinder seal kits (86570919 / 86570931 / 86570922 cross-fit on NH/Case alpha-series), piston-pump rebuilds, electrohydraulic control valves.
  • Drivetrain: Hydrostatic dual-path pumps and drive motors, charge pump, hydraulic filters.
  • Hardware: 191048A2 axle stud & nut cross-fits broadly across the Case skid steer line.
  • Brakes / control: Park brake components, control linkage, 48139268 control shock absorber on Case/NH alpha-series control valves.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, machine controller, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness.
  • Cab / operator station: Cab HVAC, seat, gauges, joystick controls, cab glass, 47405956 windshield wiper blade on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Build year / revision. Tier 3 (Series III revision) configuration varies by build year - confirm before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Machine serial number. Confirm the serial on the rear frame near the engine access panel before ordering.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied between configurations; HVAC, glass, and wiper parts only apply to cab-equipped units.

Why the 450 Series III still earns a paycheck

The Case 450 Series III is the mid-2000s Case skid steer loader that bridged the XT-series and the current alpha lineup. Tier 3 (Series III revision) emissions, modern electronics, and the cab ergonomics that put the operator in a better seat than the XT predecessor.

Maintenance priorities on a 450 Series III: clean fuel and tight water separation for common-rail injection, EGR cooler condition, hydrostatic charge pressure, drive-motor seal condition, and cylinder seal integrity on the loader arms.
