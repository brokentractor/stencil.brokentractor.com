Where the 1021F fits in the Case wheel loader line

The 1021F sits at the top of the F-series production wheel loader range, between the 921F and the larger 1121F. It carries Tier 4 Interim emissions, the F-series cab, articulated frame, and a power-shift drivetrain sized for large production work. The successor is the G-generation 1021G.

The 1021F was the largest production-class machine in the Case F-series wheel loader line until the 1121F arrived above it. Across the F-series, controls, cab layout, and ECU architecture share a common pattern, which means many cab, electrical, and climate components cross-fit across the 521F through 1121F range.

What defines the F-generation 1021

  • Tier 4 Interim emissions. The F-series moved Case's large production wheel loaders to Tier 4 Interim with DPF aftertreatment and cooled EGR.
  • Common-rail fuel system. Electronic injectors and tight water-separator filtration. Clean fuel matters more on the F than on earlier mechanical-injection 1021s.
  • F-series cab. Larger sealed-and-pressurized operator station, refined HVAC, full-color monitor display, and standardized control layout across the F-series.
  • Power-shift drivetrain. Production-class power-shift transmission with planetary final drives in the axles.
  • Cross-fit components. Cab climate parts (blower resistors, cab filters), brake hydraulics, and articulation steering parts share part numbers across multiple F-series sizes.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 1021F runs a Tier 4 Interim Case diesel through a power-shift transmission with DPF aftertreatment. The base engine drives a piston-pump hydraulic circuit for the loader and steering, and pulls accessory drive for the cooling fan, charge-air cooler, and air-conditioning compressor.

Loader hydraulics use a piston pump sized for the 1021's production loadout. Articulation steering uses paired steering cylinders pivoting the front and rear frame halves around the center pin. Service brakes are wet-disc, hydraulically actuated through a master cylinder and slave assemblies.

Common service items on the 1021F

  • Engine internals: Case diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets. R49482 engine motor mount is a recurring service item.
  • Tier 4 Interim aftertreatment: DPF service, EGR cooler service, related sensors and differential pressure lines.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, water separator, fuel filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Loader hydraulics: Cylinder seal kits, piston-pump rebuilds, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Articulation steering: Steering cylinder seal kits, articulation pivot pins and bushings. G109152 steering cylinder bushing fits 521F, 621, 721, 821 series.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, bushings, pivot pins, quick coupler hardware.
  • Drivetrain: Power-shift clutch packs, transmission internals, axle seals, planetary final drives. Transmission shift lever assembly available for service.
  • Brakes: Wet-disc service brakes. S300718 brake shoe, A12985 slave cylinder, and N8695 master cylinder are recurring service items across the platform.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, wiring harness. 87528688 AC blower speed resistor fits 521E through 1121F.
  • Cab and climate: Seat, gauges, control levers, cab filtration. 47880728 cab fresh-air filter on cab-equipped units.

What to verify before ordering

  • Generation matters. The 1021-series saw multiple generations - confirm F-series by serial before ordering.
  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the left machine frame near the front axle before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Case diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Aftertreatment configuration. Tier 4 Interim DPF and EGR part numbers vary across the production run.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied - confirm before ordering cab or HVAC parts.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Multiple bucket configurations and coupler options were available.

Why the 1021F still earns a paycheck

The 1021F is the F-series large production wheel loader - production-class capacity with Tier 4 Interim emissions and the F-series cab. For customers who run a 1021F in a quarry, aggregate yard, or production loading site, the machine is sized to fill articulated trucks efficiently and run a long shift without operator fatigue.

Maintenance priorities on a 1021F: clean fuel and tight water separation (common-rail injectors are not tolerant of contamination), DPF regen behavior, hydraulic system condition, brake service intervals, and articulation pivot wear.
