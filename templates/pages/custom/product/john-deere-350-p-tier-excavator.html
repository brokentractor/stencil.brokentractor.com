Where the 350 P-Tier fits in the JD excavator line

The 350 P-Tier sits between the 345 P-Tier and 380 P-Tier in the modern JD P-Tier construction excavator lineup. It represents the current production 35-Ton class JD excavator, running Tier 4 Final emissions and the latest cab and control refinements. Direct-fit service items like the PG200046 swing bearing (350DLC/350GLC/2954D/3754D) and FYA00037707 cab assembly (250G/290G/350G) carry forward platform commonality with the G-Tier predecessors.

Locating your serial number On the John Deere 350 P-Tier the serial number plate is mounted on the upper frame near the cab entry, typically on the right-hand side beneath the door threshold or beside the operator’s seat. Always verify the serial number before ordering parts to ensure proper fitment - the P-Tier production run includes running changes that affect part numbers.

JD/Hitachi platform sharing and what it means for parts

John Deere’s construction excavators are built on the JD/Hitachi shared platform, meaning many service parts carry across multiple JD G-Tier and P-Tier models as well as Hitachi-branded equivalents. For owners of a 350 P-Tier this is a real advantage - cross-fit components like the AT317252 cross-pin fit a broad JD family including the 180CW, 190DW, 200/200C/200G, 210G/210P, 225C/225D, 230GW, 245G, 2054, 2154D, and many others.

That platform sharing means parts availability stays strong even as machines age. Pump, valve, cylinder, and undercarriage components have wide cross-fit applicability across the family.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 350 P-Tier runs a Tier 4 Final diesel paired with DPF and SCR aftertreatment, feeding a hydrostatic drive system into a steel-track undercarriage. The Tier 4 Final emissions package requires DEF (diesel exhaust fluid) dosing and periodic DPF regeneration cycles - clean fuel and quality DEF are non-negotiable on this generation.

The hydraulic system uses variable-displacement piston pumps with electronic flow management, providing fuel-efficient operation and smooth multi-function control. The steel-track undercarriage uses the proven JD/Hitachi excavator pattern with sealed-and-lubricated track chains, planetary final drives, and JD-pattern rollers and idlers.

Common service items on the 350 P-Tier

  • Engine: Tier 4 Final diesel internals - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gasket kits.
  • Aftertreatment: DPF service, DEF dosing units, SCR catalyst, NOx sensors, DPF pressure sensors.
  • Cooling: Radiator, hydraulic oil cooler, EGR cooler, water pump, thermostat, heat exchanger.
  • Hydraulics: Main pumps, control valve, swing motors, travel motors, boom/arm/bucket cylinders, seal kits.
  • Boom pins: AT317252 cross-pin - broad JD family cross-fit.
  • Swing bearing: PG200046 swing bearing (350DLC/350GLC/2954D/3754D).
  • Undercarriage: Steel track chains, shoes, idlers, rollers, sprockets, final drives.
  • Cab and electrical: FYA00037707 cab assembly, glass, alternator, starter, sensors, harnesses, ECU, monitor display.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The 350 P-Tier’s DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. DEF tank must stay topped up. DPF will request periodic high-temperature regenerations. NOx sensors fail occasionally and trigger derate conditions if not replaced promptly. For customers running 350 P-Tier machines in production, factor aftertreatment service into the maintenance budget.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the upper frame before ordering year-specific parts. P-Tier production includes running changes.
  • Engine and aftertreatment serial. Tier 4 Final emissions parts are serial-specific - DPF, SCR, dosing units, and sensors can vary across the production run.
  • Aftertreatment generation. DPF and SCR revisions saw running changes. Match against aftertreatment serial tags.
  • Boom-pin position. AT317252 cross-pin fits a broad JD family - confirm pin position (boom, arm, bucket) before ordering.
  • Hydraulic pump generation. Main pump and valve part numbers can vary across production.
  • Undercarriage configuration. Track shoe width and undercarriage length variants exist - confirm against existing components.

Why the 350 P-Tier still earns a paycheck

The 350 P-Tier stays in service because modern P-Tier 35-Ton JD excavators deliver Tier 4 Final fuel efficiency and operator-station refinements that contractors rely on for production work. JD/Hitachi platform parts sharing means service parts are widely available even years into the machine’s life - cross-fit components across the G-Tier and P-Tier construction excavator line keep downtime short.

Maintenance priorities on a 350 P-Tier: clean fuel and quality DEF (Tier 4 Final common-rail injectors and SCR catalysts don’t tolerate contamination), aftertreatment service discipline, clean cool hydraulic oil, boom-pin service using the broad AT317252 cross-fit, undercarriage replacement on schedule, and cab and electrical service.
