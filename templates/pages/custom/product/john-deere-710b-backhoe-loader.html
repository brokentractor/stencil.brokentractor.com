John Deere 710B Backhoe Loader: Largest B-Series Industrial Backhoe

Released in the late 1980s, the 710B was the largest and most powerful model in JD's B-Series backhoe lineup. Built specifically for contractors needing maximum reach, breakout force, and loading capacity, the 710B set the production-class benchmark for the JD industrial backhoe line.

Where the 710B fits in the JD backhoe line

The 710 designation marks JD's largest production backhoe loader. The 710B introduced the platform in the late 1980s, sized above the 610B for contractors who needed maximum digging depth, reach, and lift capacity in a backhoe-loader configuration - pipeline projects, large-site site-prep, deep utility trenching, and any work where the dimensions of the job demanded a top-tier production backhoe.

The engine is a JD turbocharged diesel sized for the largest chassis in the B-Series lineup. The drivetrain runs a heavy mechanical transmission through wet-disc brakes into reinforced planetary final drives. Hydraulics are scaled larger than the 610B - more pump displacement, larger cylinder bore at the backhoe boom, and longer stick reach.

What set the 710B apart in the B-Series

  • Largest chassis in the B-Series. Sized above the 610B with reinforced frame, axles, and final drives to handle production-class digging and lifting.
  • Turbocharged JD diesel. A larger displacement, turbocharged engine - the powerplant needed to feed the largest hydraulic system in the JD industrial backhoe line.
  • Largest-class hydraulics. More pump displacement, larger cylinder bore at the backhoe boom and stick, and longer reach than any other B-Series backhoe.
  • Optional MFWD. 4WD was common on the 710B given the size - 2WD traction can be the limiting factor on a machine of this scale.
  • Wet-disc brakes. Oil-cooled brake packs sized for the heavier chassis and higher operating weights.
Pre-Tier 4, mechanical fuel injection The 710B uses mechanical fuel injection with no aftertreatment - no DPF, no SCR, no DEF. Service is straightforward for operators familiar with the JD industrial backhoe service profile from this era.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 710B's turbocharged JD diesel drives a heavy mechanical transmission through wet-disc brakes into reinforced planetary final drives. Optional MFWD adds powered front-wheel drive - important on a machine of this size where 2WD traction can be the limiting factor on soft or sloped ground.

The hydraulic system delivers production-class flow and pressure to drive the largest backhoe in the B-Series lineup. The loader circuit, backhoe boom/stick/bucket cylinders, swing tower, and stabilizers are all sized larger than anything else in the B-Series family.

Common service items on the 710B

  • Turbocharger: Turbo rebuilds, oil supply and drain lines, charge-air piping, turbo bearings.
  • Largest-class hydraulics: Pump rebuilds, loader cylinder seal kits, backhoe boom/stick/bucket cylinder seal kits (largest bore in the JD industrial backhoe line), control valves, hoses.
  • Wet-disc brakes: Brake disc packs, seals, oil-cooled brake housing components.
  • Engine internals: JD turbocharged diesel rebuild kits - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, gaskets.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump rebuilds, injectors, lift pump, fuel filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, hoses, fan belt. Cooling is critical on the largest backhoe class.
  • 4WD components (where equipped): Front axle assemblies, transfer case parts, drive shaft U-joints.
  • Loader components: Bucket teeth, cutting edges, pins, bushings (largest-class sizing).
  • Backhoe components: Bucket teeth, side cutters, swing tower pin kits (largest-class), stabilizer leg cylinders.
  • Drivetrain: Clutch packs, transmission components, axle seals, final drive gears.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, harnesses, switches, gauges.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. The 710B had running changes through its production. Match parts to the machine serial.
  • Engine serial. JD turbocharged diesel internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Turbocharger variant. Turbo part numbers can vary across the production run.
  • 2WD vs. 4WD configuration. Critical to confirm before ordering front axle or drive components - 4WD is especially common on 710B machines given the size.
  • Loader configuration. Standard and specialty bucket configurations had different cylinder and mounting specs.
  • Backhoe configuration. Standard reach vs. extended-reach configurations differ in cylinder bore and stroke - particularly critical on the largest-class 710B backhoe.
  • Cab vs. ROPS canopy. Operator station equipment varied.

Why the 710B remains a pipeline-contractor pick

The 710B is JD's largest production backhoe loader of the B-Series era - maximum reach, breakout force, and loading capacity from a backhoe-loader platform. For pipeline contractors and large-site utility operations running production excavation work, the 710B delivered capabilities that smaller backhoes couldn't match. Mechanical fuel injection, no electronics, no aftertreatment - service is straightforward for operators familiar with the JD industrial backhoe service profile.

Maintenance priorities on a 710B: turbo oil supply integrity, clean fuel, largest-class hydraulic seal replacement, wet-disc brake fluid service, 4WD front axle service (where equipped), swing tower pin service, and engine rebuilds when wear demands.
