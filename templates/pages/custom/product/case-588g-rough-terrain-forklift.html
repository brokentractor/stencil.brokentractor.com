Where the 588G fits in the Case forklift line

The 588G sits above the 586G in the G-series Case RT forklift line - 8,000 lb lift capacity (vs. ~5,000 lb on the 586G) and a heavier mast and chassis to match. It shares the 362347A1 lift cylinder seal kit, 175978A1 needle bearing, and K395075 thrust washer cross-fit with the 585G and 586G G-series siblings, which keeps service parts widely available even though the chassis itself is 588-specific.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The 588G runs a Tier 3 turbocharged diesel through a heavy-duty power-shuttle transmission. 8,000 lb mast and chassis components are 588-specific, but the 362347A1 lift cylinder seal kit, 175978A1 needle bearing, and K395075 thrust washer cross-fit with the 585G and 586G G-series siblings - a broad G-series parts ecosystem keeps service costs manageable.

The mast hydraulic circuit drives lift, tilt, and side-shift on equipped configurations. Steering is hydrostatic with a dedicated steering motor and cylinder. Front and rear axles use planetary final drives, and 4WD configurations add a transfer driveshaft and front-axle service points.

Common service items on the 588G

  • Engine internals: Tier 3 turbocharged diesel - pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets.
  • Tier 3 aftertreatment: Cooled EGR service; pre-DPF / pre-DEF era. EGR cooler condition tracking.
  • Fuel system: Injection pump, injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, hoses, belts.
  • Power shuttle / transmission: Power shuttle clutch packs, forward/reverse modulation, transmission filters and oil. The power-shuttle is a defining feature on Case RT forklifts.
  • Steering hydraulics: Steering motor, steering cylinder seal kit, control valve, hoses.
  • Mast hydraulics: Lift cylinder seal kits, tilt cylinder seal kits, side-shift cylinder service, control valves. The 362347A1 mast lift cylinder seal kit fits 585G, 586G, and 588G.
  • Mast components: Mast carriage rollers, lift chain inspection and replacement, fork carriage bushings, side-shift attachment service.
  • Drivetrain: Front and rear axles, planetary final drives, driveshaft, axle differential service. The 175978A1 needle bearing 10-pack and K395075 thrust washer fit 585G / 586G / 588G plus loader-backhoe applications.
  • Brakes: The A168790 parking brake disc cross-fits Case forklift / loader-backhoe / trencher applications - a high-volume service item. Plus service brakes and brake hydraulics.
  • Cab / operator station: Seat, joystick / lever controls, gauges, key switch, ROPS canopy or enclosed cab. The 377594A5 cab air filter fits Case loader tractor / backhoe / forklift cabs.
  • Electrical: Starter, alternator, wiring harness, gauges, switches.
Power-shuttle service - the defining wear item The power-shuttle transmission is what defines a Case RT forklift in daily service. Forward/reverse cycling under load is constant, and clutch-pack condition determines whether the machine modulates smoothly or jerks under shift. Clean transmission oil and correct filtration are the cheapest insurance against an expensive shuttle rebuild.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the serial on the Case 588G forklift before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Internal engine parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Mast configuration. Lift height, carriage width, fork length, and side-shift options vary - confirm mast spec before ordering carriage / chain / cylinder parts.
  • Two-wheel vs. four-wheel drive. Some Case RT forklift configurations are available in 2WD and 4WD - drivetrain parts differ.
  • Power-shuttle generation. Power-shuttle transmissions saw revisions across the G-series production run - confirm by serial before ordering clutch-pack components.

Why the 588G still earns a paycheck

The Case 588G is the high-capacity G-series RT forklift - 8,000 lb of lift in a rough-terrain platform suits forestry, large construction, heavy lumber, and industrial material handling where the 586G’s 5,000-6,000 lb capacity falls short. The G-series shared-parts story keeps service-parts pricing reasonable on a class of machine that would otherwise feel like an orphan in the catalog.

Maintenance priorities on a 588G: clean fuel and quality filtration, Tier 3 turbo and EGR service, power-shuttle clutch-pack condition, mast hydraulic seal replacement (362347A1 direct fit), driveline bearing service (175978A1 / K395075 cross-fit), and brake service (A168790 cross-fit).
