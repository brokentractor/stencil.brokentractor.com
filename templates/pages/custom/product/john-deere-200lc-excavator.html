The John Deere 200LC is a workhorse 20-ton hydraulic excavator manufactured from 1996 through 2006. Where the 200LC fits in the John Deere excavator line

The 200LC is the heritage 20-ton long-carriage JD excavator - the size class that defines mid-large construction excavator work. Mechanical, simple, and well-supported by both JD and Hitachi (EX200 ancestry) cross-fit parts, it sits squarely in the production sweet spot for utility, foundation excavation, light demolition, and infrastructure jobs.

Engine, hydraulics, and undercarriage

The 200LC runs a JD diesel through a main hydraulic pump into the boom, arm, bucket, swing, and travel circuits. The long-carriage steel-track undercarriage gives it the stability the 20-ton class demands. Direct-fit service parts available at Broken Tractor include the AT174029 radiator, 4333459 A/C compressor (200LC/230LC/230LCRD), AT317252 cross-pin covering a broad JD excavator family, the 4602563R lower windshield (200CLC cross-fit), and the 9186437 track recoil + adjuster (200CLC/DLC + Hitachi ZX).

Common service items on the 200LC

  • Engine: JD diesel internals - pistons, rings, sleeves, bearings, head gaskets, overhaul kits.
  • Cooling - radiator: AT174029 radiator (200LC direct fit).
  • Cooling - other: Water pump, fan, hoses, charge-air cooler.
  • A/C system: 4333459 A/C compressor (200LC + 230LC + 230LCRD).
  • Hydraulics: Main pump, control valve, swing motor, travel motors, cylinders. Hitachi EX200 ancestry parts cross-fit.
  • Undercarriage: 9186437 track recoil + adjuster. Steel tracks, shoes, idlers, rollers, sprockets.
  • Boom pins: AT317252 cross-pin covering a broad JD family.
  • Cab glass: 4602563R lower windshield (cross-fit 200CLC).

Why the 200LC still earns a paycheck

The 200LC stays in service because well-maintained 20-ton mechanical hydraulic excavators from the 1996-2006 era still earn paychecks every day - utility, foundation excavation, light demolition, and infrastructure work. JD reliability plus Hitachi cross-fit parts availability plus straightforward mechanical design adds up to long service life.
