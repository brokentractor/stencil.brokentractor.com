Where the CX220E fits in the Case excavator line

The CX220E is a 22-ton class E-Series excavator and the newest mid-size production excavator in the Case lineup. It sits between the CX210D and the CX225 SR in the full-size range, sharing the E-Series platform updates that brought Tier 4 Final emissions, refined electrohydraulic controls, and an updated operator station to the Case excavator family.

Engine, hydraulics, and drivetrain

The CX220E runs a Tier 4 Final diesel through a hydrostatic dual-path drivetrain into a steel-track undercarriage. The main hydraulic pump - commonly a load-sensing piston pump - drives boom, dipper, bucket, swing, and propel functions through electrohydraulic control valves. Aftertreatment includes a DPF for particulate control and SCR with DEF dosing for NOx reduction.

The hydrostatic dual-path drive provides independent track control for steering and counter-rotation. The swing system uses a dedicated motor with planetary reduction driving a swing bearing built for production cycle times.

Common service items on the CX220E

  • Engine internals: Tier 4 Final diesel pistons, rings, sleeves, head gaskets. Common-rail injection.
  • Tier 4 Final aftertreatment: DPF service and regeneration; DEF tank and dosing module; SCR catalyst; EGR cooler service.
  • Fuel system: Common-rail injectors, lift pump, primary and secondary filters with tight water-separator standards.
  • Cooling system: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, charge-air cooler, hoses, belts.
  • Hydraulic system: Boom / dipper / bucket / swing cylinder seal kits, main pump rebuild, electrohydraulic control valves, hoses.
  • Boom and dipper: Pin and bushing service at every joint, bucket pins, thumb attachment hardware, quick coupler service.
  • Undercarriage: Steel track inspection and replacement, rollers, idlers, drive sprockets, recoil springs.
  • Swing system: Swing motor and reduction gear, swing bearing, swing brake.
  • Electrical / ECU: Engine ECU, instrument cluster, sensors, harness, telematics module on equipped units.
  • Cab glass and wiper: KHN16820 24-inch wiper blade, KHN3046 front lower glass, and KHN2632 front upper glass cross-fit across Case and Link-Belt excavators.
  • Operator station: Seat, joystick controls, pedal linkage, instrument panel, cab HVAC.
Tier 4 Final aftertreatment - service implications The CX220E’s DPF and SCR systems are reliable when fed clean fuel and quality DEF, but they require attention. Keep the DEF tank topped up with quality fluid, expect periodic high-temperature DPF regenerations, and replace NOx sensors before derate conditions force the issue.

What to verify before ordering

  • Serial number. Confirm the upper-frame serial before ordering year-specific parts.
  • Engine serial. Engine internal parts must match the engine serial number.
  • Hydraulic cylinder bore. Cylinder seal kit fit depends on bore dimension - measure before ordering.
  • Tier 4 emissions revision. DPF and DEF/SCR components saw revisions during production - confirm by serial.
  • Engine ECU calibration. Common-rail injectors and ECU sensors are calibrated to specific build years.
  • Bucket and quick coupler. Quick-coupler interface varies - confirm before ordering attachment-side hardware.

Why the CX220E still earns a paycheck

The Case CX220E is a 22-ton class production excavator built for general site work, utility installation, residential and commercial earthmoving, and mining or quarry work depending on configuration. As the current production E-Series 22-ton machine, parts availability is strong and aftermarket support is growing.

Maintenance priorities on a CX220E: clean fuel and tight water separation for the common-rail injection, DPF regeneration behavior tracking, hydraulic oil condition, swing-bearing lubrication, steel-track tension and undercarriage wear monitoring, and pin and bushing service at every boom joint.
